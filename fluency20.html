Day 20 - Catch-up Speaking

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Listen to Recording 4.5 from 1 to 3 times, without the transcript and try to understand it as much as you can.

✔️ With the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 4.5 from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Then, with the transcript, listen again, and practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 4.5 from 1 to 2 times. Practice until you can keep up with the character's speed.

✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 4.5 at least 2-3 times.

✔️ Note: Focus on the pronunciation of each word, especially the stress and the ending sounds.

Recording 4.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Delivering Bad News

David – Mary, could you tell everybody to go to the meeting room now? I have an important announcement for them.

Mary – Yes, David.

Kate, James, could you both please go to the meeting room. David has something important to say to everybody.

David – Thank you everyone for being here. I´ll just give it to you straight. Mr. Smith has reduced his order, and I think we’ve just lost a big part of the contract.

Mary – That must be because Kate refused his dinner invitation!

Kate – I’m so sorry for that! I honestly didn´t know what to do. I didn´t know how to handle the situation any better.

David – Oh, my! Was it really that? You did not do anything wrong in my eyes, Kate.

James – I think so, too. Well done! Kate, you stuck up for yourself.

David – Yes, yes. Now, we can´t do anything about this now, but things aren’t looking good at the moment. In fact, the picture is downright gloomy. We´ve just lost a client, and the competition seems to be getting harder. Besides, I’ve got to announce a profit warning. With this order lost, we simply aren’t selling enough product to make any money. We´re only breaking even, at best.

David – So, this calls for action. My plan is…we’re going global.

Kate – Global? Are we going to move to another country? I hope it´s one with a lot of beaches!

David – No, Kate. We’re going to sell our services to other countries as well. I’m also bringing in a marketing executive named Rose Miller to help out. She´ll be here starting next week.

Mary – Oh! I’ll be sure to get everything ready for her in the office.

David – Thank you, Mary. I expect to improve our results and profits. If not, our necks will all be on the line. We´ll be sitting ducks!

 

Topic 2: Cold-Calling

Kate – Going global sounds promising, David.

James – Yeah, but what exactly are we going to do about that?

David – Well, Kate´s supposed to take the lead on that. This is the chance to improve and prove yourself at the same time, Kate.

Kate – Oh. Great. Oh, wait, what should I do first? I don´t want to make a false first step.

David – What I’m thinking is that we need to know more about the market. In fact, you guys can do some cold-calling. Kate, could you look for some international enterprises, possibly some that have branches in Korea?

Mary – Be persuasive, but straight to the point. It’s really expensive to call abroad. We can´t afford to run up our phone bill.

Trader – Hello, I´m Lee Chul Soo, the training manager here. How may I help you?

Kate – Hello, Mr. Lee. Can you spare a few minutes of your time to talk about something that can really help improve your company´s language training?

Trader – Sure.

Kate – Hello, my name´s Kate, from E+ Education. Do you mind if I talk about English-learning products and services with you?

Trader – Not at all. It’s one of our top priorities to improve our employees’ English levels.

Kate – That’s so good to hear, and I´m sure we have something you need. I’d like to tell you briefly about our great new course, X3 Coaching.

Trader – Could you give me more pertinent details, please?

Kate – We´re E+ Education, one of the biggest names in international English teaching and learning. Our new course, X3 Coaching, has been developed by top experts and supported by the best teachers. I hope you´ll be able to try and enjoy it as well.

Trader – That all sounds very interesting.

Kate – So, thank you so much for your time. I hope you consider our course.

Trader – We will certainly take a look at it.

Kate – Wonderful! Thank you again. We hope to hear back from you soon. Goodbye for now.

 

Topic 3: IT Support

Kate – Gosh! What’s happened? My computer´s frozen, it won´t budge. Mary, Mary, could you help me?

Mary – Let me see. I think we… I think you need to call IT Support. This is their number. They´ll figure out the problem and get your computer back to work in no time.

Kate – Oh, okay, I’ll call them right away.

IT Man – Hello, this is IT, Matt speaking. How can I help you?

Kate – Oh hello, Matt. I’m having some problems with my computer.

IT Man – Okay. What type of computer are you currently using?

Kate – What do you mean? I didn´t catch what you said.

IT Man – Are you using a desktop, laptop, or something with Windows XP?

Kate – Well, I believe I´m using Windows 7 on a desktop.

IT Man – Good. Were you using many applications at the same time it froze up on you?

Kate – What exactly are applications? I´m not really familiar with computer lingo.

IT Man – I mean, did you open a lot of things, like documents, spreadsheets, videos, and other kinds of software?

Kate – Well, I think so. I like to open everything I know how.

IT Man – Okay, Kate, now you can try pressing the control, alt, and delete keys at the same time, and after that, try opening Windows again.

Kate – Windows? We already have almost all the windows open in our office to let in some fresh air!

IT Man – I´m talking about Windows 7, the software on your computer.

Kate – Ah! I see, but it doesn’t work. The screen is frozen. Oh God! Oh, I think I’ve lost some critical documents.

IT Man – In that case, all you can do is reboot it.

Kate – Reboot it? Do you mean I need to start playing football with my computer?!

IT Man – I mean, shut it down and restart your computer.

Kate – Let me try.

No, that hasn’t fixed it. What on earth am I going to do now?

IT Man – No need to worry! I’ll come down to your desk and check it out now.

Kate – Thank you.

 

Topic 4: Misunderstanding

Mary – Good morning, Kate. David´s on his way back from a business trip. He’s excited to hear about your new proposal.

Kate – Oh, Mary…

Mary – I heard that he was talking to Mr. Lee from KoreTech, and they really want to get the proposal as soon as possible to get the deal going.

Kate – I’ve lost all the documents. I’m working my way back from the crash now.

Mary – Katie. You know how strict he is. You’d better explain it to him before anything else.

David – Good morning, everyone.

Kate & Mary – Good morning, David.

David – Kate, excellent work on the KoreTech project. I´d like to finish the proposal by the end of day, today. Now, I’m running off to a meeting, so see you in the afternoon.

James – Kate, do you need some help with that work?

Kate – Thank you, James. I think I´ll have to make more of an effort, or do things differently next time.

Mary – Oh, no! Now my computer is frozen, too. I’m supposed to send David the purchase report this afternoon.

Kate – Mary! Oh, not yours, too. This is a big issue for all of us, and that’s much more urgent than what I´m doing now. Here , you can use my email to access it.

David – Kate, would you come to my office, please. I´d like a brief about your progress up to now.

David – So, how´s it going?

Kate – I’m sorry, but I’m only half finished with the document.

David – What? Oh this is such a disappointment!

Kate – Oh, well, David…I need to explain.

David – This is simply unacceptable, Kate. At first, I wanted to tell you that if you ever need help, you have to raise your voice. But it turns out that not only didn´t you do your own work, but you tried to do others’ too!

Kate – Oh, no, David. Gosh! I think there’s been a misunderstanding. Let me explain exactly what happened.

David – Okay, I’m all ears.

Kate – While you were away on your trip, my computer crashed.

David – Oh, really? And…?

Kate – Oh, well, I thought I could finish the proposal by the end of today, but Mary had an urgent job to finish with the purchase report, and suddenly it was her computer’s turn to crash, and she couldn’t log in to her email.

David – The purchase report? That´s right, it’s due today. But you didn’t have to do it for Mary. She could´ve done it herself.

Kate – No, I think there’s been a bit of a mix-up. Mary only used my email account to send the report to you.

David – Oh, so that’s it. I’m sorry for misunderstanding you. But you still have to give it your all to finish that proposal ASAP.

Kate – Sure, David. I´ll get to it right now!

Step 2: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Please use the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 4.6 from 1 to 3 times.

✔️ Then, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 4.6 from 1 to 2 times. You can use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary. Practice until you can keep up with the character's speed.

✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 4.6 at least 2-3 times until you feel confident with your speed.

✔️ Note: You should spend a lot of time on this step. You need to focus on the intonation, the linking sounds and the natural speed of the native speakers.
