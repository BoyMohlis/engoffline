Day 50 - Catch-up Speaking

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Listen to Recording 9.5 from 1 to 3 times, without the transcript and try to understand it as much as you can.

✔️ With the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 9.5 from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Then, with the transcript, listen again, and practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 9.5 from 1 to 2 times. Practice until you can keep up with the character's speed.

✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 9.5 at least 2-3 times.

✔️ Note: Focus on the pronunciation of each word, especially the stress and the ending sounds.

Recording 9.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Lesson 09 - Working From Seoul

Recording 9.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Recording 9.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Briefing and Reporting

James – You’re really impressive, Kate. I mean, you totally got that Mr. Lee. He’s eating out of your palm of your hand.

Kate – No, you are. This trip is a success thanks to how well you know your baby.

James – We’d better check on our stock, and make sure that we have enough DVDs.

Kate – That’s right. That’s my first task when I get back to the office.

James – Wow, here we are at the airport already. Let me help you check in.

Kate – Thank you, James. It’s very nice of you.

James – Kate! I just found out that our flight has been canceled!

Kate – What are we going to do now? I wanna go home!

James – I’ve got us another one, but at the earliest only tomorrow.

Kate – What about tonight?

James – We’ll need a hotel and taxi service to the airport tomorrow. Let me get that done as well.

Kate – You’re very handy, James.

James – Okay, done. Now we need to keep David in the picture, I mean keep him informed.

Kate – Oh, yes. You’re right, it’s always a good idea to keep the boss updated on what’s going on. Keep him in the loop.

David – Hello?

Kate – Hi David. It’s Kate here. I’ve got some good news!

David – What’s that? Oh, Oh, is it about the new contract in Korea?

Kate – Yes! We’ve closed the deal with KoreTech Global, and the contract has been signed.

David – Excellent!

Kate – To keep them happy, we need to proceed with the order as quickly as possible.

David – Of course. So, what´s your plan to do that?

Kate – Well, obviously, I’m committed to getting the order delivered right and on time, but I might need some help doing that.

David – Oh, sure. We´re welcoming a new staff member here, Rose Miller.

Kate – Oh, that’s fantastic. I’ll need to check our stock first, oh, but unfortunately tonight we have to stay here. Our flight´s been canceled.

David – I see. Just call me if you need anything. And congratulations to you two!

Kate – Thank you, David. James, we’ve got someone new at the office.

James – That’s great. Now let’s find a restaurant. My treat!

 

Topic 2: Keeping Things Professional

James – This bar looks quite cozy. What would you like, Kate?

Kate – Well, I’m not very familiar with Korean food. Would you mind helping me?

James – Sure, actually, Korean cuisine is mainly based on rice, vegetables, and meat. They use chopsticks to eat, but I can ask for a fork and knife if you like.

Kate – Oh, well, you seem to know a lot about many things.

James – Only a bit, Kate. I´d like some beer. Today was quite a day, and we should celebrate some. The night is young. Wow, that looks good.

Kate – Oh, it is good. What’s that over there? Are you ok, James? You look kinda drunk all of a sudden.

James – I am? Maybe a bit buzzed. But Kate I want to know more about you.

Kate – Me?

James – Yes, like, are you in a relationship?

Kate – Oh… Well…

James – How many boyfriends have you had?

Kate – James, I think that´s too personal. It’s not appropriate to talk like that. Could we change the subject, please?

James – Oh, I’m sorry. Okay. So, you´re very pretty and likable, Katie. I think I´ve got a crush on you.

Kate – James, can we keep our conversation professional? There’s a lot to discuss about how to finish this order now.

James – But, I’d like to talk about you. You are fascinating to me, so please…

Kate – I think I´ll get you a lemonade or something. How about some hot coffee?

James – Never mind Kate. I think I got the message.

 

Topic 3: Checking Information

James – Sorry, Kate. I got a little carried away.

Kate – Oh, no problem. Oh, but we really need to check our stock to process this order ASAP.

James – You´re totally right. But I can’t think of anyone to help us with that at the moment.

Kate – Well, while waiting for you to sober up, I emailed Rose Miller, our new staff, and she said she’d get back to me in an hour.

James – Rose Miller? That name sounds familiar…

Kate – You know her?

James – That’s not good news, Kate. But we don’t have choice right now. Just wait for her email. I’ll tell you more about her later.

Kate – Right. Here it is. Oh no, we´ve got 100,000 blank DVDs left? That’s impossible!

James – Yeah, that doesn’t sound right. We should call back and check this with her.

Kate – I’m way ahead of you!

James – Wait, Kate! You don’t know this Madam Rose Miller. Be careful!

Kate – Hello, Rose, I’m Kate. Thank you for your timely email.

Rose – Oh, Katie Cat, you sound so emotional because of my prompt response. You can thank me later; there´s no need to call back immediately.

Kate – Sorry to bother you, I just want to check something. We´re wondering about the number of DVDs…

Rose – What? You mean you don’t trust me? Are you calling me a liar to my face?!

Kate – No, it’s not that. I just want to make sure about…

Rose – I never make a mistake. Do you hear that? Am I making myself perfectly clear?!

Kate – Of course, but since we have such a big order, we need to make absolutely sure about our stock.

Rose – Humph.

Kate – Could you possibly clarify again the number of our DVDs in stock?

Rose – 1,000.

Kate – Right. That’s different from your email, but thank you anyway.

 

Topic 4: Placing an Order

James – Kate, are you all right? What did Rose tell you?

Kate – Well, it’s not good, James. We need about 5,000 DVDs now.

James – I’ve got our agency number here. Don’t be stressed out. I’ll call them right now.

Kate – Don’t worry! I can do that, James. Hello, this is Kate from E+ Education speaking. I’d like to place an order for some blank DVDs, please. We’re going to need quite a big number. Could you send 5,000 please. That’s right, we’d like the highest quality. Could we also have the name and logo of our company on the boxes? And finally, when can we expect to receive them? Okay, oh, thank you, and goodbye for now.

James – Kate, you sound really professional!

Kate – I do? Thank you.

James – Now we should get some rest before tomorrow´s flight. I guess big trouble is waiting for us at the office.

Kate – You mean Rose Miller?

James – Yeah, you got that right. But first let me send a quick email to David, telling him about this mess, so that we can start working on this order as soon as we get back.

Kate – That’s right. Copy me in so that I don’t miss anything.

James – Sure, Kate!

Step 2: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Please use the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 9.6 from 1 to 3 times.

✔️ Then, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 9.6 from 1 to 2 times. You can use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary. Practice until you can keep up with the character's speed.

✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 9.6 at least 2-3 times until you feel confident with your speed.

✔️ Note: You should spend a lot of time on this step. You need to focus on the intonation, the linking sounds and the natural speed of the native speakers.
