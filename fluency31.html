Lesson 06 - Expanding

Recording 6.1 - Conversation - Discussion at Work

Narrator – Welcome back to E+ Education where everyone is busy working on their new projects. Do you remember what happened with James last time? Let’s see if his sprained ankle affects his working attitude. 

Mary – Everyone, just a quick reminder that the meeting on our X3 Coaching Launch will begin in 5 minutes! 

Kate – Okay, I´m almost ready to join in. 

David – Hello, everyone. Where´s James, by the way? 

Mary – He’s taking a day off to see the doctor, and I have to do all the paperwork for him now. It’s his ankle, not his head! Why can’t he come to work as usual? 

David – Oh, oh, oh! Now, be patient, Mary. Let the guy have a rest. 

Mary – Okay, but I have enough things to do without worrying about his job too. 

David – Okay, everyone, listen up. KoreTech Global has expressed their great interest in our X3 Coaching program. But this is only an early success. We need to really put it out there in the market, and reach more and more people. 

Mary – We could offer 20% off to all clients who license our course this month. 

Kate – Oh no! That’s too much of a discount. 

Mary – Maybe, we can deliver flyers to people on the streets to introduce the course. 

Kate – Oh no. No, I disagree. Handing out flyers is for nothing. Nobody ever buys anything based on a flyer! It´s a useless way to promote your product. 

Narrator – Slow down there, Katie, hold your horses. We’d better find another way, a more sensitive way, to say you disagree. What’s the problem you really want to solve? 

Mary – What’s the problem, Kate? You’ve never been so aggressive before in the office! 

Kate – Oh, well, I’m really sorry. I’m so stressed out about James seeing the doctor. I hope he’s fine. We need him back in the office! 

Narrator – Okay, Kate, I´m pretty sure I have some phrases that can help you with this situation:
“I see what you’re saying, but…”
“I respect your point of view, but from my perspective…”
Or:
“Well, I’m not sure about that…” (Then you can explain why you´re not sure.)
Or, start your sentence with “but” when you want to disagree with what has just been said. Stick to your guns, but always be polite.

Kate – Oh, that’s right. I’ll try again. 

Narrator – And be sure to give good reasons and justification, too. You need to persuade them rather than just say no. 

Kate – Well, I’m not sure about offering 20% off the course to all clients who purchase it. I think we can contact our biggest clients, such as companies and schools, and offer them 10% off the course at the start. And if they can bring in more learners, they can get more and bigger discounts. 

David – Hmm? Will that be attractive enough for them? 

Kate – I think we can really improve our sales by giving discounts to organizations which share our course information on their official websites, Facebook fan pages, etc. And just like that, we can increase virtual exposure to our image, improve product awareness, and then increase sales. 

Mary – What about the flyers I mentioned? 

Kate – Well, I respect your point of view, but from my perspective, this is an online course. It’s better if we run online strategies first, instead of offline ones. In this case, I think we should apply the “80/20 Principle”. 

David – I like that idea. Offering regular clients 10% off the course at the start. Good thinking! And, if they can get more and more learners, they can get more and more discounts. Kate, you need to build some detailed tactics and then present them to us in the Friday meeting. 

Kate – Okay, yes, that´s fine with me. I´m good to go! 

David – All right, now… 

Narrator – It’s not always easy to say that you disagree, but just like Kate, you can be nice and sensitive while giving your own opinion. For Kate, the next challenge is making a presentation to the whole team. Join us again to find out how she handles this big responsibility. Do you think she can pull it off? 


Recording 6.2 - Conversation - Doing Overtime

Lesson 06 - Expanding

Recording 6.2 - Conversation - Doing Overtime

Narrator – We´re here again at E+ Education where things are getting intense with James off nursing his sprained ankle, and Kate in charge of a big presentation. Let’s see how they are doing in their respective pursuits. 

Mary – Oh hi, James! It’s nice to meet you right here in front of the office! Welcome back to work! 

James – Hi, Mary. Thank you for all the paperwork you’ve done for me. That does take up a lot of your time, huh? 

Mary – No, don’t mention it. Here, let me help you get in. It looks like you´re moving rather slowly. 

James – Looks like somebody’s already inside. 

Kate – Second, let me… let me introduce hmmm… 

Mary – Kate! Wake up, Kate! You can´t sleep here! 

Kate – No! You don’t understand what I mean! 

James – Kate! Hey, are you all right?! 

Kate – Hi guys. What are you doing here?
In fact, what am I doing here? 

James – You´re obviously burning the candle at both ends! You need to go home and sleep a bit. 

Kate – Really? Did I do this in my dream? Did I cause a fire? 

Mary – No, Kate! We´re not exactly talking about real candles. 

Narrator – “Burning the candle at both ends” means working too much, sometimes late into the night, and very early before the next day. We also say, “You´re burning the midnight oil.” We´ve all done it, but we need to always be careful to get enough sleep or “shuteye”. 

Kate – Oh, you´re right. 

James – Kate, you´re really putting in the hours. 

Kate – What do you mean? 

James – You´re working more hours every day. And I can see that you´re getting ready to burn out. 

Kate – Burn out? You guys talk a lot about fire and candles, I´m confused. 

Mary – Yeah. You´re running out of energy because you´re working too hard and too much. When a candle burns low and then finally burns out, it´s like a person running out of energy. 

Kate – But this presentation must be finished and finished well. I mean, I’m excited right now, so I can still work some more. 

Mary – I bet you are by listening to you. You´re excited but exhausted! 

James – Don’t let this overtime take a toll on you. In the end, you´ll just make more mistakes! You know what people say: “Haste makes waste.” 

Kate – But… the tactics must be detailed and perfect. I just want to do a very good job. 

James – You know what? You just want to be there to tell everyone about your good job. 

Mary – That’s true. Try not to be as useful as a chocolate teapot. 

Kate – A chocolate teapot? Now you´ve gone from candles to teapots! 

James – You know, a teapot that´s made of chocolate; it can’t be used for any purpose at all. If you heat chocolate, it melts, so it´s useless in fact. 

Mary – Gosh! You don´t look so good either. Talking of chocolate, do you want a hot cup of it? 

Kate – I think so, yes thank you, Mary. That’s very kind of you. 

Narrator – Kate seems to have learned her lesson before it’s too late. Your life, work balance is very important. Devoting all your time to your job will never be welcome if it destroys your health. But you’ll never regret devoting yourself to learning (especially another useful language like English). Enjoy! 


Recording 6.3 - Conversation - Opening a Meeting

Lesson 06 - Expanding

Recording 6.3 - Conversation - Opening a Meeting

Narrator – Today´s an important day for Kate. She’s going to talk about her new product launch plan. Let’s see how things are going for her?
Hey, Katie. Are you feeling better now? Ready for the challenge yet? 

Kate – Yes. I certainly am. Now I’m feeling great after a good night´s sleep, and can totally go in and kill the presentation. 

Narrator – Oh! Be careful of violence, but keep up the positive attitude! 

Kate – Oh… wait. There´s an email here from David.
“Hi all,
I’m running off for an urgent meeting with one of our potential customers. I´ll be about 30 minutes late for today’s discussion on the X3 Coaching launch, I´d like to ask Kate to do the honors and open things.
I hope to be back by the time Kate starts her presentation.
Cheers,
David”
Oh no! I know nothing about opening anything, including an important business meeting! 

Narrator – Okay, calm down, calm down, take a deep breath, in and out. You’ve prepared for this meeting very carefully. Just go ahead. Everything will be just fine. You´re a natural. 

Kate – But, oh, I don’t know how to start the meeting. What should I say? 

Narrator – You can say: “Good morning everyone, it’s a pleasure to be here. Thanks for coming today.” 

Kate – Just that? What more? 

Narrator – Look at the agenda and ask the participants: “Does everyone have a copy of the agenda so that you can follow along?” 

Kate – The agenda. Yes, it’s here. 

Narrator – Tell the participants how many items it has. 

Kate – Items? What does that mean? Things I need to buy later at the store?! 

Narrator – It´s just a way to put things in order; it sounds important and official. You can list them by using words like: “Firstly, secondly, next, and finally”. Remember to add time at the end for any other business that arises before wrapping up. 

Kate – Any other business? What do you mean by that? 

Narrator – There should be time for any other topic people want to discuss other than those on the agenda. To finish, you can say: “Let’s wrap up, or let’s bring the meeting to a close.” 

Kate – Okay. Well, I’ll try my best. Oh, oh no, everybody is already here. It’s time.
Good morning, it’s a pleasure to be here. And thanks for coming today. David will be here in a few minutes which is why he asked me to open this meeting.
Does everyone have a copy of the agenda?
Oh, that’s great. Now, as you can see, there are three main items on the agenda today. Firstly, the new product launch plan. Secondly, a team-building activity. And finally, it’ll be time for any other business, before we wrap up. 

David – Thank you, Kate, for standing in. Oh, you did a really good job on that opening. 

Kate – Oh hi, David. It’s perfect timing. We´re all ready for the presentation on the product launch now. 

David – Good, I can’t wait to hear your presentation and the tactics you´ll put forward. 

Narrator – It wasn´t actually a plan for Kate to open this meeting, but she did a good job anyway. Can she do the same with her presentation? Let´s hope so! Before you find out, let’s practice this one first. Enjoy, and let your voice ring out! 


Recording 6.4 - Conversation - Giving a Presentation

Lesson 06 - Expanding

Recording 6.4 - Conversation - Giving a Presentation

Narrator – In the last lesson, you saw Kate successfully open a meeting. Now, it’s time for her to give her presentation on X3 Coaching. Let’s join her in action! 

Kate – Ted, how should I introduce what I’m gonna say in my upcoming presentation? 

Narrator – You can make it clear by drawing the outline for the participants, like:
“Today I’m going to speak about…” and explain. Then say:
“I’ll start by sharing…”
And then: I’d like to move on to…”
“And finally…”

Kate – Okay, I’ll give it a try…
“Today, I’m going to talk about the first item: Our product launch plan.
I’ll start by telling you about our Coaching Course, its features, and all the benefits for our customers.
Then I’m going to move on to the detailed launch tactics for the Christmas event.
So let’s begin.”
Our new product that we are introducing is going to be X3 Coaching, with it whole entire…

Narrator – Once again, Kate has dealt with the challenges facing her with relative ease. 

Mary – In this chart, you can see the projection…
Oh, no! What’s the matter? Why didn’t the chart come up? 

Narrator – Well, apparently your computer is frozen again or someone pull the plug, Katie. Now, you´ll simply have to do it without the slides and notes. Keep talking naturally as if nothing happened! 

Kate – But I can’t. I can’t think of anything to say without my trusty notes. 

Narrator – List all the key points, and give them in some detail one by one. Firstly, you should start by giving an overview of your company and the product you want to present. These phrases can help you:
“This product has its own key strengths. They are…”
“We’re particularly proud of…”
“E+ Education has a strong record of creating…” 

Kate – Okay. The X3 Coaching Course is one of the online English learning series from our company, E+ Education. As you may know, our company has a strong track record of high-quality e-learning systems, thanks to the use of proven learning methods, as well as updated technology. We’re particularly proud of this outstanding e-learning system, with stellar Online English courses. Now, we’re about to launch the X3 Coaching Course, a first in the market. The X3 Coaching course’s key strengths are one-on-one guidance, flexible scheduling, and personalized learning plans. At this moment, I’d like to move on to the detailed launch tactics for the course… 

Narrator – Wow, excellent! Then, you can talk about your sales tactics for the course. It’s good if you use phrases such as:
“I suggest that…”
“My suggestion is to do…”
“It’s necessary that we…”

Kate – I suggest that we discount 10% off the course for our regular clients. Let´s give 15% off the course if each client brings in the registration number of 1,000. Go to 20% off if they bring in more than 2,000 registrations. It’s necessary that we…ah we… we… 

Narrator – You know it! Kate still worked well, presenting naturally, even though she was in an unexpected situation, the electricity went off, and using only her notes. Well done, you pulled it off! 

David – Sounds interesting! We’ll start to carry out the tactics tomorrow. Kate, you’ll be the one to take care of this project.
Hello, David speaking! Yes… Yes! Oh… Oh… We’re really sorry. We’re going to check the order right now. Give us one minute to get back to you please! 

Mary – What’s going on? 

David – Somehow, we delivered the wrong products to Mr. Takashi Matsuo. Who was it that was in charge of his last order? 

James – It was me! I thought everything was in order!! 

David – Well, unfortunately it wasn´t!! He asked us to make an appointment with him tomorrow to deal with the problem. 

James – I’m sorry, but tomorrow I’m going to have three appointments with some other big clients of our company. So, I really can’t meet with him tomorrow… Maybe the next day… 

Narrator – That’s tough. James will have to take responsibility for sorting out this mistake. What’s going to be the verdict? Join us next time to figure it out!
