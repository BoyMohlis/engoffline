Day 09 - Rapid Response - Getting Acquainted

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 2.5 from 1 to 2 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 2.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Lesson 02 - First Day at Work

Recording 2.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Getting Acquainted

David – Good morning Kate. Welcome aboard.

Kate – Oh woah! Thank you, sir.

David – Oh, just call me David. So, first things first: Let’s go meet everybody in the office.

Kate – Okay, great. Thanks for showing me around.

David – Oh. It’s not a problem. This is Mary Jones, our office assistant. If you want to know anything about our company, you just ask her. She’s got a ton of information at her fingertips.

Kate – Nice to meet you, Mary.

Mary – Nice to meet you, too.

David – Mary will show you to your desk. I’ll see you two later.

Kate – Thank you very much, David.

So Mary, how long have you worked here?

Mary – Long enough to know how much sugar David wants in his coffee. Okay.This is your desk. Feel free to tell me if you need anything.

Kate – Thank you kindly, Mary. Hello, I don’t think we’ve met. I’m Kate Williams, the new Sales Executive. I’ve just joined the team.

James – Nice to meet you.

Kate – Nice to meet you too. Have you worked here long?

James – Oh, yes. I’m really sorry, but I’m quite busy and can’t talk much at the moment.

Kate – Oh, okay, well. That’s fine. I hope to talk to you later.

Mary – Don’t worry. He’s actually a nice guy. He’s just as quiet as a mouse. David asked if all four of us can go out to lunch today.

Kate – That’s a good idea. I’d love to.

James – Yes, fine by me.

 

Topic 2: Having Lunch with Colleagues

James – Here we are… The spectacular Sanwy Restaurant!

Mary – Where should we sit? Ooh, shall we sit in that corner?

David – Yes, let’s sit over there where it’ll be quieter, and we can chat and get to know each other better.

James – OK. Let’s order something to eat. I’m so hungry I could eat a horse!

Waitress – Hello everyone! What would you like to order this afternoon?

Mary – Let me see. Hmm… A plate of chicken, fried rice, sweet and sour prawns for me, please.

David – And, you know. I’m thinking I’ll have the fried chicken this time with fries and a side salad please.

James – The steak with fries and the mixed vegetables for me, if you don’t mind. Kate, what would you like to eat?

Kate – Oh. I’d like to have the roast beef with vegetables and chicken salad on the side 

Waitress – OK. Your orders will be ready shortly.

David – Alright. Kate, it’s your first day working with the team. How’s your day going? Is everything all right so far?

Kate – Quite well, David. People seem interested in our courses. I’m really glad when some of them want to introduce our courses to their friends.

Mary – Really? Well, that’s a good start for you.

David – Yeah, well done, Kate!

Kate – Are you looking forward to the weekend? What are you planning to do?

David – I was thinking I’m going to go back and visit my hometown.

Mary – At this time tomorrow, I’ll be lying on the beach enjoying the sunshine and a few delicious cocktails. Perfect, isn’t it?!

James – Wow, that sounds amazing, Mary! I’ll still be working. There’s so much work I need to finish. What about you, Kate?

Kate – Oh, well, I’ve just joined a swimming club. I often go there on weekends.

 

Topic 3: Asking for Help

Kate – Hi Mary. Could I interrupt you for a moment?

Mary – Yes, Kate? What can I do for you?

Kate – Please, can you help me get the projector ready for the meeting right now?!

Mary – Ohhh, I’m a bit busy at the moment. Can’t you ask somebody else to help?

Kate – Yes, I guess I can try. Thank you anyway.

Mary – Unbelievable. Does she think she’s my boss or something?!

Narrator – Oh Katie, I think you’ve upset Mary…

Kate – What? Why? Did I do something wrong? I was only asking for a little help.

Narrator – Using the word ‘please’ only to ask somebody to do something may sound a little rude.

Kate – Oh, I see what you mean. I’ll keep that in mind. Oh no, the projector is here, but why doesn’t the big screen come up. James? Excuse me, James?!

James – Yeah?

Kate – Could you possibly help me set the projector up for the meeting, please?

James – I’m in the middle of sending this email to a new client. It’s quite important. It’s tough to break away to help you at this very moment!

Kate – But, it’s really urgent, if you could. Would you mind writing that a bit later? It won’t take long to set up the projector. I’m sure!

James – Okay, it’s only because you’ve asked so nicely. Let me see what we can do here.

 

Topic 4: Giving a Hand

James – Arrgh! What should I do? What should I do now?

Kate – You look worried, James. Is everything under control?

James – Yes, I’m worried, but I’m fine.

Kate – Is there anything I can do for you?

James – Thanks for your offer, but no.

Kate – Oh. Please let me help. What’s wrong exactly?

James – Everything! Someone messed up the advertisements for two different courses for two important clients.

Kate – I see. Can I give you a hand?

James – Nobody can help me with Mr. Matsuo. Meanwhile, Mr. Smith is on fire right now.

Kate – Let me help, if possible. I’ve always been a good firefighter.

James – Thanks again Kate, but this really is a difficult and sticky situation.

Kate – I see. Well, just give me a shout any time if you need anything!

James – Um, Kate? Could you help me after all? Maybe? They’re both here. Could you help me with Mr. Smith? We need to do everything possible to keep them on our good side!

Kate – He’s the angry one, then?

James – Yes, I think you can just do some small talk with him. Can you manage that, Kate?

Kate – OK, sure. I’ll do my best to take care of it right away.

James – Mr. Smith! Good to see you again! Are you well? Please, let me introduce you to Kate!

Kate – Hello, Mr. Smith. It’s a pleasure to meet you! Let me help you with your coat.

Smith – Thank you for nothing!

Step 3: Practice rapid response

✔️ Without the transcript, listen to Recording 2.8 from 1 to 2 times. Each time, try to give short answers as fast as you can.

✔️ Use the transcript to get sample answers. Practice responding as instructed in the transcript from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Practice answering to Recording 2.8 from 3 to 5 times until you can give full-sentence responses. Listen and practice your response until you feel confident with your answers.

Note: You need to achieve the following goals:

✔️ Building Comprehension: You can respond to each question with at least 1 out of the 3 given sample answers. 

✔️ In Other Words: You can say complete sentences with similar meanings.

✔️ Language to Go: Can give responsive sentences that correspond to the instructional sentence about the situation.

Part A: Building Comprehension

Has Mary worked there long enough to know how the boss likes his coffee?

Yes, she has.

She´s worked there long enough to know how he likes it.

She´s worked there long enough to know how much sugar he wants in his coffee.

Is Kate excited or nervous on her first day?

She’s both excited and nervous.

She’s excited but nervous.

Kate is both excited and nervous.

Has James worked at the company for a long time?

Yes, he has.

He’s worked there a long time.

He says he has.

Is James too busy to talk at the moment?

Yes, he’s too busy to talk.

He says he can’t talk much right now.

He can’t talk now because he´s too busy.

Is Kate learning the ropes?

Yes, she’s learning her way around.

Indeed she is.

She’s new so she needs to learn the ropes.

What does James Brown do?

He’s the Content Manager.

He manages the company’s content.

James is the Content Manager at the company.

When did Kate join the company?

She just joined.

She just did.

Kate only joined the company recently.

Does Kate have a nickname?

Yes, it’s Katie.

The narrator calls her Katie.

Her nickname’s Katie.

Is James a talkative man?

No, he isn’t.

He isn’t. He’s quiet.

He´s as quiet as a mouse.

Does David invite them out for lunch or dinner?

For lunch.

It’s for lunch.

He invites them out to have lunch.

Part B: In Other Words

We need to start working hard to finish the job on time.

We need to get down to work to finish the job on time.

I hope you won’t make a mistake in class with these irregular verbs.

I hope you don’t screw up in class with these irregular verbs.

Do you think we started well with that client?

Do you think we got off on the right foot with that client?

Would you like me to take you to the best places in the city?

Would you like me to show you around the city?

The secretary seems to find any information easily.

The secretary has any information at her fingertips.

Joe’s a really quiet guy.

Joe’s as quiet as a mouse.

Part C: Language to Go – How to Get Acquainted

Hello. Good morning, and welcome aboard!

Thanks. It’s very nice to be here.

Yes. Nice to meet you.

Nice to meet you too!

Have you worked here long?

No, I’ve just joined the team.

You must be Kate.

Yes, I’m the new Sales Executive. And you are…?

Would you like to go out to lunch with us?

That’s a good idea. I’d love to!

What could you say to introduce yourself to someone new?

Hello, I don’t think we’ve met. I’m Kate Williams, the new Sales Executive. I’ve just joined the team.
