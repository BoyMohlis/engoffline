Day 66 - Rapid Response - Customer survey

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 11.6 from 3 to 5 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Topic 1: Performance Review

Mary – Kate, David wants to talk to you in his office.

Kate – Okay. I’ll go see him right now. Hello, David. Did you want to talk to me?

David – Hi Kate. Yes. Come in and have a seat. So, I need to tell you that Rose was let go.

Kate – Let go? You mean she was fired?

David – Yes, but actually she quit before I could fire her. But that’s not what I want to talk to you about, we’re here to discuss your appraisal.

Kate – So David, do you want to talk about my performance first?

David – Right, I do. What has gone well for you in this past year?

Kate – Well, as for contracts, I landed a big one for the X3 Coaching program… A really big fish!

David – Excellent job! I’m with you on that.

Kate – Well, as for people, working with Rose wasn’t easy. And that Mr. Smith as well.

David – That’s right. You got those two taken care of. And?

Kate – I’ve improved my email language, phone manners, and cold-calling techniques.

David – Yes, I must give you credit for those. Now, what could you be doing more of going forward?

Kate – I guess I could expand the market for X3 Coaching, oh, and try to think of a new product.

David – That’s exactly what we need now, Kate.

Kate – Oh, okay. I can manage that.

David – Now, that’s where you can look ahead and set some objectives. I’d really like you to develop a new product to help us take our company to a new level.

Kate – Well, okay. It won’t be easy, but I’m game.

David – So, would you please write up your appraisal and send it back to me by the end of this week?

Kate – Sure, David.

James – You seem worried. Is it about the talk you had with David just now?

Kate – Well, I’m going to develop a new product. I’m just thinking about that. I’ve already got a headache as a result!

James – You know what, we could do it together.

Kate – That’s great thinking, James. I’d appreciate that!

James – Maybe we could stay a bit later today. We can work on it.

 

Topic 2: Conducting a Proposal

Kate – Thank you, James, for helping me with this.

James – You’re very welcome. Besides, I’ve ordered pizza since I’m really hungry. Hope you don’t mind. In fact, I’m as hungry as a bear.

Kate – Not at all. I should invite you for dinner some time, you’ve been really nice to me.

James – Don’t mention it. Let’s wait for the pizza and then we can start work. So, do you like pizza?

Kate – Yeah, sure. I’m crazy about pizza. And you?

James – Of course. I sometimes even cook my own.

Kate – Wow, you’re really amazing, James. Don’t laugh, but I can’t cook. I’m helpless in the kitchen.

James – It’s okay, we all have our goods and bads, our ups and downs.

Kate – Yeah, but it seems that you don’t have anything bad. Oh, I got it! I’ve got a brilliant idea.

James – What?

Kate – English for Cooking!

James – Yeah! And you can learn cooking while you’re learning English. That would certainly attract a lot of modern people.

Kate – We can partner with a cooking school to do this. We need to put a proposal together to give to David. Oh but what should we say?

James – I got this. We can use positive emphatic language such as ‘exceptional’, ‘fantastic’, and ‘quality’. The proposal’s gotta be brief, clear, and talk about how it would bring benefits. We can use words like ‘profit’, ‘efficiency’, and ‘value for money’.

Kate – I see! I’d better write it down. Keep going!

James – We can also say how we could target modern people. They’ll be begging for our course. Oh, this is so exciting.

Kate – I’m writing it all down. This exceptional product has fantastic potential and see here…… And by making the production more efficient, our clients will get value for money and our profits will increase. Done.

James – Good job, Kate. We can pitch this to David first thing tomorrow.

 

Topic 3: Customer Survey

Kate – Morning, James. Are you ready for today?

James – Well, yeah, I’m still thinking…

Kate – Don’t tell me you’re having second thoughts and suddenly you think this product can’t work.

James – No, Kate. Before proposing to David, we need to do some research, don’t we?

Kate – What kind of research?

James – A customer survey would be best.

Kate – I think I’m gonna need some help cold-calling. Would you mind, James?

James – No problem. We could also ask Mary to help.

Kate – That’s right. Mary always likes to give a helping hand.

James – Hey, Mary. Would you help us call some people? We need to find out how exactly they want to learn English.

Mary – Nice. I love talking on the phone.

James – Lucky us to have you on board.

Kate – Thanks, Mary. Let’s start, everybody! Here I have a list that I’ve made for you. Could you make calls to these people here?

Mary – Of course. I’ll get on it right away.

Kate – Great, and James, could you help me by cold-calling some new sources?

James – Sure. I’ve got my list here.

Kate – I suppose I’d better make some calls too.

 

Topic 4: Pitching Your Proposal

James – So Kate, what does David think about your proposal?

Kate – Our proposal, James. Give yourself credit actually, you deserve most of the credit. But I haven’t talked to him yet.

Mary – Why not? Are you chickening out again? You’ve got to get up the nerve!

Kate – No, Mary. I’m still brainstorming some ideas.

James – Right, Kate. You should be well-prepared, but don’t take too long. There’s no time like the present-did you ever hear that before?

Kate – You’re absolutely right. I should talk to him as soon as possible. Hello David. James and I have come up with an exciting new idea – Learning to Cook in English.

David – Good! Good! Well?!

Kate – This fantastic product will be a great addition to modern learning.

David – Interesting, but how will it be a profitable addition to our company?

Kate – We can use the format and resources of the last courses to increase efficiency, and at the same time, maintain quality.

David – What about profits?

Kate – By my calculation, we can increase profits by 20%. That’s based on selling the same quantity we now sell of X3 Coaching.

David – That’s an impressive figure! How did you come up with that?

Kate – We’ve conducted a survey and already found 15 new organizations which would buy this, and 36 existing customers who have shown interest in it too…… And I’m completely confident that this new product will be a huge success.

David – Yeah, it does sound promising. I’ll take a closer look at what you wrote here.

Kate – Thanks, David.

Some facts and figures from their research.

Kate’s going to add some facts and figures from their research to make her presentation more persuasive.

Learning to Cook in English.

Part C: Language to Go – How to Present Your Proposal

What could you say to your colleague to urge her to pitch her proposal now?

Remember, there’s no time like the present to pitch your proposal.

I can add some facts and figures from our latest research to do that.

How could you tell your boss that you’ve created an exciting new idea?

We’ve come up with an exciting new idea.

What could you say to emphasize that this will be a great addition to your area?

This fantastic product will be a great addition to modern learning.

How could you stress that your proposal will increase your company’s profits by 20%?

By my calculation, we can increase our company’s profits by 20%.

I’m completely confident that this new product will be a huge success.
