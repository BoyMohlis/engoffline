Lesson 02 - First Day at Work

Recording 2.1 - Conversation - Getting Acquainted

Narrator – In the last lesson, thinking on her feet got Kate the job at E+ Education. Now it’s time to get down to work. Kate, how´re you feeling on your first day?

Kate – I’m excited, but nervous at the same time. I hope I don’t screw up and say anything wrong.

Narrator – Oh, don’t worry, Kate. I have some expressions for you to introduce yourself politely here. For example: “Hello, I don’t think we’ve met before.” “You must be…” and then say the person’s name. “I’ve just joined the team.” “Very nice to meet you.” “Have you worked here long?”

Kate – Oh, thanks so much for getting me off on the right foot! I need all the help I can get.

David – Good morning Kate. Welcome aboard.

Kate – Oh woah! Thank you, sir.

David – Oh, just call me David. So, first things first: Let’s go meet everybody in the office.

Kate – Okay, great. Thanks for showing me around.

David – Oh. It’s not a problem. Eh… This is Mary Jones, our office assistant. If you want to know anything about our company, such as company policies or employment procedures, you just ask her. She´s got a ton of information at her fingertips.

Kate – Nice to meet you, Mary.

Mary – Nice to meet you, too.

David – Mary will show you to your desk. I’ll see you two later.

Kate – Thank you very much, David. So Mary, how long have you worked here?

Mary – Long enough to know how much sugar David wants in his coffee. Okay. This is your desk. Feel free to tell me if you need anything.

Kate – Thank you kindly, Mary.

Narrator – Good start, Katie. Now get busy introducing yourself to the others. That’s James Brown, the Content Manager, for example.

Kate – Hello, I don’t think we’ve met. I’m Kate Williams, the new Sales Executive. I’ve just joined the team.

James – Nice to meet you.

Kate – Nice to meet you too. Have you worked here long?

James – Oh, yes. I’m really sorry, but I’m quite busy and can´t talk much at the moment.

Kate – Oh, okay, well. That´s fine. I hope to talk to you later.

Mary – Don’t worry. He’s actually a nice guy. He’s just as quiet as a mouse. David asked if all four of us can go out to lunch today.

Kate – That’s a good idea. I’d love to.

James – Yes, fine by me.

Narrator – You did a good job, Katie. You´re learning the ropes. Now it’s time for you to practice before seeing what’s waiting for Kate ahead.


Recording 2.2 - Conversation - Having Lunch with Colleagues

Lesson 02 - First Day at Work

Recording 2.2 - Conversation - Having Lunch with Colleagues

James – Here we are… The spectacular Sanwy Restaurant!

Mary – Where should we sit? Ooh, shall we sit in that corner?

David – Yes yes yes yeah, let’s sit over there where it’ll be quieter, and we can chat and get to know each other better.

James – Okay. Let’s order something to eat. I’m so hungry I could eat a horse!

Narrator – Katie, this is your first lunch with your colleagues. Remember to turn off your phone. Having lunch with colleagues is not a time to send text messages or check your incoming emails. And, don’t bring up business at your meals while eating with colleagues. That may turn their stomachs! To order food, you can say these phrases: “I’d like…” “I’ll have…” And don’t forget to say “please” when you finish your order.

Kate – Hmm. Yes, I’ll remember all your tips! Thanks a million!

Waitress – Hello everyone! What would you like to order this afternoon?

Mary – Let me see. Hmm… A plate of chicken, fried rice, sweet and sour prawns for me, please.

David – And, you know. I’m thinking I’ll have the fried chicken this time with fries and a side salad please.

James – The steak with fries and the mixed vegetables for me, if you don’t mind. Kate, what would you like to eat?

Kate – Oh. Erm. Well, uhm, I’d like to have the roast beef with vegetables and chicken salad on the side please.

Waitress – Okay. Your orders will be ready shortly.

David – Alright. Kate, it’s your first day working with the team. How´s your day going? Is everything all right so far? I hope that everything will be comfortable and we hope that you´ll be comfortable working with us.

Kate – Quite well, David. People seem interested in our courses. I’m really glad when some of them want to introduce our courses to their friends.

Mary – Really? Well, that’s a good start for you.

David – Yeah, well done, Kate!

Narrator – Now it’s time for you to break the ice with some of the questions about your colleague’s hobbies, their families, and their plans for the weekend, such as: “What hobbies interest you? Do you play (or Do you follow) tennis?” “Are you looking forward to the weekend? Do you have any plans? Are you going to do anything unusual?”

Kate – Oh, yeah! Are you looking forward to the weekend? What are you planning to do?

David – Well, you know, I was thinking I’m going to go back and visit my hometown. I haven’t been there for so long.

Mary – At this time tomorrow, I’ll be lying on the beach enjoying the sunshine and a few delicious cocktails. Perfect, isn’t it?!

James – Wow, that sounds amazing, Mary! I’ll still be working. There’s so much work I need to finish. What about you, Kate?

Kate – Oh, well, I’ve just joined a swimming club. I often go there on weekends.

Waitress – Here are your meals. If you need anything else, just ask. Enjoy your lunch.

Narrator – So they have lunch together for the first time. Everything seems to be going smoothly, and hopefully it’ll continue for the day. Now it’s time for you to practice before seeing Kate in the afternoon. No dessert until you practice, so enjoy!


Recording 2.3 - Conversation - Asking for Help

Lesson 02 - First Day at Work

Recording 2.3 - Conversation - Asking for Help

Narrator – Here we are again at E+ Education where Kate is managing to get by in her first week. How are you doing out there, Katie? Are you navigating the waters?

Kate – I’m still struggling to get used to everything. I have to ask for help all the time.

Narrator – Well, that’s perfectly normal in the beginning. But remember: When you’re asking for help, use the words “would” and “could” to be polite, or the word “please”. Be careful also because it can sometimes be considered rude to ask for too much help.

Kate – I’m gonna look at my checklist for today’s meeting preparation. *Book the room – check *Print the agenda – check *Prepare the documents – check *Set up the projector – Oh no! I need to admit that I left this out because I have no idea how to do it. I’m totally lost when it comes to projectors. I have to go ask Mary and get it done now.

Mary – See? I told you that you need matching shoes

Kate – Hi Mary. Could I interrupt you for a moment?

Mary – Hold on a moment. Yes, Kate? What can I do for you?

Kate – Please, can you help me get the projector ready for the meeting right now?!

Mary – Ohhh, I’m a bit busy at the moment. Can’t you ask somebody else to help?

Kate – Yes, I guess I can try. Thank you anyway.

Mary – Did you hear that? Unbelievable. Does she think she’s my boss or something?!

Narrator – Oh Katie, I think you’ve upset Mary…

Kate – What? Why? Did I do something wrong? I was only asking for a little help.

Narrator – Using the word ‘please’ only to ask somebody to do something may sound a little rude.

Kate – Oh, I see what you mean.

Narrator – You could say, “Could you possibly help me prepare the projector in the meeting room?” or “Would you mind preparing the projector in the meeting room?” instead of “Please prepare the projector!” That sounds like a command, not a request!

Kate – Thank you for your kind advice! I’ll keep that in mind. Oh no, the projector is here, but why doesn´t the big screen come up. James? Excuse me, James?!

James – Yeah?

Kate – Could you possibly help me set the projector up for the meeting, please?

James – I’m in the middle of sending this email to a new client. It’s quite important. It´s tough to break away to help you at this very moment!

Kate – But, it’s really urgent, if you could. Would you mind writing that a bit later? It won’t take long to set up the projector, I’m sure!

James – Okay, it’s only because you’ve asked so nicely. Let me see what we can do here.

Narrator – The projector seems to be working well, but how well can Kate and Mary get on with each other after this? Now it’s time for you to practice before you get to know more about how the story unfolds.


Recording 2.4 - Conversation - Giving a Hand

Lesson 02 - First Day at Work

Recording 2.4 - Conversation - Giving a Hand

James – Arrgh! What should I do? What should I do now?

Kate – Ted, it looks like James is struggling with his work. I want to help, but what should I say?

Narrator – Well, that’s a really good observation, Kate. You can say something like: “Is there anything I can do for you?” “Let me help you!” Or, to be a bit more informal, you can say: “Give me a shout out any time if you need anything!” “Can I give you a hand?” “Do you need a helping hand?”

Kate – Thank you. I’ll go ask to help him right away. You look worried, James. Is everything under control?

James – Yes, I’m worried, but I’m fine.

Kate – Is there anything I can do for you?

James – Thank you for your offer, but no.

Kate – Oh. Please let me help. What’s wrong exactly? 

James – Everything! Someone messed up the advertisements for two different courses for two important clients. Now, one of them, Mr. Mark Smith is quite angry, and the other, Mr. Takashi Matsuo, wants to find out more about the course, “Ulti-Pronunciation”, that was sent to him by mistake.

Kate – I see. Can I give you a hand?

James – Nobody can help me with Mr. Matsuo because “Ulti-Pronunciation” is in the final revision stage, and I´m the only one who understands it! Meanwhile, Mr. Smith is on fire right now.

Kate – Let me help, if possible. I’ve always been a good firefighter.

James – Thanks again Kate, but this really is a difficult and sticky situation.

Kate – I see. Well, just give me a shout any time if you need anything!

James – Um, Kate? Could you help me after all? Maybe? They’re both here. Could you help me with Mr. Smith? He’s the president of Bright International School. They buy 5,000 “English Breaking” course sets each quarter. We need to do everything possible to keep them on our good side!

Kate – He’s the angry one, then?

James – Yes, I think you can just do some small talk with him. Start by saying sorry on behalf of E+ Education. Our E-Learning website crashed in the middle of an exam. Can you manage that, Kate?

Kate – Okay, sure. I’ll do my best to take care of it right away.

James – Mr. Smith! Good to see you again! Are you well? Please, let me introduce you to Kate!

Kate – Hello, Mr. Smith. It’s a pleasure to meet you! Let me help you with your coat.

Smith – Thank you for nothing!

Narrator – Looks like Kate is suddenly in a difficult situation. But, before you find out how she handles an angry client with kid gloves, you have to practice today´s lesson first. Have fun!
