Day 70 - Rapid Response - Immediate challenges

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 12.5 from 1 to 2 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 12.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Lesson 12 - New Chances

Recording 12.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Being in Charge

Kate – Mary, have you seen David? Isn’t he supposed to be here today?

Mary – He’s just called in sick, Kate. He wanted you to call him back. Looks like something’s important.

James – What’s that, Mary?

Mary – David caught a bad cold and is now in the hospital. Can you believe it?

Kate – I’ll call him now.

David – Hello,

Kate – Hello David, it’s Kate from your office. Are you okay?

David – Oh, not really. I caught a cold yesterday, and with a full night’s rest, I should’ve recovered by now. But that strong coffee I drank kept me awake all night. And now I’m in the hospital, feeling totally run down.

Kate – Oh dear, you should really cut down on your coffee. So, you’re not coming to work today?

David – I’m afraid not, I need to rest for a few days, so I’m asking if you could take my place for just a few days?

Kate – David, I don’t know what to do, let alone how to do your job.

David – It’s nothing big or difficult. You can ask Mary for the task list to know what must be finished in terms of priority. Thank you in advance, Kate.

Narrator – That’s great, Kate. You’ve got all the skills, and now you need to really take charge and show everyone who’s boss!

Kate – Now everyone, as you know, David has put me in charge for now, so if you have any issues about your work, please come and speak to me directly.

James – Good to hear, Kate. At the same time, tell me if you need anything.

Kate – Right. Now, it’s business as usual, and if there’s anything you want to ask, my door is always open. And James, could I have a word with you in my office now?

 

Topic 2: Immediate Challenges

James – So, what’s the matter, Kate?

Kate – I found this note on David’s calendar. It says – Closing date – finalized.

James – What exactly does that mean?

Kate – Is this possibly the end for E+ Education?

James – Well, I don’t think so, but…

Kate – Mary? How long have you been here?

Mary – I just got here now. I didn’t hear anything.

James – Oh, dear. Mary, you mustn’t spread rumors in the office, okay?

Mary – What? Of course not. Oh no, Natalie, we’re all gonna be fired.

Kate – Oh, no, Mary is spreading rumors about job cuts. Everyone’s starting to panic.

James – Call a meeting to clear the air right away, Kate!

Mary – Are you going to fire us all?!

Kate – No, Mary. Any rumors about firing are simply not true.

James – That’s right. Rumors are rumors. Don’t pay attention to the rumor mill!

Kate – I’m trying to establish the facts, and if I get any information, I’ll let you know straight away.

Mary – But, what if they are true?

Kate – Ignore the rumors, and please don’t pass them on. Could you please just carry on with your work as normal? Thank you all for your efforts.

James – Call David, Kate. He’s the one who knows.

Kate – You’re right. I’ll do it now.

 

Topic 3: Award Winner

David – Hello, David speaking.

Kate – Hello David. It’s Kate. Sorry to bother you. I know you’ve been out sick. Are you feeling better?

David – I’m good, getting better. Thanks. So, is everything okay over there?

Kate – Not really. I found one of your notes that says “Closing date – finalized” and now, there are rumors swirling around about closing down the company.

David – Oh dear! It’s about the ESL conference tomorrow. I managed to register E+ Education just before the closing date.

Kate – I see. That’s it? As simple as that?!

David – And we’ve been nominated for the Innovative Education Award. Kate, you will have to be there with, well with some kind of acceptance speech.

Kate – But I’ve never done it before. I haven’t the least idea of how to make an acceptance speech!

David – Ask James and Mary for their help. I bet you’ll kill it.

Kate – Ok, I will, and try not to get killed in the process! Get well soon. James, can I speak to you for a minute please?

James – Sure! Did you talk to David already?

Kate – Just hung up the phone with him. So no job cuts, no layoffs, nobody fired. I’ll ask Mary to tell everyone.

James – That’s good news for all of us.

Kate – We’ve been nominated for the Innovative Education Award. Please can you go with me tomorrow? I’m a bit nervous. In fact, I’m already shaking like a leaf!

James – Sure. I’ll be right by your side. So, here we are at the conference. Are you ready to receive the award yet, Kate?

Kate – If we win, I am.

Announcer – Ladies and gentlemen, please take your seats for our special award ceremony. Thank you everyone for being here. And the highlight of today is the winner of our Innovative Teaching Award. And this year, the winner is… E+ Education with its brand new X3 Coaching program.

James – Well, Kate, it’s time for you to shine. Break a leg!

Kate – Ladies and gentlemen. “On behalf of everyone at E+ Education, thank you for our award. Our company is extremely proud to stand at the forefront of teaching innovation.” This award is a real boost for everyone working at the company. We pride ourselves on innovation, quality, and of course, cutting-edge education. And coming soon, we’ll be launching a series of new courses. Stay tuned! So thank you again everyone, this really means a lot to us.

 

Topic 4: Networking

James – David asked us to do some networking, so this is your best chance, Kate.

Kate – What exactly am I supposed to do?

James – You can talk to some potential customers, finding connections with people and organizations to expand your network. Be yourself, and wear a smile.

Kate – Thanks. I’ll give it a go. Hello, I’m Kate, E+ Education Sales Executive.

Bob – Oh hi, congratulations on your award. I’m Bob Sanders from the School of Elite. The conference is very well organized, isn’t it?

Kate – It is. I was a bit nervous about speaking to a big audience here, but I guess I did all right.

Bob – Well, you did a good job, in my humble opinion. So, you’re about to launch a new product?

Kate – Oh yes, I think it will be attractive to our target learners. So, can you tell me more about your school, the School of Elite you said? It’s a most unique name!

Bob – Well, we are a boarding school for girls. Our aim is to educate smart, independent, free-thinking and good-hearted girls. We’re also devoted to providing a thorough education, filled with knowledge and life skills.

Kate – I can tell you, that is exactly how we try to develop our courses at E+ Education.

Bob – Great. We’re having trouble with our English curriculum now. Most of the textbooks are too academic and frankly boring, you know. Nothing about real life unfortunately.

Kate – That’s true. It’s been always an issue in schools.

Bob – Yes, can you imagine how difficult it is for us to design our program with such limited English courses available?!

Kate – I completely understand. I think we might be in a good position to help you with that.

Bob – Oh well, I certainly hope that’s true. We need help from somebody, and soon!

Kate – If I could have your contact details, I’ll send you one of our brochures and some other relevant information

Bob – That’s great. Thank you so much! I’m going to reach out to you soon.

Kate – It’s my pleasure.

Part A: Building Comprehension

Why is E+ Education a little chaotic?

Because David is hospitalized, and Kate is the acting boss now.

E+ Education is a little disordered because David is in the hospital and Kate is in charge of things.

E+ Education is a bit chaotic since David got sick and Kate’s the acting boss.

What’s the note on David’s calendar?

The note says: “Closing date – finalized”.

The note on David’s calendar is: “Closing date – finalized”.

“Closing date – finalized” is the note on David’s calendar.

What does Kate think the note is about?

She thinks this is possibly the end for E+ Education.

Kate thinks the note means that this is the end for E+ Education.

Kate thinks that E+ Education will be closed.

Who heard James and Kate’s conversation?

Mary heard the conversation.

Mary heard the conversation in reality, but said she didn’t hear anything.

Mary said she didn’t hear anything, but in fact she heard all their conversation.

Does James ask Mary not to spread rumors in the office?

Yes, he asks Mary not to spread rumors in the office.

James tells Mary to keep silent about the note in the office.

He asks her not to tell anyone in the office about the note.

Why’s everyone starting to panic?

Because Mary’s spreading rumors about job cuts.

Everybody is starting to panic because Mary is spreading rumors about job cuts.

Everyone in the office is afraid because rumors about job cuts are spreading.

What does James tell Kate to do?

James tells Kate to call a meeting.

He tells Kate to call a meeting to clear the air right away.

James tells her to clear the air right away in the meeting.

What does Kate say to stop the nasty rumors?

“There are some rumors going around that aren’t true.”

“I’m busy trying to establish the facts, and I’ll let you all know as soon as I have some clear information.”

“I would be grateful if you hear any rumors, not to pass them on.”

Can Kate handle the rumors?

Yes, she can.

Kate says this is hard, but she can handle it.

Kate says she can put a damper on these rumors.

What do Kate and James say to stop the rumors?

“Any rumors about firing are simply not true.”

“Rumors are rumors. Don’t pay attention to the rumor mill!”

“Ignore the rumors, and please don’t pass them on.”

Part B: In Other Words

She insists on repeating false information.

She insists on spreading rumors.

The president wanted to clarify the economic situation.

The president wanted to clear the air on the economic situation.

Why does he continue to make fun of his boss behind his back?

Why does he go around making fun of his boss behind his back?

They were occupied with the preparation for the sales conference all week.

They were busy doing the preparation for the sales conference all week.

The secretary asked me to relate the message to my colleague later.

The secretary asked me to pass the message on to my colleague later.

Our boss asked us to stop joking so much during working hours.

Our boss asked us to put a damper on our joking during working hours.

Don’t focus on the bad things people are saying about our company, they’re not true!

Don’t pay attention to the bad things people are saying about our company, they’re not true!

If you learn you’re doing the wrong thing, then don’t continue to do it.

If you learn you’re doing the wrong thing, then don’t carry on doing it.

There will be many obstacles in your path, but you must persevere.

There are many obstacles lying ahead, but you must persevere.

Part C: Language to Go – How to Extinguish Office Rumors

What could you say if there are rumors going around your office that you believe aren’t real?

There are some rumors going around the office that aren’t true.

How could you let your colleagues know that the information at the moment isn’t clear?

I’m busy trying to establish the facts, and I’ll let you know as soon as I have some clear information.

What could you say to your coworkers to discourage them from passing rumors on?

Ignore the rumors, and please don’t pass them on.

What would you say if you wanted everybody to squash or stop the rumors?

We need to put a damper on these rumors.

How could you tell your colleagues that you want to clarify the situation?

I want to clear the air regarding these rumors.

How would you tell your colleagues to continue working despite rumors?

Could you please just carry on with your work as normal despite these rumors?
