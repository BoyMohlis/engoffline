Day 34 - Rapid Response - Doing overtime

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 6.5 from 1 to 2 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 6.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Lesson 06 - Expanding

Recording 6.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Discussion at Work

David – Hello, everyone. Where’s James, by the way?

Mary – He’s taking a day off to see the doctor, and I have to do all the paperwork for him now. It’s his ankle, not his head! Why can’t he come to work as usual?

David – Now, be patient, Mary. Let the guy have a rest.

Mary – Okay, but I have enough things to do without worrying about his job too.

David – Okay, everyone, listen up. KoreTech Global has expressed their great interest in our X3 Coaching program. But this is only an early success. We need to really put it out there in the market, and reach more and more people.

Mary – We could offer 20% off to all clients who license our course this month.

Kate – Well, I’m not sure about offering 20% off the course to all clients who purchase it. I think we can contact our biggest clients, such as companies and schools, and offer them 10% off the course at the start. And if they can bring in more learners, they can get more and bigger discounts.

David – Will that be attractive enough for them?

Kate – I think we can really improve our sales by giving discounts to organizations which share our course information on their official websites, Facebook fan pages, etc. And just like that, we can increase virtual exposure to our image, improve product awareness, and then increase sales.

Mary – What about the flyers I mentioned?

Kate – Well, I respect your point of view, but from my perspective, this is an online course. It’s better if we run online strategies first, instead of offline ones. In this case, I think we should apply the “80/20 Principle”.

David – I like that idea. Offering regular clients 10% off the course at the start.

 

Topic 2: Doing Overtime

Mary – Oh hi, James! It’s nice to meet you right here in front of the office! Welcome back to work!

James – Hi, Mary. Thank you for all the paperwork you’ve done for me. That does take up a lot of your time, huh?

Mary – No, don’t mention it. Here, let me help you get in. It looks like you´re moving rather slowly.

James – Looks like somebody’s already inside.

Mary – Kate! Wake up, Kate! You can´t sleep here!!

James – Kate! Hey, are you all right?!

Kate – Hi guys. What are you doing here? In fact, what am I doing here?

James – You’re obviously burning the candle at both ends! You need to go home and sleep a bit.

Kate – Oh, you’re right.

James – Kate, you’re really putting in the hours.

Kate – But this presentation must be finished and finished well. I mean, I’m excited right now, so I can still work some more.

Mary – I bet you are by listening to you. You’re excited but exhausted!

James – Don’t let this overtime take a toll on you. In the end, you´ll just make more mistakes! You know what people say: “Haste makes waste.”

Kate – But… The tactics must be detailed and perfect. I just want to do a very good job.

James – You know what? You just want to be there to tell everyone about your good job.

Mary – That’s true. Try not to be as useful as a chocolate teapot.

 

Topic 3: Opening a Meeting

Narrator – Hey, Katie. Are you feeling better now? Ready for the challenge yet?

Kate – Yes. I certainly am. Now I’m feeling great after a good night´s sleep, and I can totally go in and kill the presentation.

Narrator – Oh! Be careful of violence, but keep up the positive attitude!

Kate – Oh… Wait. There’s an email here from David. “Hi all, I’m running off for an urgent meeting with one of our potential customers. I’ll be about 30 minutes late for today’s discussion on the X3 Coaching launch, I’d like to ask Kate to do the honors and open things. I hope to be back by the time Kate starts her presentation. Cheers, David”. Oh no! I know nothing about opening anything, including an important business meeting!

Narrator – Okay, calm down, calm down, take a deep breath, in and out. You’ve prepared for this meeting very carefully. Just go ahead. Everything will be just fine. You´re a natural.

Kate – But, I don’t know how to start the meeting. What should I say?

Narrator – You can say: “Good morning everyone, it’s a pleasure to be here. Thanks for coming today.”

Kate – Just that? What more?

Narrator – Look at the agenda and ask the participants: “Does everyone have a copy of the agenda so that you can follow along?”

Kate – The agenda. Yes, it’s here. Okay. Well, I’ll try my best. Oh, oh no, everybody is already here. It’s time. Good morning, it’s a pleasure to be here. And thanks for coming today. David will be here in a few minutes which is why he asked me to open this meeting. Does everyone have a copy of the agenda? Oh, that’s great. Now, as you can see, there are three main items on the agenda today. Firstly, the new product launch plan. Secondly, a team-building activity. And finally, it’ll be time for any other business, before we wrap up.

David – Thank you, Kate, for standing in. Oh, you did a really good job on that opening.

Kate – Oh hi, David. It’s perfect timing. We’re all ready for the presentation on the product launch now.

David – Good, I can’t wait to hear your presentation and the tactics you’ll put forward.

 

Topic 4: Giving a Presentation

Kate – “Today, I’m going to talk about the first item: Our product launch plan. I’ll start by telling you about our Coaching Course, its features, and all the benefits for our customers. Then I’m going to move on to the detailed launch tactics for the Christmas event. So let’s begin.” The X3 Coaching Course is one of the online English learning series from our company, E+ Education. As you may know, our company has a strong track record of high-quality e-learning systems, thanks to the use of proven learning methods, as well as updated technology. We’re particularly proud of this outstanding e-learning system, with stellar Online English courses. Now, we’re about to launch the X3 Coaching Course, a first in the market. The X3 Coaching course’s key strengths are one-on-one guidance, flexible scheduling, and personalized learning plans. I suggest that we discount 10% off the course for our regular clients. Let´s give 15% off the course if each client brings in the registration number of 1,000. Go to 20% off if they bring in more than 2,000 registrations.

David – Sounds interesting! We’ll start to carry out the tactics tomorrow. Kate, you’ll be the one to take care of this project. Hello, David speaking! Yes… Yes! Oh… Oh… We’re really sorry. We’re going to check the order right now. Give us one minute to get back to you please!

Mary – What’s going on?

David – Somehow, we delivered the wrong products to Mr. Takashi Matsuo. Who was it that was in charge of his last order?

James – It was me! I thought everything was in order!!

David – Well, unfortunately it wasn’t!! He asked us to make an appointment with him tomorrow to deal with the problem.

James – I’m sorry, but tomorrow I’m going to have three appointments with some other big clients of our company. So, I really can’t meet with him tomorrow.

Part A: Building Comprehension

Does James have a nurse taking care of his sprained ankle?

No, he’s nursing it himself.

James is taking care of his ankle, not a nurse.

James is nursing his ankle by himself.

What’s Kate in charge of at the moment?

A big presentation.

She’s in charge of a big presentation.

Kate’s responsible for an important presentation.

Which task takes up a lot of Mary’s time?

Doing the paperwork for James.

Doing paperwork for James takes up a lot of her time.

Looking after James’s paperwork takes up a lot of Mary’s time.

Who’s burning the candle at both ends?

It’s Kate.

Kate’s burning the candle at both ends.

Kate’s guilty of it.

What must Kate do now?

She must finish the presentation well.

Kate has to finish her presentation well.

She needs to finish the presentation and well.

Is the overtime taking its toll on her?

Yes, it is.

The overtime is taking its toll.

Kate’s suffering from the overtime.

Why’s Kate running out of energy?

Because she’s working too hard.

Kate’s working too hard and too much.

She’s doing way too much work.

Is Kate in danger of burning out?

Yes, she is.

She is because she’s really putting in the hours.

Kate’s at risk of burning out.

Why do people say “Haste makes waste”?

Because when you hurry, you make mistakes.

Because when you rush, you make more mistakes.

It’s because haste creates waste and mistakes.

Does Kate want a cup of hot chocolate?

Yes, she does.

She wants one.

Kate wants a cup of it.

Part B: In Other Words

Why do you think his job is occupying all his time?

Why do you think his job is taking up all his time?

We’re all working so much that we’re totally exhausted.

We’re all burning the candle at both ends so we’re totally exhausted.

He has the habit of staying up late every night to read.

He has the habit of burning the midnight oil every night to read.

If they don’t slow down and relax more, they’re going to run out of energy.

If they don’t slow down and relax more, they’re going to burn out.

Working late every night has badly affected her relationships.

Working late every night has taken a toll on her relationships.

Remember that by going so fast, you make many mistakes, so try to practice your English more slowly and carefully.

Remember that haste makes waste, so try to practice your English more slowly and carefully.

The teacher doesn’t want us to feel bad or sorry that we didn’t study English so much!

The teacher doesn’t want us to regret studying English so much!

I’m not worried about dedicating more time to my career.

I’m not worried about devoting more time to my career.

Part C: Language to Go – What to Say to Someone Doing Too Much Work

I’m so completely tired that I can’t even think straight!

You’re obviously burning the candle at both ends, you need to go home and sleep a bit.

He’s been working until after midnight all week.

He’s burning the midnight oil, so he needs to be careful to get enough sleep.

I can feel that I’m getting ready to burn out.

In that case, you’re running out of energy because you’re working too much. You should take a rest.

I’m really tired, but I still need to finish this presentation by tomorrow.

Don’t let this overtime take its toll on you. Try to take more breaks while you’re working on the presentation.

I’m working so fast that I’m starting to make a lot of mistakes.

Haste makes waste! You must slow down when you’re working so you don’t make so many mistakes.

I feel like I’m devoting all my time to my job, and I have almost no free time to spend with my family and friends.

Maybe you need to have a chat with your boss about your life-work balance.
