Lesson 11 New Product

Recording 11.1 - Conversation - Performance Review

Narrator – Here we are again at E+ Education where Kate has just had a difficult time working with another colleague. Will the situation improve at all after their serious talk last time? Let’s find out. 

Mary – Kate, David wants to talk to you in his office. 

Kate – Oh, okay. I’ll go see him right now. Hello, David. Did you want to talk to me? 

David – Hi Kate. Yes. Come in and have a seat. So, I need to tell you that Rose was let go. 

Kate – Let go? You mean she was fired? 

David – Yes, but actually she quit before I could fire her. But that’s not what I want to talk to you about. We’re here to discuss your appraisal. 

Kate – What is that? Do you want to tell me what I’m worth? 

David – Well, it’s actually an annual discussion with our staff about their work over the past year, what has been good, what hasn’t, and set the stage for the future, and what we can do to address any shortcomings. 

Kate – Oh, so it’s about my performance review and objectives for the next year? 

Narrator – That’s exactly right, Katie. This section is to improve your performance and skills. David might ask you these questions: “What’s gone well for you this year?” “What didn’t go so well?” “What should you do more of?” “Let’s look ahead and set some objectives for the near future.” 

Kate – Oh, okay. Thank you for helping me prep myself. So David, do you want to talk about my performance first? 

David – Right, I do. What… What has gone well for you in this past year? 

Narrator – Kate! This is your chance to sell yourself. Think about what you’ve achieved in terms of skills, contracts, and how you’ve dealt with people, clients and, so on and so forth. 

Kate – Well, as for contracts, I landed a big one for the X3 Coaching program… A really big fish! 

David – Excellent job! I’m with you on that. 

Kate – Well, as for people, working with Rose wasn’t easy. And that Mr. Smith as well. 

David – That’s right. You got those two taken care of. And? 

Kate – I’ve improved my email language, phone manners, and cold-calling techniques. 

David – Yes, I must give you credit for those. Now, what could you be doing more of going forward? 

Kate – I guess I could expand the market for X3 Coaching, oh, and try to think of a new product. 

David – That’s exactly what we need now, Kate. 

Kate – Oh, okay. Well, I can manage that. 

David – Now, that’s where you can look ahead and set some objectives. I’d really like you to develop a new product to help us take our company to a new level. 

Kate – Well, okay. It won’t be easy, but I’m game. 

David – So, would you please write up your appraisal and send it back to me by the end of this week? 

Kate – Sure, David. 

James – You seem worried. Is it about the talk you had with David just now? 

Kate – Well, I’m going to try to develop a new product. I’m just thinking about that. I’ve already got a headache as a result! 

James – You know what, we could do it together. 

Kate – Oh, that’s great thinking, James. I’d appreciate that! 

James – Maybe we could stay a bit later today. We can work on it. 

Narrator – So, that’s it for the appraisal, and also a chance for Kate and James to spend some time together. Let’s see if their time together results in more than just a new product. 


Recording 11.2 - Conversation - Conducting a Proposal

Lesson 11 New Product

Recording 11.2 - Conversation - Conducting a Proposal

Narrator – We’re here again at E+ Education where Kate and James are about to stay late to work on a new product, and give each other a helping hand. Let’s see if things go well for them. 

Kate – Thank you, James, for helping me with this. 

James – You’re very welcome. Besides, I’ve ordered pizza since I’m really hungry. Hope you don’t mind. In fact, I’m as hungry as a bear. 

Kate – Oh, no, no, not at all. I should invite you for dinner some time. You’ve been really nice to me. 

James – Don’t mention it. Let’s wait for the pizza and then we can start work. So, do you like pizza? 

Kate – Yeah, sure. I’m crazy about pizza. And you? 

James – Of course. I sometimes even cook my own. 

Kate – Ha, you’re really amazing, James. Don’t laugh, but uh, I can’t cook. I’m helpless in the kitchen. 

James – It’s okay, we all have our goods and bads, our ups and downs. 

Kate – Yeah, but it seems that you don’t have anything bad. Oh, oh I got it! I’ve got a brilliant idea. 

James – What? 

Kate – English for Cooking! 

James – Yeah! And you can learn cooking while you’re learning English. That would certainly attract a lot of modern people. 

Kate – We can partner with a cooking school to do this. We need to put a proposal together to give to David. Oh but what should we say? 

James – I got this. We can use positive emphatic language such as ‘exceptional’, ‘fantastic’, and ‘quality’. The proposal’s gotta be brief, clear, and talk about how it would bring benefits. We can use words like ‘profit’, ‘efficiency’, and ‘value for money’. 

Kate – I see! I’d better write this down. Keep going! 

James – We can also say how we could target modern people. They’ll be begging for our course. Oh, this is so exciting. 

Kate – I’m writing it all down. This exceptional product has fantastic potential and see here…… And by… By making the production more efficient, our clients will get value for money and our profits will increase. Done. 

James – Good job, Kate. We can pitch this to David first thing tomorrow. 

Kate – Thanks to you for your inspiration. 

Narrator – So, the proposal has been completed, James and Kate now need to convince David that they are developing a promising product. Let’s review the emphatic language they use: Exceptional Fantastic High-Quality Profitable Efficient Value for money Now it’s time for you to practice before finding out how things go. Let´s keep things cooking!


Recording 11.3 - Conversation - Customer Survey

Lesson 11 New Product

Recording 11.3 - Conversation - Customer Survey

Narrator – Today at E+ Education, Kate and James will pitch their proposal to David, and hopefully, they’ll get through to him. But before that, let’s see if there’s anything else standing in their way. 

Kate – Morning, James. Are you ready for today? 

James – Well, yeah, I’m still thinking… 

Kate – Don’t tell me you’re having second thoughts and suddenly you think this product can´t work. 

James – No, Kate. Before proposing to David, we need to do some research, don’t we? 

Kate – What kind of research? 

James – A customer survey would be best. 

Kate – Oh, Ted. I’ve never done that. What should I do? How can I ask questions in a survey? 

Narrator – You can ask questions like: “Could I ask for your opinion on a new product we’re hoping to develop?” “What English course do you currently take?” “What courses would you like us to make (or create) for you in the near future?” “How do you feel about the idea of combining English and practical skill learning?” “Are there any particular types of English you’d like to learn?” 

Kate – Oh, okay, oh, so it’s a kind of questionnaire. 

Narrator – That’s true. You got it. Nothing too difficult. 

Kate – Thanks, Ted. You’re always on the ball. I think I’m gonna need some help cold-calling. Would you mind, James? 

James – No problem. We could also ask Mary to help. 

Kate – Oh, that’s right. Mary always likes to give a helping hand. 

James – Hey, Mary. Would you help us call some people? We need to find out how exactly they want to learn English. 

Mary – Ooh, nice. I love talking on the phone. 

James – Lucky us to have you on board. 

Kate – Thanks, Mary. Let’s start, everybody! Here I have a list that I’ve made for you. Could you make calls to these people here? 

Mary – Of course. I’ll get on it right away. 

Kate – Great, and James, could you help me by cold-calling some new sources? 

James – Sure. I’ve got my list here. 

Kate – I suppose I’d better make some calls too. 

Narrator – That’s the spirit, E+ Education! But how would these pieces of information help them pitch their proposal to David? Let’s find out in the next lesson. But before that, it’s time for practice. Enjoy (and don´t forget to keep questioning, especially in English)! 


Recording 11.4 - Conversation - Pitching Your Proposal

Lesson 11 New Product

Recording 11.4 - Conversation - Pitching Your Proposal

Narrator – Hello again from E+ Education where everybody is working hard on a new product. They´ve completed a customer survey, and now it’s up to Kate to pitch her ideas to David. 

James – So Kate, what does David think about your proposal? 

Kate – Our proposal, James. Give yourself credit actually, you deserve most of the credit. But I haven’t talked to him yet. 

Mary – Why not? Are you chickening out again? You’ve got to get up the nerve! 

Kate – No, Mary. I just… I’m still brainstorming some ideas. 

James – Right, Kate. You should be well-prepared, but don’t take too long. There’s no time like the present. Did you ever hear that before? 

Kate – You’re absolutely right. I should talk to him as soon as possible. 

Narrator – True, Katie. You go, girl. Remember to sound excited, but also be clear. These words, again, may help you: Exceptional! Fantastic! High-Quality Profitable Efficient Value for money 

Kate – Okay, I’ve got all of them down here in my notes. I’m also adding some facts and figures from our research. 

Narrator – Go talk to him whenever you’re ready. Don’t be shy! 

Kate – I’m going in now. 

Narrator – Remember, Katie, he doesn’t have much time, so you should be quick and concise. Tell him about your product, and the market for it. Then talk about how you hope it’ll be good for business. Give him the evidence you have from your customer survey to prove that it´ll be successful and convince him it´ll make good money. 

Kate – Okay, I got it. Hello David. James and I have come up with an exciting new idea. Learning to Cook in English. 

David – Good! Good! Well?! 

Kate – This fantastic product will be a great addition to modern learning. 

David – Interesting, but how will it be a profitable addition to our company? 

Kate – We can use the format and resources of the last courses to increase efficiency, and at the same time, maintain quality. 

David – What about profits? 

Kate – By my calculation, we can increase profits by 20%. That’s based on selling the same quantity we now sell of X3 Coaching. 

David – That’s an impressive figure! How did you come up with that? 

Kate – We’ve conducted a survey and already found 15 new organizations which would buy this, and 36 existing customers who have shown interest in it too…… And I’m completely confident that this new product will be a huge success. 

David – Yeah, it does sound promising. I’ll take a closer look at what you wrote down here. 

Kate – Thanks, David. 

Narrator – Well done, Katie. David seems to be opening his door to this product. E+ Education is on its way up fast. But will there be any roadblocks soon? Let’s find out in the next unit. Before that, it’s time for you to practice. Enjoy!
