Day 47 - Rapid Response - Booking flight

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking to Recording 8.6 from 3 to 5 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 8.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Lesson 08 - A Business Trip

Recording 8.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Topic 1: A New Project

Kate – Hello, David. I’m sorry to hear about the hotel.

David – No problem, Kate. Luckily your complaints guaranteed me a pretty good stay, at least for the rest of my trip there.

Kate – That’s thanks to Mary. She’s very good at complaining.

Mary – It’s a skill, Kate, after hundreds of unpleasant haircuts.

David – Now, Kate and James, from this trip, I want to tell you that a good product can deliver benefits. But to keep those benefits coming, you need good customer care.

James – That’s true, David. You care a lot for our customers!

David – I did get that mess tidied up. But I also got a piece of tidings that our rival, Better English, is also looking at Korea. Now I want to hear about what you guys plan to do, and it’d better be good.

Kate – Oh, well, as you know, we’ve done some cold-calling and already got a client from Korea – KoreTech Global. They are a big international organization with more than 2,000 employees.

David – Sounds promising.

James – Yes, David. That’s due to Kate’s good work. We also got the marketing materials finished, along with other things.

David – Very impressive. I want to hear about all the details.

Kate – First, we think our prices must be more competitive.

David – And that means…?

Kate – Well we need prices that can stand out in the market. We need to corner the market so that everyone knows we are absolutely the best choice for English learning. We need to beat the others’ hands down in terms of price!

David – Exactly! I´m with you, but we can also consider improving our service, not just cutting our prices too much.

Kate – And, we need to build good relationships with our client, as you just said.

David – Sure, absolutely right! Good relationships will win the day.

James – And our marketing strategies must highlight the quality we have here in our X3 Coaching program at E+ Education.

David – Really well done, guys. Now I want you to contact them as soon as you can, and make them sign on the dotted line!

 

Topic 2: Negotiation

Kate – James, I’m gonna call KoreTech now. Hopefully Better English hasn’t reached them yet.

James – But if they have, do you know what to say?

Kate – Well, that’s what I’m worried about. They usually offer a very low, rock-bottom price which easily attracts people.

James – In that case, maybe we should work out some new figures.

Kate – That’s right. But how? We have to make a profit too.

James – You know what? We can make less profit. We need to focus more on market share, and less on profit.

Kate – Yeah, it´s better than nothing. Well, what should I say?

James – You could say that you are sorry to hear that they´re not happy with our prices. That we, E+ Education, pride ourselves on quality and good value for money. You can also flatter them and say they are a valued client, and of course, their business is very important to us.

Kate – And what shall I say about the price?

James – We could give them an extra 10% discount with a bigger order.

Kate – That’s a good idea, James. I’d better call them now. James, you were right. Better English has already offered them a very low price.

James – So, did you talk them into using our courses?

Kate – I think they haven’t made up their minds yet. It’s not easy to convince them 100%.

James – We have to think of what more we can do for them.

David – You guys have done a good job so far. But this deal may open the door to a much bigger market, so my thinking is we must get it at all costs.

Kate – What exactly should we do now?

David – Fly there, and get it done, once and for all. I want you both to get ready to go to Korea and do the deal.

 

Topic 3: Booking A Flight

James – Really? What… Me and Kate… Going to Korea?

David – Yes, it’s Kate’s contract, but we need someone who knows the product like the back of his hand.

James – Right, it’s my baby, so that´s gotta be me.

Kate – Oh, I see. Now I have to book our flights online.

James – You’d better ask Mary about that. I´ll go prepare all the necessary documents.

Kate – Hi, Mary. Have you got a second? David says I’m going to Korea with James, so could you help me reserve the flight tickets?

Mary – Really, you two are going on holiday together?!

Kate – No, Mary. We´re meeting with KoreTech Global to seal the deal. But I don’t know how to book a flight online. I´ve never had this experience!

Mary – Come here and we´ll do it together. Okay, what´s the departure date?

Kate – Tomorrow, and we can go there and come back the same day.

Mary – Business or Economy class? I think Economy is more appropriate.

Kate – It’s cheaper, isn’t it? What about this I see on the site, Flexible or Fixed?

Mary – A flexible ticket allows you to change your flight, but a fixed one doesn’t, but the latter is cheaper, so we’ll choose that one. You aren’t going to change your flight at the last minute, are you?

Kate – No, I don´t think so.

Mary – Now, give me your full names, and we can select your seats. Do you want a seat by the aisle or the window?

Kate – An aisle seat please, Mary.

Mary – Okay. Good. Do you have a frequent flyer number?

Kate – I don’t because I don´t fly frequently! In truth, I hardly fly at all.

Mary – That’s okay. Okay, now, we need to pay. I´ll use the company account, that’s for sure. It’s done. You’re all set to fly.

Kate – Thank you, Mary. You do know a lot about this stuff. You’re a whiz!

Mary – Ha, everyone has their good sides. There, your e-tickets are printed and ready. And don’t forget your passports.

 

Topic 4: Seal the Deal

Kate – Annyeonghaseyo, Mr. Lee. I’m Kate from E+ Education. This is my colleague James. We’re really grateful that you could spare the time to meet us.

Mr. Lee – You’re welcome. How was your flight?

James – It was good. Korean Airlines here has exceptionally good service.

Mr. Lee – That’s right. They´re one of our best companies.

Kate – I understand that Better English has offered you good prices for their programs, but as I said on the phone, we can make our deal even better for you.

Mr. Lee – Yes, but how can you help us exactly? How can you sweeten the deal?

Kate – Easy. You simply have to buy more!

James – What she means is our X3 Coaching program employs modern English teaching principles while providing the service online. Our 24/7 one-on-one coaching system will enable your staff to learn English anywhere and anytime they want.

Kate – James, that’s well said!

James – Remember, it’s my baby!

Kate – So, we really feel that we have a product that you’ll be proud to display. And to get this fantastic price, you just need to place an order of 10,000 accounts.

James – Each account comes together with a high-quality DVD and license. I know you won’t be disappointed.

Mr. Lee – It sounds good. I think we can go with it, but we´ll need to hear all the details before committing.

Kate – So, I would be very grateful if you could just sign the contract. We’ll send you the complete account information after only a week, and the DVDs will arrive after just two weeks.

Mr. Lee – Good. We’re ready to sign on the dotted line. There you go.

Kate – Thank you. It was a pleasure doing business with you.

Mr. Lee – Perfect!

✔️ Practice listening and answering to Recording 8.9 from 5 to 7 times until you feel confident in giving full-sentence responses.

Part A: Building Comprehension

Who is being sent on a business trip abroad?

Kate and James.

Kate and James are the ones who are being sent on a business trip abroad.

Kate and James are being sent.

How does James know about the product?

Like the back of his hand.

He has very good and detailed knowledge of this product.

James knows it like the back of his hand.

Does Kate think James have children?

Yes, she does.

Kate does think he is a dad.

Kate thinks James has children.

A flexible ticket vs. a fixed ticket, which is cheaper?

The fixed one.

The fixed ticket is much cheaper than the flexible one.

The cheaper ticket is the fixed one.

Where do you think Kate prefers to sit on the airplane?

In an aisle seat.

She wants a seat by the aisle.

She prefers an aisle seat to a window one.

Does Kate have a frequent flyer number?

No, she doesn’t.

Kate doesn’t fly frequently so she doesn´t have this number.

Actually, she hardly flies at all.

Who are they meeting with to seal the deal?

KoreTech Global.

They’re meeting with KoreTech Global to seal the deal.

They have a meeting with KoreTech Global to reach the contract.

Where are Kate and James going to?

Korea.

They are going to Korea to meet their client.

Korea is where they are going to go.

Who can help Kate book their flights online?

Mary can.

It’s Mary, she has much experience with this stuff.

Mary can help Kate reserve the flight tickets.

Are James and Kate going on holiday together?

No, they aren’t.

They are going to Korea for business purposes.

They’re meeting with KoreTech Global to close the deal.

Part B: In Other Words

You know all the streets around here very well.

You know all the streets around here like the back of your hand.

It´ll be hard to make changes to this special program he created.

It´ll be hard to make changes to his brainchild.

She really has no clue about how to get home from here.

She really has no idea about how to get home from here.

When will you be on vacation this year?

When will you be on holiday this year?

My boss wants us to finish the negotiation with this client by tonight!

My boss wants us to seal the deal with this client by tonight!

Why do you always wait until so late to do your English homework?

Why do you always wait to do your English homework at the last minute?

My mother speaks almost no English, even after taking classes for one year!

My mother speaks hardly a word of English at all, even after taking classes for one year!

Part C: Language to Go – How to Book a Flight

What are your departure and return dates?

I´d like to depart on Friday, July 21, in the morning, and return one week later on July 28, in the evening. Could you tell me if that´s possible please?

Do you prefer to fly business or economy class?

Of course, I prefer to fly business class, but right now I don´t have a lot of money. Just kidding. I´d like to fly economy class this time please.

Do you want a fixed or flexible ticket?

I´m not planning to change my flight at the last minute, so I´ll go with a fixed ticket, if you please.

What kind of seat would you like then: A window, middle, or aisle seat? I also have an exit row seat available, but you´ll have to agree to help if there´s an emergency on the plane.

I´ll probably be sleeping so I´d prefer to have a window seat. And if possible, could you put me in the front of the plane?

All right. I´ll see what I can do for you. Do you have a frequent flyer number?

What frequent flyer program do you use? I think I have MileagePlus, Smiles, and Amigo, if not more!

In fact, we use Lotusmiles. If you´re not a member, you can join at the airport. Is there anything else I can help you with?

No, that´s great for now. Maybe you can tell me where the best Duty-Free Shops are at the airport… Just joking again. Thanks for all your help.
