Recording 7.1 - Conversation - Learn to Compliment

Lesson 07 - Double Trouble

Recording 7.1 - Conversation - Learn to Compliment

Narrator – Here we are again, back at E+ Education where Kate has given a successful presentation. But at the end, an angry client called David and threatened to cancel the order because of James’s mistake. Let’s see how they are going to deal with this sticky situation. 

David – Good morning. 

Kate & James – Good morning, David. 

David – Kate and James, I´d like to have a quick word with both of you in the meeting room now, please. 

Kate – Oh! Oh my God. 

Narrator – You don’t have to worry, Kate, I think it’s James who’s got to be prepared. 

Kate – I still have a bad feeling about all this. 

Narrator – Well, I think he’ll say something to you like:
“Well done.”
“You handled it well.”
“Great job.”
“I’m really impressed with your work.” 

Kate – And what about James? 

Narrator – Well, I guess you just have to wait and see. 

David – Well, James and Kate, yesterday was a great day until I received a call from Mr. Takashi Matsuo. But let’s focus on the good first. Kate! 

Kate – Yes? 

David – Well done with the presentation. I think I´ll take it a step further and allot a bigger budget for this project. 

Kate – Oh, Oh that’s exciting! 

David – You were in a difficult spot in the presentation when the electricity died, without your slides and notes, but you know, you handled it very well. Great job. You remained calm, friendly, and professional. I was really impressed. 

Kate – Thank you, David. 

David – X3 Coaching is the result of very hard work, led by James. So, James, I´d like you to work with Kate on the project this time. 

James – Sure, David, I can go along with that. 

David – We´re making this one of our primary products. I want it not only to compete with the best quality, but also attractive to the market as well. Can you two work on this plan? 

Kate – No problem, David. 

James – Yes, we can handle it. 

David – Good. Good! Good!
Now, James, I think I should have a private word with you.
Kate, on your way out, could you please close the door behind you? 

Kate – Yes, David.
Oh Ted, will James be all right there by himself? 

Narrator – We´re not there to know, Katie. This sounds pretty serious! Check out what David is going to say to James in the next topic. Now it’s time for you to practice. Enjoy your time to talk, and be sure to talk it up! 


Recording 7.2 - Conversation - Give and Receive Feedback

Lesson 07 - Double Trouble

Recording 7.2 - Conversation - Give and Receive Feedback

Narrator – Here we are again at E+ Education where James is about to hear some stern words from David. He’s just messed up an important order. Let’s see how things go. 

David – James, I´m a little concerned about some things related to you recently. Is everything okay? 

James – Yes, everything is good, David. 

David – I know that this course is a big deal, and you’ve put a lot of effort into it. But that’s not your only task. Do you follow me? 

James – Sorry, David. 

David - Perhaps you should think about working on your time management, and how you distribute your energy to each of the jobs you need to do. 

James - Sure, you´re probably right. 

David - I think we´ve been having too much trouble with Mr. Matsuo and his organization recently. 

James – I’m so sorry, David. The computer crisis caused us a lot of trouble, as you know. But it was my fault not to double check before sending out Mr. Matsuo’s order. 

David – As long as you take responsibility for your actions, James, I can bear with you. 

James – Sure. I’m going to contact him right away, explain the situation, and discuss some kind of compensation for him. 

David – Good. Mistakes happen, but we need to learn from them, and how to deal better with the situation at hand. 

James – I´ll try my best. I promise to give it my best shot. 

David – Good to hear. Now, since the messed-up order this time is for another branch, which is way out of town, and we caused Mr. Matsuo a lot of inconvenience, I should go there myself straight away and deal with the situation in person. 

James – I’m really sorry, David. 

David – No, no, no, no, no worries. I need to go on this business trip. And let’s hope things turn out fine by the time I get back. Now back to work. 

Kate – James, oh, is there any problem? 

James – Everything’s good, Kate. 

Kate – I thought David was going to have a serious talk to you about the Matsuo order. I thought he was going to set the record straight! 

James – Well, he did. And he also said I should work on my time management skill. 

Kate – What does that mean? 

James – He’s right. I haven’t done a good job managing my time recently. He’s going to meet with Mr. Matsuo to personally deal with this problem, Kate. 

Kate – Oh, okay. Wait! Mary isn´t here. I wonder who’s gonna help him get ready for his trip. 

Narrator – That maybe you, Katie.
So, David is going out of town, and there´s a lot to prepare for the boss. Check out what Kate can do in the next lesson.
But first, let’s review how David gives negative feedback to James:
“I´m a little concerned about…”
“Perhaps you should think about working on…
“Mistakes happen, but…”
Now it’s time for you to practice. Enjoy (because everybody enjoys giving feedback)!


Recording 7.3 - Conversation - Hotel Booking

Lesson 07 - Double Trouble

Recording 7.3 - Conversation - Hotel Booking

Narrator – We´ve seen that a messed-up order made David travel. But Mary has called in sick. Who’s going to help him prepare now in a pinch? 

David – Kate, since Mary called in sick today, would you mind helping me get ready for my trip? 

Kate – Of course. Poor old Mary, I hope her illness isn’t too serious. 

David – Don’t worry, she says it’s just a simple health checkup or something. Now, this trip is a big deal since Mr. Matsuo and his organization is one of our main clients. Kate, could you help me reserve a hotel room? 

Kate – Yes, I suppose I could… 

Narrator – Katie, don’t worry, booking a hotel room is simple: It’s a piece of cake. 

Kate – Are you talking about eating some dessert? 

Narrator – It means there’s nothing difficult about it. Just call a hotel and say:
“I would like to make a room reservation at your hotel.”
“Could you tell me the availability (on these dates) and prices for a room please.”
“Does the price include breakfast?”
It’s suggested that you ask them about business facilities like: “Are there any business facilities such as WiFi?”
David needs to be as relaxed as possible before meeting his customer, so you can ask them if there’s a good room facing the sea.

Kate – Hm, Thank you, Ted. But how do I find these hotels? 

Narrator – Try searching on the internet. Look for a five-star hotel to start with (as David likes the best). 

Kate – Okay, it´s my pleasure to help the boss go on the road.
Okay, and… Here it is…The Luxury Hotel. 

Receptionist – Hello. This is the Luxury Hotel; how may I help you? 

Kate – Hello! I´d like to make a reservation at your hotel. Could you tell me the availability and prices for a room, please? 

Receptionist – When do you need the room? 

Kate – I need the room for three nights, starting from tonight. 

Receptionist – We have a wide range of services. The best single room is $350 per night. 

Kate – Does the price include breakfast? 

Receptionist – Yes, the breakfast is a full buffet with various food options and juice, as well as lots of bread, meat, and cheese. 

Kate – Oh, and good coffee? 

Receptionist – Yes, of course, we even have espresso and other flavored coffees! 

Kate – Oh, what about business facilities? Do you have things like internet access, pens, paper, maybe a projector? 

Receptionist – Yes. We’ve got all these things. All you have to do is ask at the front desk for assistance. 

Kate – Wonderful! Great! I´m wondering if you happen to have a room with an ocean view? 

Receptionist – Yes, we do. There´s a gorgeous view of the sea from all the rooms on the second and third floors. 

Kate – Excellent. I’d like to go ahead and make a reservation for my boss please. 

Receptionist – Yes. Now, what name will the reservation be listed under? 

Kate – Mr. David Johnson.

Receptionist – Could you spell the last name for me, please? 

Kate – Sure. J-O-H-N-S-O-N. 

Receptionist – And is there a phone number where Mr. Johnson can be reached…? 

Narrator – The room is booked, but is Task Number 1 completed here? Let´s find out in the next topic. But before that, spend some quality time practicing this one. Enjoy! 


Recording 7.4 - Conversation - Complaining

Lesson 07 - Double Trouble

Recording 7.4 - Conversation - Complaining

Narrator – Welcome back to E+ Education where David is on a business trip and everyone else is totally focused on their jobs. Everyone is in the zone! 

Mary – I can’t choose which hairstyle I’m going to change to. I need to consult with a hair professional rather than spending all day looking at the stupid computer screen. 

James – That’s not good, Mary. 

Mary – You know what I mean. 

James – Well, rather than looking at the screen, you’d better finish this paperwork before David gets back to the office. 

Mary – James, give me a break! Anyway, I’m going to the hairdresser now. 

Kate – Oh. Mary’s just gone out. I’ll get it.
Hello, E+ Education. This is Kate speaking…
David, did you sleep well?
…Yes … yes… Really?
… Oh, well … Okay … yes … goodbye… 

James – Is everything okay Kate? You look troubled by something. 

Kate – Actually, David isn’t happy about his hotel room. The room is too noisy because there’s a live show nearby every night all night, and he couldn’t sleep well. The AC is also too loud and blowing cold air directly on the bed. And, here´s the disaster of all disasters: There was no coffeemaker in the room! 

James – Oops! Nothing could be worse than that, especially for David! We all know how crazy he is about good, strong coffee!! 

Kate – Yes. And he said the clerk didn’t even greet him when he entered the hotel. 

James – You’ve got to be kidding me. David would never accept such an attitude. 

Narrator – Oh, Kate. Now you need to make some complaints on his behalf. What are you going to say? 

Kate – I don’t know. Could you show me how to make complaints? I´m afraid I´ve never done it in a business context. 

Narrator – Keep your cool and politely tell the hotel the reasons that make you or your boss unhappy. Try not to be angry, sarcastic, or threatening in any way. Say:
“I have a simple complaint to make.”
“Excuse me, but there is a problem with …”
“I’m afraid I’ve got a complaint about…”
“I’m sorry to say this but…”
Then you can say:
“The standard service is not good enough because…”
“The room I reserved didn´t meet my expectations.”

Kate – Okay. Well, here it goes. 

Receptionist – Hello. Luxury Hotel. 

Kate – Erm… Hello. Hello I want to…I´d like to… I want to complain. 

Receptionist – Complain? About what? 

Kate – Erm…I mean…I’m sorry to say this but…I´m really disappointed with your service. Especially the room I booked for my boss. Room 269 to be exact. 

Receptionist – Why’s that then? 

Kate – The room is not good enough. It… it simply didn’t meet his expectations…Excuse me? 

Mary – Kate, give me the phone. 

Kate – Thank God, Mary, you’re back. 

Mary – I’ll deal with this…Hello? 

Receptionist – Yes. Something about a complaint? 

Mary – This is the office assistant, I mean manager here. I have a complaint to make. The room you gave my boss is really not good enough. No greetings at the front desk, a noisy room with a noisy live band and a noisy AC, and especially…no coffeemaker for my boss who loves coffee more than anything??? I would like you to resolve this matter as quickly as possible, or I´ll have to cancel our reservation. 

Receptionist – Sadly, the coffeemaker has been out of order recently. 

Mary – Okay. I’d like to speak to your manager please. We need to make absolutely sure that our boss is getting his money´s worth at your hotel. 

Narrator – That’s how Mary deals with complaints and she doesn´t hold back! Hopefully David can have better service now. Before turning the page to see what happens next, let’s practice this lesson. Cheers (and good complaining)!
