Day 57 - Rapid Response - Standing your ground

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 10.5 from 1 to 3 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 10.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Lesson 10 Tricky Colleague

Recording 10.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Standing Your Ground

David – Hello, Kate. This is Rose, who I’ve mentioned to you.

Kate – Hi, I’m Kate. I´m a Sales Executive here at E+ Education.

Rose – I know. New in the industry, freshly graduated, kind of a spring chicken, aren’t you?

David – Well, not really. Anyway, I think you two are going to learn to get along. Kate, Rose will be your co-worker on the new contract now.

Rose – What do you mean by co-worker? I thought I’d be in charge?!

David – Listen, Rose. You’ve just come here, and the contract is the result of Kate’s hard work. I want you two to work together. Do you understand me clearly?

Rose – Yes, sir. You’re the boss man!

David – Well, have a good day.

Rose – I’ll have a good day if I go to the bar now.

Kate – Rose, I understand you have lots of experience, so maybe we could leverage your skills for this task?

Rose – Okay, since you recognize that. We need those plastic discs as cheap as possible, it’s as simple as that.

Kate – Rose, I really like your enthusiasm, but can we discuss this a bit more so we can agree on a plan of action?

Rose – Of course, let me handle it. I’ll see where to get the cheapest discs.

Kate – Well, I think that our client is expecting the best quality as we promised in the contract. So, if you could just respect my decision this time, we can move on.

Rose – Okay, but only this time, Katie Cat.

 

Topic 2: Giving A Coworker Feedback

Kate – You sent out what? The cheapest ones?!

Rose – Chilly chill, Katie Cat, they’re just plastic discs.

Kate – They should be high-quality DVD’s, and I’d appreciate it if you stop calling me Cat, now.

Rose – Whatever, it’s fine. I’m out for an early lunch.

James – It’s only 9 AM. Kate, Mr. Lee wanted the best and most durable ones.

Kate – I think I’ll to have to talk to David about this. David, may I speak to you, please?

David – Ooh, any time, Kate. What can I do for you? What´s on your mind?

Kate – You know, it’s Rose. I’m finding it very difficult to work with her.

David – Oh, really. Why is that?

Kate – I’m a little bit concerned that she’s made a mistake and it might adversely affect our business.

David – What was it? Could you tell me please?

Kate – She’s sent cheap DVD’s for the first part of our new contract in Korea while the client asked for top-quality products.

David – Oh. That’s unacceptable! I need to get to the bottom of this.

Kate – And personally speaking, I don’t think she’s pulling her weight. She’s just gone out for lunch and it’s still early, we only just had our morning coffee break!

David – Holy smokes, it’s only 9 AM! Look Kate, you work with her most of the time, and you certainly know her best of anyone here. If things are that bad, you have my permission to talk to her and find a way to discipline her.

 

Topic 3: Customer Care

Kate – Hello, I’m Kate from E+ Education. How are you today?

Mr. Lee – Now, here you are. What can you say to us?

Kate – By now, I hope you’ve received our X3 Coaching DVD´s and that you´re happy with the order.

Mr. Lee – Very happy? You hope we’re happy with your service?

Kate – Oh, we do hope you’re pleased with our service and that you choose to use us again.

Mr. Lee – We’re certainly not. The first DVD we tried broke inside the computer. Now the computer has stopped working too. We ordered the best products. Didn’t we say it clearly enough? This is unacceptable!

Kate – Oh my God. We are so sorry for any inconvenience, sir. I’ll figure this out immediately.

 

Topic 4: Discipline and Warning

James – Hi, guys. Kate, I managed to make the new order for the top-quality DVD’s.

Kate – That’s great!

James – Yes, we have to send them to Mr. Lee ASAP.

Kate – Thank you, James. And about dinner, yes, let’s go out some time soon.

Rose – Hey, Katie Cat! What is that funny face all about?

Kate – I need to talk to you about something that’s bothering me.

Rose – Okay, talk. Fire away, but I’ve only got a minute to spare!

Kate – Rose, I wanted to let you know that you’ve made some pretty serious mistakes. You’ve really upset our new client.

Rose – Ooh! Scary! I hope the client survived!

Kate – These mistakes simply cannot happen again. If they do, I’ll have to take matters higher up, and it could result in some disciplinary action.

Rose – All right, all right, Katie Cat. You’ve scared me enough for now. Halloween’s come early!

Step 3: Practice rapid response

✔️ Without the transcript, listen to Recording 10.7 from 1 to 2 times. Each time, try to give short answers as fast as you can. 

✔️ With the transcript, practice responding as instructed in the transcript from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Practice listening and answering to Recording 10.7 from 3 to 5 times until you feel confident in giving full-sentence responses.

Note: You need to achieve the following goals:

✔️ Building Comprehension: You can respond to each question with at least 1 complete answer. 

✔️ In Other Words: You can say complete sentences with similar meanings.

✔️ Language to Go: You can give responsive sentences that correspond to the instruction sentence about the situation.

Recording 10.7 - Rapid Response - Standing Your Ground

Recording 10.7 - Rapid Response - Standing Your Ground

Part A: Building Comprehension

Where is the new staff member waiting for Kate and David?

In the meeting room.

She’s waiting for them in the meeting room.

The new staff member´s been waiting in the meeting room.

What is Rose’s position in the company?

A Sales Executive.

She takes a role as a company Sales Executive, the same as Kate.

Rose Miller is Kate’s colleague and also a Sales Executive in the company.

What does David think Kate and Rose need to learn?

They need to learn to get along.

Kate and Rose need to learn to start a friendly working relationship.

They should learn to have a harmonious relationship.

Which position did Rose think she would have with the new contract?

She thought she’d be in charge.

Rose thought that she´d manage the new contract.

She believed she´d occupy an important position as project manager.

What does David want between Kate and Rose?

He wants them to work together.

They are co-workers which is what David wants.

They will work in a team at the same level.

Has Rose made Kate feel uncomfortable?

Yes, she has.

Kate hasn´t been comfortable because of Rose.

Rose has made Kate feel uneasy.

What advice does the narrator give Kate when she´s in an awkward situation?

“I understand you have lots of experience, so I would like to leverage your skills to help with the task.”

“Together we can do this job really well.”

“Two heads are better than one!”

Does Kate think Rose has lots of experience?

She does. She thinks Rose has lots of experience.

What Kate thinks is Rose has many job skills.

Kate thinks Rose has much knowledge at work.

What does Rose think about their plastic disks?

She thinks they need those plastic disks which are the cheapest ones.

She doesn’t care much about the quality, but the cost of those disks should be as cheap as possible.

Those disks only need to be the cheapest ones.

Does Rose agree with Kate’s decision this time?

Yes, she does.

Rose agrees but she says that only this time she agrees with Kate.

She finally respects Kate’s decision.

Part B: In Other Words

Since the new staff is young and energetic, we can ask him to do everything for us.

Since the new staff is a spring chicken, we can ask him to do everything for us.

Why isn´t it possible for us to work well together?

Why isn´t it possible for us to get along?

To speak English on the phone sometimes seems very strange.

To speak English on the phone sometimes seems awkward.

After a long time, I finally made things better with my old business partner.

After a long time, I finally smoothed things out with my old business partner.

We need to take advantage of our location as we´re right in the city center.

We need to leverage our location as we´re right in the city center.

My answer is yes, no more needs to be said.

My answer is yes, as simple as that.

My boss and I seem to reach an agreement on almost everything in principle.

My boss and I seem to agree on almost everything in principle.

Do you have a well-defined strategy for taking action?

Do you have a well-defined plan of action?

When you have a strong opinion, you must stick to it.

When you have a strong opinion, you must stand your ground.

Part C: Language to Go – How to Defend Your Own Point of View

What would you say to a person who disagrees with you to suggest more discussion?

I understand your point, but can we discuss this a bit more so we can agree on a plan of action?

How would you ask a person to respect you more?

Please respect my decision, and let´s move on.

What could you say to a disagreeable person to make them want to work together?

I realize you may have a different point of view, but together we can do this job very well.

How could you talk to this person to give them credit for their skills?

I understand you have lots of experience, so maybe we can leverage your skills for this job?

What´s an expression we use to mean that two people work better together than only one?

“Two heads are better than one!”

What could you say to emphasize the enthusiasm of the other person even though you disagree?

Even though we don’t always agree, I really like your enthusiasm.
