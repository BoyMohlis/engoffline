Day 88 - Rapid Response - Conference

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 15.5 from 1 to 2 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 15.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Lesson 15 At a Crossroad

Recording 15.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Priority

James – So Kate, just a quick check. How are you doing on the conference prep for tomorrow? Everything’s almost ready now?

Kate – I have a lot on my plate at the moment. We haven’t got our printouts of the flyer yet, so I need to go to the printers to check it. In the meantime, a speaker refused to come at the last minute, so I’m desperately looking for someone else. And the presentation, that hasn’t been finished yet!

James – I can’t believe there´s so much trouble. You just need to get organized and prioritize. Your plate´s obviously big, but you can handle it.

Kate – How do you suggest I do that?

James – Decide what tasks need doing first, and maybe delegate some tasks to others. Mary and I can help, maybe even David in the clutch.

Kate – Thanks, that´s pretty good advice. There, that’s my list finished. Mary, sorry to bother you, but the printouts for tomorrow´s conference aren´t ready yet. I was wondering if you could help me out with this. Please?

Mary – Of course Kate. I’m going to do it for you right away.

James – Hey Kate, what are we going to do about the free-learning account cards? Do you want to put them in the gift bags?

Kate – I’m going to have to put that off until tomorrow, I’m afraid.

James – Oh right. I guess I can help you with the presentation.

Kate – Thank you, James. Finding an alternative speaker is the most important thing we have to take care of, so I’m going to concentrate on that first.

James – That’s absolutely right. You can ask David if you need help, he has a lot of contacts in this industry.

Kate – Thanks. I’ll go check with him right now. We need to take advantage of all his contacts. Hello David, so sorry to bother you. I’m a bit worried…I just have way too much to do and need some help.

David – Yes, of course. Come right in. I don’t want you feeling stressed.

Kate – Unfortunately for us, our speaker canceled at the last minute. Could you recommend someone to fill in that I can contact?

David – Sure. You can leave it to me.

Kate – Thank you, David. I knew I could count on you.

 

Topic 2: The Conference Arrives!

David – Okay, everyone, you ready? I’ll make an introduction now.

James – I’ll help you change the slides on the computer, David. I’m good with technology.

David – Okay, thanks, James. Here goes. Excuse me everyone, could you all gather round? Our conference is about to start. Thank you. Thank you, ladies and gentlemen, thank you for coming to our presentation. We have an exciting new course to introduce you to here.

Kate – Everyone, good morning! This course is designed with the highest-quality ideas and learning materials. I’m convinced it’s something your business really needs, and will obviously benefit from.

Client – Yeah, but why would I want to buy it?

Kate – This course is perfectly suited for your school. It will clearly show your open and flexible approach to modern education.

David – And, it is a very interesting course. No doubt about it!

Kate – Yes. We’re taking orders today and… If you place an order for more than 100 accounts, we can offer you a generous discount. Plus, all our DVD’s come with a fast, money-back guarantee.

Client – I like it! I think I´ll order as soon as I get back to my office.

 

Topic 3: Resignation

David – Good work guys. Really good work! Actually, I have something important to say.

Kate – What is it?

David – E+ Education is having a strategic review. And they’ve been looking at how the business is operating and how it should operate in the future. And as part of this review, I think that I want to step aside.

Kate – Oh no. I don’t understand, David. Why?

David – I think the company needs new direction and more dynamic leadership at this juncture.

James – But David, you’ve been and still are a great boss!

David – Thanks for that, James. I guess it’s the economic climate, but we’re not making enough money, so cutting staff is inevitable. And, well, I’ve decided to accept an offer abroad. As a result, Kate, would you like a promotion?

Kate – A promotion?!

David – Yes. You´ve been an outstanding member of the team since you joined us, so how would you like to be the new general manager of E+ Education?

 

Topic 4: Promotion

Kate – Wow! Thank you. But why me? Can I even do the job? I’m not as experienced as James. He´s been around much longer than me. I´m still kind of a greenhorn.

Narrator – I think we´ve seen over the past year that you definitely have more actual working experience than James. Go on Kate, you can do it. Step up!

David – So Kate, what do you say? Will you replace me as the new general manager of E+ Education? Come on, Kate. This promotion recognizes your achievements at the company. It’s the opportunity of a lifetime.

Kate – David, this news has come as a bit of a shock to me, and it´s all very sudden. Thank you for your kind words, but please could I have some time to consider your offer?

David – Okay Kate.

Kate – I’ll answer you soon. Thanks for understanding.

James – You can do it, Kate. C´mon! We´re behind you all the way!

Kate – You’ve always encouraged me, James. You know what, you should be offered this position, not me.

James – No, Kate, I love my job, just developing and building what’s not there in the first place. I´m happy to work more behind the scenes.

Kate – You’ve developed me, James!

James – You’re also my baby! Well, I hope you know what I meant by that…

Kate – What are you talking about?

James – Kate, I really like you. You´re smart and beautiful and charming. You´re all a man could hope for in a girlfriend.

Kate – What?!

James – I’m asking you to be my girlfriend. I wanna be with you all the time, inside and outside the office!

Kate – Look, James. I like you too but…Wow, I think I really need some time away, before answering both of these tough questions.

Step 3: Practice rapid response

✔️ Without the transcript, listen to Recording 15.8 from 1 to 2 times. Each time, try to give short answers as fast as you can.

✔️ Use the transcript to get sample answers. Practice responding as instructed in the transcript from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Practice listening and answering to Recording 15.8 from 3 to 5 times until you feel confident in giving full-sentence responses.

Recording 15.8 - Rapid Response - The Conference Arrives

Lesson 15 At a Crossroad

Recording 15.8 - Rapid Response - The Conference Arrives

Recording 15.8 - Rapid Response - The Conference Arrives

Part A: Building Comprehension

Who will make an introduction now?

David will.

David will make an introduction now.

David is the person who will make the introduction.

What will James do to help David?

He’ll change the slides on the computer.

James will help David changes the slides.

He’ll do the technical things for David.

What is James good with?

Technology.

James is good with technology.

Technology because he understands how it works.

Is Kate nervous about the presentation?

Yes, she is.

Kate’s a bit nervous.

Kate feels a little bit nervous about her presentation.

What advice does the narrator give Kate to persuade her audience?

“We believe this high-quality course is something you and your business really needs.”

“Our new course has been designed with the highest-quality ideas and materials.”

“This course is perfectly suited for your school and your learners.”

Is this course suited for schools?

Yes, it´s perfectly suited for schools.

This course goes well with school education.

This course and modern education is the best combination.

Is the course interesting?

Yes, it is.

David says it’s a very interesting course.

David said: “No doubt about it!”. The course is awesome.

What are the advantages of their services and products?

A great discount, fast delivery, and a money-back guarantee.

All their DVDs come with a fast, money-back guarantee. And they will offer a big discount if their client orders more than 100 accounts.

They have great discount program, and all their DVDs will be delivered fast with a guarantee.

What happens if their client places an order for more than 100 accounts?

Their client will receive a generous discount.

A great discount is offered.

They´ll give their client a generous discount.

Does the client like this course?

Yes, he does.

He likes it and decides to order this course after he gets back to his office.

The client likes it.

Part B: In Other Words

He’s really capable of fixing any computer problem.

He’s really good with computers and can fix any problem.

When I was finally ready to present, all the power suddenly went off.

When I was about to present, all the power suddenly went off.

If you don’t feel excited about travelling this weekend, then let’s stay home.

If you don’t feel up to travelling this weekend, then let’s stay home.

We got a guarantee that our money will be refunded if the product breaks down.

We got a money-back guarantee in case the product breaks down.

Are you really persuaded that you can learn English fast as that company promised?

Are you really convinced that you can learn English fast as that company promised?

Since she´s very capable, no one questioned about it when she did an excellent job on that.

Since she´s very capable, there was no doubt about it when she did an excellent job on that.

You should not make plans that depend on something good happening before you know that it has actually happened.

You should not count your chickens before they hatch.

Part C: Language to Go – How to Introduce Your Product

How could you show your audience that your product is something they really need?

We believe this high-quality product is something you and your business really needs.

What could you say to your audience to persuade them of your product´s quality?

Our new product has been designed with the highest-quality ideas and materials.

What could you say to stress that your product is suitable for the audience?

This product is perfectly suited for your business and your customers.

How can you persuade your audience that your product will show them in a good light?

This product will clearly show others your open and flexible approach to modern education.

What could you say to your audience to make them feel they can benefit from your product?

I’m convinced it’s something your business really needs, and will obviously benefit from.

How could you let your audience know about the guarantee that stands behind your product?

All our products come with a fast, money-back guarantee.
