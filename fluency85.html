Recording 15.1 - Conversation - Priority

Lesson 15 At a Crossroad

Recording 15.1 - Conversation - Priority

Narrator – Only a few hours before the conference where E+ Education will launch their new course. Let’s check on how everybody´s doing? 

James – So Kate, just a quick check. How are you doing on the conference prep for tomorrow? Everything’s almost ready now? 

Kate – I have a lot on my plate at the moment. We haven’t got our printouts of the flyer yet, so I need to go to the printers to check on it. In the meantime, a speaker refused to come at the last minute, so I’m desperately looking for someone else. And the presentation, that hasn’t even been finished yet! 

James – I can’t believe there´s so much trouble. You just need to get organized and prioritize. Your plate´s obviously big, but you can handle it. 

Kate – How do you suggest I do that? 

James – Decide what tasks need doing first, and maybe delegate some tasks to others. Mary and I can help, maybe even David in the clutch. 

Narrator – That’s right, Kate. You need to identify the most important items to concentrate on first, and then you could ask other people for help. You can say things like: “It would be a great help if you could take on this other task for me.” You could also say, “I’ll have to put off some tasks until tomorrow”. And if you still feel under pressure, say to your boss, “I really need help at this moment, I´ve got too much to do.” 

Kate – Thanks, that´s pretty good advice. 

Narrator – Writing a list is a great place to start. Jot everything down, prioritize, and good luck! 

Kate – Okay, let see. There! That’s my list finished. Oh, Mary, sorry to bother you, but the printouts for tomorrow´s conference aren´t ready yet. I was wondering… it would be a great help…if you could help me out with this. Please? 

Mary – Of course, Kate. I’m going to do it for you right away. 

James – Hey Kate, what are we going to do about the free-learning account cards? Do you want to put them in the gift bags? 

Kate – I’m going to have to put that off until tomorrow, I’m afraid. 

James – Oh right. I guess I can help you with the presentation. 

Kate – Thank you, James. Finding an alternative speaker is the most important thing we have to take care of, so I’m going to concentrate on that first. 

James – That’s absolutely right. You can ask David if you need help, he has a lot of contacts in the industry. 

Kate – Thanks. I’ll go check with him right now. We need to take advantage of all his contacts. Hello David, so sorry to bother you. I’m a bit worried. I just have way too much to do and need some help. 

David – Yes, of course. Come right in. I don’t want you feeling stressed. 

Kate – Unfortunately for us, our speaker canceled at the last minute. Could you recommend someone to fill in that I can contact? 

David – Well, sure. You can leave it to me. 

Kate – Thank you, David. I knew I could count on you. 

Narrator – Well done, Kate. Everything seems to be in place. Let’s come back and check out E+ Education in the next unit. Now, don´t feel stressed but it’s time for you to practice. Enjoy! 


Recording 15.2 - Conversation - The Conference Arrives!

Lesson 15 At a Crossroad

Recording 15.2 - Conversation - The Conference Arrives!

Narrator – It’s an exciting day at E+ Education when they organize a conference to introduce their new course. Let’s listen in to see how things go. 

David – Okay, everyone, you ready? I’ll make an introduction now. 

James – I’ll help you change the slides on the computer, David. I’m good with technology. 

David – Okay, thanks, James. Here goes. Excuse me everyone, could you all gather around? Our conference is about to start. Thank you. Thank you, ladies and gentlemen, thank you for coming to our presentation. We have an exciting new course to introduce you to here. 

Narrator – Kate, it’s almost time to make your presentation. Do you feel up to it? 

Kate – I know it´s gonna be my turn soon. I’m a little nervous now, hope I don’t forget any important point. 

Narrator – Well, you need to persuade everyone that this is the best learning course for them, and that it’s something they really want and need. You could say: “We believe this high-quality course is something you and your business really need.” “Our new course has been designed with the highest-quality ideas and materials.” “This course is perfectly suited for your school and your learners.” Then, let´s pray that the orders come flying in! 

Kate – Okay, then. Yes, everyone, good morning! This course is designed with the highest-quality ideas and learning materials. I’m convinced it’s something your business really needs, and will obviously benefit from. 

The Customer – Yeah, but why would I want to buy it? 

Kate – This course is perfectly suited for your school. It will clearly show your open and flexible approach to modern education. 

David – And, it is a very interesting course. No doubt about it! 

Kate – Yes. We’re taking orders today and… and… If you place an order for more than 100 accounts, we can offer you a generous discount. Plus, all our DVD´s come with a fast, money-back guarantee. 

The customer – I like it! I think I´ll order as soon as I get back to my office. 

Narrator – Well done Kate. You gave a clear presentation, and sounded enthusiastic about the course. E+ Education is now experiencing a huge success, isn’t it? But don´t count your chickens before they hatch! Let’s find out about it in the next unit. For now, it’s your time to get busy and practice.


Recording 15.3 - Conversation - Resignation

Lesson 15 At a Crossroad

Recording 15.3 - Conversation - Resignation

Narrator – Welcome back to E+ Education where the team has just finished organizing and delivering a successful conference. Are they going to celebrate? Let’s find out now. 

David – Good work guys. Really good work! Actually, I have something important to say. 

Kate – What is it? 

David – E+ Education is having a strategic review. And they’ve been looking at how the business is operating and how it should operate in the future. And as part of this review, I think that I want to step aside. 

Kate – Oh no. I don’t understand, David. Why? 

David – I think the company needs new direction and more dynamic leadership at this juncture. 

James – But David, you’ve been and still are a great boss! 

David – Thanks for that, James. I guess it’s the economic climate, but we’re not making enough money, so cutting staff is inevitable. And, well, I’ve decided to accept an offer abroad. As a result, Kate, would you like a promotion? 

Kate – A promotion?! 

David – Yes. You´ve been an outstanding member of the team since you joined us, so how would you like to be the new general manager of E+ Education? 

Narrator – What a fantastic opportunity, and one that Kate surely can’t refuse-or can she? Find out what her answer is in the next topic. But before that, it’s your turn to practice. Improve your English, and you too will have more opportunities and promotions! Enjoy!!


Recording 15.4 - Conversation - Promotion

Lesson 15 At a Crossroad

Recording 15.4 - Conversation - Promotion

Narrator – Hello again, and welcome back to the “twisted” results of the E+ Education Conference where David has resigned and asked Kate to replace him. What will she say to this offer? Let’s find out. 

Kate – Wow! Thank you. But why me? Can I even do the job? I’m not as experienced as James. He´s been around much longer than me. I´m still kind of a greenhorn. 

Narrator – I think we´ve seen over the past year that you definitely have more actual working experience than James. Go on Kate, you can do it. Step up! 

David – So Kate, what do you say? Will you replace me as the new general manager of E+ Education? Come on, Kate. This promotion recognizes your achievements at the company. It’s the opportunity of a lifetime. 

Kate – I know. It seems like a good deal. Too good to turn down! 

Narrator – Kate, if you need time to think it over, you could say, “Thank you for your kind words” and, “Could I please have more time to consider your offer?” 

Kate – Hmm, David. This news has come as a bit of a shock to me, and it´s all very sudden. Thank you for your kind words, but please could I have some time to consider your offer? 

David – Oh…Okay Kate. 

Kate – I’ll answer you soon. Thanks for understanding. 

James – You can do it, Kate. C´mon! We´re behind you all the way! 

Kate – You’ve always encouraged me, James. You know what, you should be offered this position, not me. 

James – No, Kate, I love my job, just developing and building what’s not there in the first place. I´m happy to work more behind the scenes. 

Kate – You’ve developed me, James! 

James – You’re also my baby! Well, I hope you know what I meant by that… 

Kate – What are you talking about? 

James – Kate, I really like you. You´re smart and beautiful and charming. You´re all a man could hope for in a girlfriend. 

Kate – What?! 

James – I’m asking you to be my girlfriend. I wanna be with you all the time, inside and outside the office! 

Kate – Look, James. I like you too but…Wow, I think I really need some time away, before answering both of these tough questions.

Narrator – Kate has two important questions to answer now. That’s a major decision; actually, two huge decisions. What will her answers be after some time away? I´m dying to find out, and you? Let’s find out together in the final lesson. Now it’s time for you to practice.
