Online Practice - Exercises
Exercise 1

Use the prompts to make a sentence with the appropriate modal verbs.

  1. Some kind of animals / survive / for days / without food or water.
  2. Before electricity, people / use / torches or candles / for light.
  3. Humans / hold their breath / underwater / longer than 5 minutes.
  4. You / study / or you / fail / the final exam.
  5. Clinton / be / the American president / with the highest end-of-office approval rating.
  6. Some giraffes / grow / to / nearly 15 meters.
  7. The tsunami / come / southeastern coast tomorrow.

Exercise 2

Two boys are discussing gifts for their teacher. Complete the conversation, using may or must.

Kyle: What do you think would be a good gift for Miss Marina? I think she (1) ________ like a novel or a science book.

Jack: No, I don’t think she’s a fan of science. She (2)  ________be into romance novels.

Kyle: How about makeup? Every girl (3)  ________have makeup!

Jack: We don’t have enough money to buy makeup. You know that. Most good makeup costs a lot of money.

Kyle: You (4)  ________be right. So, what gift (5)  ________be the best for her? I’m starting to feel confused.

Jack: I think that she would like a Chinese romance novel and it (6) ________fit our budget better, too.

Kyle: I agree with you this time. Let’s go.

Exercise 5

Write 2 reasons for the situations below. Use may (not) , might (not) or could.

No Situation Suggestion
1. He arrived at the bus station but he didn’t see anybody waiting for him. –  midnight / nobody / there.
– late / his friends / go / somewhere else.
2. James comes to the court at 7 o’clock and nobody’s here. – The game / start / 10 o’clock / nobody / here
– James / not be / the right court.
3. The tiger chases that man. After it catches him, it runs away and doesn’t eat him. – The man / have / gun / he / scare / the tiger.
– The man / enter / another animal’s territory / the tiger / leave.
4. The witch forced a couple to choose who might deserve to live. Then, she cursed the girl and kidnapped the boy. – The witch / need / a male servant / she / kidnap / the boy.
– The witch / be jealous /  the girl’s appearance /she / curse / the girl.
5. When he went to the house he didn’t go in. – He / go to / wrong house.
– He / forget / the key.
6. Kira comes to class, but none of the students look at her. – Kira / not be / their teacher.
– Kira / not be / student/this class
7. Johnny is engaged to Sam, and he is marrying Samantha right now. – Sam / be / nickname/Samantha / .
– Johnny and Sam / have / problems / each other / they / ended/ their engagement.

Answers

Ex. 1

  1. Some kinds of animals can survive for days without food or water.
  2. Before electricity, people must have used torches or candles for light.
  3. Humans cannot hold their breath underwater longer than 5 minutes.
  4. You must study or you will fail the final exam.
  5. Clinton might be the American president with the highest end-of-office approval rating.
  6. Some giraffes can grow to nearly 15 meters.
  7. The tsunami may come to the Southeastern coast tomorrow.

  2. B: He must have been drunk.
  3. B: They must have been on their summer vacation.
  4. B: It must have been hard to see.
  5. B: You must have gone to sleep very early.
  6. B: He must have gone to jail.
  7. B: She must have gotten an A.
  8. B: It must have had beautiful scenery.

Ex. 4

  1. must be friendly
  2. must be disappointed
  3. must be bad
  4. must be interesting
  5. must be happy
  6. must be tired
  7. must be funny
  8. must be poor

Ex. 5

1.
• It might have been midnight, so nobody was there.
• He might have been late so his friends left and went somewhere else.
2.
• The game may start at 10 o’clock so nobody’s here.
• James may not be at the right court.
3.
• The man may have a gun so he scares the tiger.
• The man may enter another animal’s territory so the tiger leaves.
4.
• The witch might need a male servant so she kidnapped the boy.
• The witch might be jealous of the girl’s appearance so she cursed the girl.
5.
• He might have gone to the wrong house.
• He might have forgotten the key.
6.
• Kira may not be their teacher.
• Kira may not be a student in this class.
7.
• Sam might be a nickname for Samantha.
• Johnny and Sam might have problems with each other so they ended their engagement.
