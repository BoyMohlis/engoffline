Recording 9.1 - Conversation - Briefing and Reporting

Lesson 09 - Working From Seoul

Recording 9.1 - Conversation - Briefing and Reporting

Narrator – Here we are again, coming back to check on Kate and James after they´ve had a successful meeting and got a big pioneer contract. 

James – You know, you´re really impressive, Kate. I mean, you totally got that Mr. Lee. It’s eating out of your palm of your hand. 

Kate – No, you are. This trip is a success thanks to how well you know your baby. 

James – Haha. Oh we’d better check on our stock, and make sure that we have enough DVDs. 

Kate – That’s right. That’s my first task when I get back to the office. 

James – Wow, here we are at the airport already. Let me help you check in. 

Kate – Oh, wow. Thank you, James. It’s very nice of you. 

James – Kate! I just found out that our flight has been canceled! 

Kate – What? What are we going to do now? I wanna go home! 

James – I’ve got us another one, but the earliest, only tomorrow. 

Kate – What about tonight? 

James – We´ll need a hotel and taxi service to the airport tomorrow. Let me get that done as well. 

Kate – You´re very handy, James. 

James – Okay, done. Now we need to keep David in the picture, I mean keep him informed. 

Kate – Oh, yes. You´re right, it’s always a good idea to keep the boss updated on what’s going on. Keep him in the loop. 

Narrator – Katie, brainstorm what you want to say, something like:
“I have some good news to share.”
“We’ve closed the deal, and the contract is signed.”
“We need to proceed with the order as quickly as possible.”
“I’m committed to getting the order delivered right and on time.”

Kate – Right, thank you, Ted. 

David – Hello? 

Kate – Hi David. It’s Kate here. I’ve got some good news! 

David – What’s that? Oh, is it about the new contract in Korea?

Kate – Yes! We’ve closed the deal with KoreTech Global, and the contract has been signed. 

David – Excellent! 

Kate – To keep them happy, we need to proceed with the order as quickly as possible. 

David – Of course. So, so what´s your plan to do that? 

Kate – Well, obviously, I’m committed to getting the order delivered right and on time, but I might need some help doing that. 

David – Oh, sure. We’re welcoming a new staff member here, Rose Miller. 

Kate – Oh, that’s fantastic. I´ll need to check our stock first, oh, but unfortunately tonight we have to stay here. Our flight´s been canceled. 

David – I see. Just call me if you need anything. And congratulations to you two! 

Kate – Thank you, David. James, we’ve got someone new at the office. 

James – That’s great. Now let’s find a restaurant. My treat! 

Narrator – So that’s it. David´s been kept informed, and now all we care about is what may happen to Kate and James in romantic Seoul, alone. But more on that later, now it’s time for you to practice. Get to it! 


Recording 9.2 - Conversation - Keeping Things Professional

Lesson 09 - Working From Seoul

Recording 9.2 - Conversation - Keeping Things Professional

Narrator – So, here we are again to see what could happen when two young hearts are lost in a romantic city. It seems that James has taken Kate out for dinner, and he´s treating… 

James – Yeah, this bar looks quite cozy. What would you like, Kate? 

Kate – Well, I’m not very familiar with Korean food. Would you mind helping me? 

James – Sure, actually, Korean cuisine is mainly based on rice, vegetables, and meat. They use chopsticks to eat, but I can ask for a fork and knife if you like. 

Kate – Oh, well, you seem to know a lot about many things. 

James – Only a bit, Kate. I´d like some beer. Today was quite a day, and we should celebrate some. The night is young. Wow, that looks good. 

Kate – Oh, it is good. What’s that over there? Are you feeling okay, James? You look… You look like you’re kinda drunk all of a sudden. 

James – I am? Maybe a bit buzzed. But Kate I want to know more about you. 

Kate – Me? 

James – Yes, like, are you in a relationship? 

Kate – Oh… Well… 

James – How many boyfriends have you had? 

Kate – Oh, Ted, he’s getting too personal! What should I do now? 

Narrator – All the beer has made him more talkative and inquisitive than usual. You can say things like:
“It’s not really appropriate to talk like (or about) that.”
“I´d prefer not to talk about that right now, if you don´t mind.”
“Could we change the subject, please?”

Kate – Oh, okay, thank you, Ted. Maybe you´ll get me off the hook.
James, I think that´s too personal. It’s not appropriate to talk like that. Could we change the subject, please? 

James – Oh, I’m sorry. Okay. So, you´re very pretty and likable, Katie. I think I´ve got a crush on you. 

Narrator – I don’t think he’s got the message yet, but try to talk about work or something else. 

Kate – Okay, good idea.
James, can we keep our conversation professional? There’s a lot to discuss about how to finish this order now. 

James – But, I’d like to talk about you. You are fascinating to me, so please… 

Kate – I think I´ll get you a lemonade or something. How about some hot coffee? 

James – Never mind Kate. I think I got the message. Another drink for you? 

Narrator – Sometimes conversations with your colleagues can get too personal, but this is a reminder of what to say to keep things professional:
“It’s not appropriate to talk like that.”
“Could we change the subject, please?”
“Can we keep our conversation professional, please? Let´s focus on business.”
But this story can´t just end here, can it? You can go to the next topic to find out what happens. But before that, it’s time for practice. Up close and personal!


Recording 9.3 - Conversation - Checking Information

Lesson 09 - Working From Seoul

Recording 9.3 - Conversation - Checking Information

Narrator – Welcome back! After some awkward moments, James and Kate are now getting back on track with their work. 

James – Sorry, Kate. I got a little carried 

Kate – Oh, no problem. Oh, but we really need to check our stock to process this order ASAP. 

James – You´re totally right. But I can’t think of anyone to help us with that at the moment. 

Kate – Well, while waiting for you to sober up, I emailed Rose Miller, our new staff, and she said she´d get back to me in an hour. 

James – Rose Miller? That name sounds familiar… 

Kate – Oh, you know her? 

James – Oh, that’s not good news, Kate. But we don’t have choice right now. Just wait for her email. I´ll tell you more about her later. 

Kate – Right. Here it is. Oh no, we´ve got 100,000 blank DVDs left? That’s impossible! 

James – Yeah, that doesn’t sound right. We should call back and check this with her. 

Kate – I´m way ahead of you! 

James – Wait, Kate! You don’t know this Madam Rose Miller. Be careful! 

Kate – Oh Ted, James has made me so nervous. I’ve never talked to her and now I need to call her to say I´m skeptical about her work. 

Narrator – Calm down, Katie. Just be polite, stick to the issue, and start by saying things like:
“I just want to make sure that…”
“Could you possibly clarify…”
“I only want to check…”
“Just to be absolutely clear…”
“One thing I wasn’t sure of was…”
Good luck! We know you can do it.

Kate – Hello, Rose, I’m Kate. Thank you for your timely email. 

Rose – Oh, Katie Cat, you sound so emotional because of my prompt response. You can thank me later; there´s no need to call back immediately. 

Kate – Sorry to bother you, I just want to check something. We´re wondering about the number of DVDs… 

Rose – What? You mean you don’t trust me? Are you calling me a liar to my face?! 

Kate – Oh no! No, it’s not that. I just want to make sure about… 

Rose – I never make a mistake. Do you hear that? Am I making myself perfectly clear?! 

Kate – Of course, but since we have such a big order, we need to make absolutely sure about our stock. 

Rose – Hm. 

Kate – Could you possibly clarify again the number of our DVDs in stock? 

Rose – 1,000. 

Kate – Right. Well, that’s different from your email, but thank you anyway. 

Narrator – Well done, Kate. Rose seems a bit hard to work with, but you said all the right things. Now let’s hope for the best. Maybe she´ll straighten up and fly right… 


Recording 9.4 - Conversation - Placing an Order

Lesson 09 - Working From Seoul

Recording 9.4 - Conversation - Placing an Order

Narrator – Welcome the offices of E+ Education… 

James – Kate, are you all right? What did Rose tell you? 

Kate – Well, it’s not good, James. We need about 5,000 DVDs now. 

James – I’ve got our agency number here. Don’t be stressed out. I’ll call them right now. 

Kate – Oh, don’t worry! I can do that, James.
Hello, this is Kate from E+ Education speaking. I want some blank DVDs now. Snap to it! 

Narrator – Katie! 

Kate – What? 

Narrator – Let’s try to make this order a bit more politely, okay? 

Kate – Oh okay! I’m sorry, but I’m so nervous right now. My hands are shaking! 

Narrator – Calm down, Katie. To put in an order, here´s what you need to say:
“I’d like to place an order for…”
“We’re going to need…”
“Could you kindly send…”
“Could we also have…”
“When can we expect to receive them?”

Kate – Right!
Hello again, sorry about that.
I’d like to place an order for some blank DVDs, please.
We’re going to need quite a big number.
Could you send 5,000 please.
That’s right, we´d like the highest quality.
Could we also have the name and logo of our company on the boxes?
And finally, when can we expect to receive them?
Okay, oh, thank you, and goodbye for now.

Narrator – Good, well done! That was much better. 

James – Kate, you sound really professional! 

Kate – I do? Oh. Thank you. 

James – Now we should get some rest before tomorrow´s flight. I guess big trouble is waiting for us tomorrow at the office. 

Kate – You mean Rose Miller? 

James – Yeah, you got that right. But first let me send a quick email to David, telling him about this mess, so that we can start working on this order as soon as we get back. 

Kate – That’s right. Copy me in so that I don’t miss anything. 

James – Sure, Kate! 

Narrator – So, things have gotten back into place.
Now, let’s remember the phrases Kate used to place her order:
“I’d like to place an order for…”
“We’re going to need…”
“Could you send…”
“Could we also have…”
“When can we expect to receive them?”
And what can possibly be waiting for them at the office? Let´s practice this lesson before finding out about a new drama in the next lesson. Cheers, and happy ordering!
