Online Practice - Exercises
Exercise 1

A couple is having dinner in an Italian restaurant. Find out what they talk about by completing the conversation below.

Waiter: Thank you for choosing our restaurant. Here is a menu for you.
Rondo: Thank you. Let me think for a second.
Waiter: That’s fine, sir. When you decide what you would like to order, please call me. My name is Anthony.
Rondo: Thank you Anthony. I (1) _____________ (remember) that. Annie, take a look at this menu. There are a lot of different kinds of pasta and pizza.
Annie: Let me see. I think I (2) _____________ (order) a small  pizza with ground beef. I love beef!
Rondo: Are you sure? I think that we (3) _____________ (find) better choices on the next page. Don’t you want to try something new?
Annie: OK, I (4)_____________ (try) pasta with Parmesan cheese, mushrooms and ground beef.
Rondo: I (5) _____________ (have) pasta with mozzarella, shrimp and lobster.
Annie: I’d like to try that pasta. (6) ____  you _____ (let)  me have a bite?
Rondo:   Sure. Excuse me, Anthony.
Anthony: What kind of pasta or pizza would you like?
Rondo: I (7) _____________ (have) pasta with mozzarella, shrimp and lobster. She (8) _____________ (have) pasta with Parmesan, mushrooms and ground beef.
Anthony: OK, I (9) _____________ (tell) the kitchen. By the way, would you like any wine or water to drink?
Rondo: I (10) _____________ (drive), so a glass of water would be good for me.
Annie: I (11) _____________ (have) some white wine. I remember hearing that it goes well with pasta.
Anthony: OK then. One pasta with mozzarella, shrimp and lobster, and one with Parmesan, mushrooms and ground beef. Your drink will be here shortly and your food order will be ready in 15 minutes. Enjoy the evening!

Exercise 2

Complete the sentences with Future Perfect or Future Continuous forms of the verbs in brackets. Use contractions where possible.

  1. _________ (he / retire) by the time he’s 60?
  2. Next year, I _________ (study) Economics at university.
  3. Hurry up! The match  _________ (start) soon.
  4. Everyone  _________ (go) home by the time you come back to the office.It’s too late now.  
  5. This time next week, I  _________ (surf) and _________ (swim) in the sea and  _________ (enjoy) my holiday.
  6. Oh my god, what do I do? I  _________ (not finish) this homework before the lesson starts!
  7. Her birthday party will start at 6. I’m hoping that the florist  _________ (send) the flowers to her address by then.

Exercise 5

The following conversation is a job interview. Complete the conversation with will, shall or won’t.

Manager: So, what (1) ____ you _____  (do) to benefit  our company?

Employee: I (2) ________  (use) some marketing tools  to find customers and persuade them to buy our products.

Manager: That sounds better than what the previous employee did. However, I don’t think that your plan is practical. It (3) ________ (work).

Employee: No sir. It (4) ________ (divide), I promise you.

Manager: Tell me why I should  trust you.

Employee: I (5) ________  (divide) my plan into three steps. First, we  (6) ________ (take) two months to explore the market and customer attitudes.

Manager: OK, and, what (7) ______  your second step _____ (be)?

Employee: The second step (8) ________ (be) to use  marketing tools like Google ads and  social networks to attract customers and survey their reactions.

Manager: That’s great to hear. What is the final step?

Employee: Well, I (9) ________ (host) an event to boost our interactions  with customers.

Manager: Personally, I’m really impressed with your plan. But, I need to discuss it with the CEO first. I (10) ________ (call)  you as soon as I talk with him, OK?

Employee: Sure, that’s fine with me.


Exercise 7

Two friends are talking to each other. Complete the conversation with the correct form of the verbs in brackets .

June: Hello May, Tom told me that you (1) _________ (fly) to London this summer.
May: Hi, Tom is partly right, I had chosen London. But, I changed my mind  and I (2) _________ (go) to Paris instead.
June: Paris? Wow, are you serious?
May: Yeah, I (3) _________ (go) to France this August. I still don’t have the exact schedule, though, because I haven’t booked anything yet.
June: That’s great. Congratulations!
May: How about you? I remember that you probably (4) _________(not go) to New Zealand anymore.
June: I’m still not sure as my visa is still being processed.
I (5) _________ (have) another appointment at the New Zealand Embassy on September 2nd.
May: I hope that you (6) _________ (receive) your visa soon and maybe one day we (7) _________ (see) each other in Europe.
June: Sure! You too, May! Enjoy every single day before you leave  and prepare for this new stage in life.
