Recording 12.1 - Conversation - Being in Charge

Lesson 12 - New Chances

Recording 12.1 - Conversation - Being in Charge

Narrator – Welcome back to E+ Education, where the team has done an excellent job on a proposal for a new product. Kate is looking forward to David’s decision to give it the green light – the problem is, he is nowhere to be found. 

Kate – Mary, have you seen David? Isn’t he supposed to be here today? 

Mary – Oh. He’s just called in sick, Kate. He wanted you to call him back. Looks like something’s important. 

James – What´s that, Mary? 

Mary – David caught a bad cold and is now in the hospital. Can you believe it? 

Kate – I´ll call him now. 

David – Hello. 

Kate – Hello David, it’s Kate from your office. Are you okay? 

David – Oh, not really. I caught a cold yesterday, and with a full night rest, I should´ve recovered by now. But that strong coffee I drank kept me awake all night. And now I’m in the hospital, feeling totally run down. 

Kate – Oh dear, you should really cut down on your coffee. So, you’re not coming to work today? 

David – Oh, no. I’m afraid not, I need to rest for a few days, so I’m asking if you could take my place for just a few days? 

Kate – Oh, David, I don’t know what to do, let alone how to do your job. 

David – It’s nothing big or difficult. You can ask Mary for the task list to know what must be finished in terms of priority. Thank you in advance, Kate. 

Narrator – That’s great, Kate. You’ve got all the skills, and now you need to really take charge and show everyone who’s boss! 

Kate – Ted, I don’t know what to say. Everybody can read it on my face that I’m extremely nervous about this. They can read me like an open book! 

Narrator – Katie, you have all the skills, so just remember: Don’t be too bossy. Here are some sentences which could be useful for you: “If anyone has any issues about their work, please come and speak to me.” “I´d like to have a meeting to discuss our work.” “If there’s anything at all you want to ask, my door is always open.” “I have an open-door policy.” 

Kate – Thanks. Now everyone, as you know, David has put me in charge for now, so if you have any issues about your work, please come and speak to me directly. 

James – Good to hear, Kate. At the same time, tell me if you need anything. 

Kate – Right. Now, it’s business as usual, and if there’s anything you want to ask, my door is always open. And James, could I have a word with you in my office, now? 

Narrator – Excellent, Kate! You’re like a true boss! But there will be challenges coming your way soon. Let’s find out in the next topic. But first, it’s your turn to practice. Enjoy, and boss those English words around! 


Recording 12.2 - Conversation - Immediate Challenges

Lesson 12 - New Chances

Recording 12.2 - Conversation - Immediate Challenges

Narrator – Here we are again at E+ Education only to find out that things are a little chaotic; David is hospitalized, and Kate is the acting boss now. Lying right ahead is a big challenge. Let’s find out how they deal with it. 

James – So, what’s the matter, Kate? 

Kate – I found this note on David’s calendar. It says: Closing date – finalized. 

James – What exactly does that mean? 

Kate – Is this possibly the end for E+ Education? 

James – Well, I don’t think so, but… 

Kate – Mary? How long have you been here? 

Mary – I… I… I just got here now. I didn’t hear anything. 

James – Oh, dear. Mary, you mustn’t spread rumors in the office, okay? 

Mary – What? Of course not. Oh no, Natalie, we´re all gonna be fired.

Kate – Oh, no, Mary is spreading rumors about job cuts. Everyone’s starting to panic. 

James – Call a meeting to clear the air right away, Kate! 

Kate – But I don’t know what to say. 

Narrator – You have to stop the nasty rumors. Say: “There are some rumors going around that aren´t true. “I´m busy trying to establish the facts, and I´ll let you all know as soon as I have some clear information.” “I would be grateful if you hear any rumors not to pass them on.” This situation can be tricky, and you have to handle it well. Good luck! 

Kate – This is hard, but I can do it. I need to put a damper on these rumors! 

Mary – Are you going to fire us all?! 

Kate – No, Mary. Any rumors about firing are simply not true. 

James – That’s right. Rumors are rumors. Don´t pay attention to the rumor mill! 

Kate – I´m trying to establish the facts, and if I get any information, I´ll let you know straight away. 

Mary – But, what if they are true? 

Kate – Ignore the rumors, and please don’t pass them on. Could you please just carry on with your work as normal? Thank you for all your efforts. 

James – Call David, Kate. He’s the one who knows. 

Kate – You’re right. I´ll do it now. 

Narrator – Is this the end of E+ Education as we know it, or just a basic misunderstanding? Let’s go on to the next topic to find out. But before that, it’s time for you to practice. Enjoy (and maybe you´ll even want to practice creating a rumor or two, but make sure it´s in English)! 


Recording 12.3 - Conversation - Award Winner

Lesson 12 - New Chances

Recording 12.3 - Conversation - Award Winner

Narrator – On Kate’s first day at work, she had to deal with rumors around the office, and now it’s time to find out the truth. Everybody appreciates honesty and it´s time to get down to the nitty-gritty. Let’s see how she’s going to do it. 

David – Hello, David speaking. 

Kate – Hello David. It’s Kate. Sorry to bother you. I know you´ve been out sick. Are you feeling better? 

David – I’m good, getting better. Thanks. So, is everything okay over there? 

Kate – Not really. I found one of your notes that says “Closing date – finalized” and now, there are rumors swirling around about closing down the company. 

David – Oh dear! It’s about the ESL conference tomorrow. I managed to register E+ Education just before the closing date. 

Kate – I see. That´s it? As simple as that?! 

David – And we´ve been nominated for the Innovative Education Award. Kate, you will have to be there with, well with some kind of acceptance speech. 

Kate – But I’ve never done it before. I haven´t the least idea of how to make an acceptance speech! 

David – Ask James and Mary for their help. I bet you´ll kill it. 

Kate – Okay, I will, and try not to get killed in the process! Get well soon. James, can I speak to you for a minute please? 

James – Sure! Did you talk to David already? 

Kate – Just hung up the phone with him. So no job cuts, no layoffs, nobody fired. I’ll ask Mary to tell everyone. 

James – That’s good news for all of us. 

Kate – But I have another pressing problem. We´ve been nominated for the Innovative Education Award, and I´ll have no choice but to speak in public if we win. 

James – Well, it’s no big deal. I can give you some sentences to use. Say: “On behalf of everyone at E+ Education, I´d like to thank you for our award.” “Our company is at the forefront of educative innovation.” “This award is a real boost for everyone working at the company.” 

Kate – Thank you. Please can you go with me tomorrow? I’m a bit nervous. In fact, I´m already shaking like a leaf! 

James – Sure. I´ll be right by your side. So, here we are at the conference. Are you ready to receive the award yet, Kate? 

Kate – If we win, I am.

Announcer – Ladies and gentlemen, please take your seats for our special award ceremony. Thank you everyone for being here. And the highlight of today is the winner of our Innovative Teaching Award. And this year, the winner is… E+ Education with its brand new X3 Coaching program. 

James – Well, Kate, it’s time for you to shine. Break a leg! 

Kate – Ladies and gentlemen. “On behalf of everyone at E+ Education, thank you for our award. Our company is extremely proud to stand at the forefront of teaching innovation.” This award is a real boost for everyone working at the company. We pride ourselves on innovation, quality, and of course, cutting-edge education. And coming soon, we’ll be launching a series of new courses. Stay tuned! So thank you again everyone, this really means a lot to us.

Narrator – Well said, Kate. Things seem to get better and better for you and your company. Let’s see if there´re any turning points ahead. But first, it’s time for you to practice. Enjoy, and get ready for your own acceptance speech in English! 


Recording 12.4 - Conversation - Networking

Lesson 12 - New Chances

Recording 12.4 - Conversation - Networking

Narrator – Welcome back to the conference where the good guys at E+ Education have just received an award. A conference is also a chance to network and promote your business. Let’s see how Kate and James do that. 

James – David asked us to do some networking, so this is your best chance, Kate. 

Kate – What exactly am I supposed to do? 

James – You can talk to some potential customers, finding connections with people and organizations to expand your network. Be yourself, and wear a smile. 

Narrator – Right, Kate. It involves meeting people and talking to them, sharing information, hopefully helping each other, and ultimately, getting new business. You don´t need to use a net, just network! 

Kate – So basically, I have to talk to a lot of strange people I don’t know? Is that the gist of it? 

Narrator – That’s it, but don´t think of them as strange (as you once did of James!). Introduce yourself, your business, what your products are, and who you can help. Ask them about themselves. Maybe you can find someone, something, some way to help yourself in the process. 

Kate – Thanks. I’ll give it a go. Hello, I’m Kate, E+ Education Sales Executive. 

Bob – Oh hi, congratulations on your award. I’m Bob Sanders from the School of Elite. The conference is very well organized, isn’t it? 

Kate – It is. I was a bit nervous about speaking to a big audience here, but I guess I did all right. 

Bob – Well, you did a good job, in my humble opinion. So, you´re about to launch a new product? 

Kate – Oh yes, I think it will be attractive to our target learners. So, can you tell me more about your school, the School of Elite you said? It´s a most unique name! 

Bob – Well, we are a boarding school for girls. Our aim is to educate smart, independent, free-thinking and good-hearted girls. We´re also devoted to providing a thorough education, filled with knowledge and life skills. 

Kate – I can tell you, that is exactly how we try to develop our courses at E+ Education. 

Bob – Great. We´re having trouble with our English curriculum now. Most of the textbooks are too academic and frankly boring, you know. Nothing about real life unfortunately. 

Kate – That’s true. It’s been always an issue in schools. 

Bob – Yes, can you imagine how difficult it is for us to design our program with such limited English courses available?! 

Kate – I completely understand. I think we might be in a good position to help you with that. 

Bob – Oh well, I certainly hope that’s true. We need help from somebody, and soon! 

Kate – If I could have your contact details, I’ll send you one of our brochures and some other relevant information. And here’s my business card, if you want to call me, feel free. I love to chat about improving education, especially English learning. 

Bob – That’s great. Thank you so much! I´m going to reach out to you very soon. 

Kate – It´s my pleasure. 

Narrator – Good job, Kate! You have a potential new customer. Let’s see how this can help the E+ Education team. But before that, enjoy your practice and keep networking!
