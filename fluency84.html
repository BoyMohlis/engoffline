Day 84 - Rapid Response - Launching preparation

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 14.6 from 3 to 5 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 14.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Recording 14.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Topic 1: Coaching

Kate – Welcome back Mary, we just couldn’t survive without you!

Mary – Oh, that´s nice of you to say.

David – Mary, since the new project is up and running, I need you to do some work on sales too. So we´ll move your table here, next to Kate´s.

Mary – I love this idea!

David – Well, I think that Kate can show you the ropes as this job is new to you anyway.

Kate – Mary, it’s so good to have you back. Shall we get started then and I’ll show you the ropes?

Mary – Of course, Kate.

Kate – I see that you´re very experienced on the phone, but put it aside for now. Just watch what I do for a while.

Mary – Oh, yes. I can follow what you´re doing. It´s becoming clear to me.

Kate – I’m just checking our database to see who we haven’t called for a while. So Mary, why don’t you follow my lead and get started with this?

Mary – Of course.

Kate – All right. I’ll leave you to it-I’m here to help, so please ask me if you need to know what to do.

Mary – Okay Kate, I won’t be long.

James – Nice work, Kate! You are really good at giving easy-to-follow instructions.

Kate – Thank you, James. You’ve helped me a lot along the way!

 

Topic 2: Design

David – Guys, listen up. We´re opening a conference next month to introduce our new course.

James – We don’t have much time to get it right.

Kate – Well, that’s great, I know we can do it. Let’s review the tasks that we have to finish.

David – James completed all the content yesterday. Good job, James! You´re really a master at putting together winning content.

James – Thank you for the compliment, David.

David – Now, what we really need is a flyer to introduce it at the conference.

James – I have some designing experience. Let’s go brighten up our course.

David – That’s right, James. You two can go and have a chat now, and get the ball rolling. Mary, you stay here, and we can discuss how to best prepare for the conference.

James – Let’s go, Kate. You just have to tell me what you want, and explain your requirements exactly.

Kate – This is quite a special project for us, so we wanna get it right.

James – Absolutely.

Kates – I already have a specific idea in mind.

James – Great, tell me more about it. What´s this brilliant idea of yours?

Kate – Well, based on what we´ll introduce at the conference, I was hoping it could be A5 paper size with good images to clearly show our product, but not too colorful. Key words will be highlighted, but not too many. Does that make sense?

James – Yeah. Quite clear. I´m a visual person, so I have to picture it in my mind or on paper.

Kate – Obviously, you´re a designer, so I’m interested in hearing your thoughts on how to improve our idea. Fire away!

James – Well, it looks very impressive already. Gosh, where are you from?

Kate – From E+ Education! Duh.

 

Topic 3: Chop Chop!

David – Kate, you´re in charge of the contract with the School of Elite, right? They´re asking for DVD´s and account numbers.

Kate – But, I thought James had already sorted that out?

David – Clearly not. I need you to pull out all the stops, get this sorted out as fast as you can.

Kate – Pull out what stops? We´re not in our car at the moment…

David – I mean, do all you can to get this order completed post-haste. Please!

Kate – I´ve gotta step on it! James, I need your help. It’s that order for the School of Elite; we need to process it urgently.

James – The order? I sent that out ages ago, with the paperwork and everything.

Mary – You mean this paperwork, James, still on your desk?

James – Oh no! I must have forgotten. It slipped my mind completely. I’ve had so much to do and so many things on my mind.

Kate – Well, they’re not happy, so we’ve got to pull out all the stops and get them what they want to keep their business.

James – OK, but what about the conference coming up tomorrow?

Kate – We’ve got to get to this now. The conference prep will have to wait. The order’s our number one priority. You go and get the DVD´s and books from the warehouse, and I’ll sort out this paperwork. Then I’ll meet you downstairs in 15 minutes.

James – Why, where are we going?

Kate – We’re getting a taxi to their offices now.

James – You´re right, Kate. We´ve gotta go there to save face. I think you saved the day once again.

 

Topic 4: Launch Preparation

David – Kate, good job on your work with the School of Elite. Now for the conference, we need to reserve a room, and arrange food and drinks.

Kate – Will do, David. Hi Mary, I’ve got to reserve the venue for our presentation at the conference. Do you have any smart ideas?

Mary – Well, I have a list of phone numbers for you. But I really recommend the Royal Center. They´ve got good prices and great service.

Kate – Oh, thanks, Mary. I’ll give it a try.

Woman – Hello, this is the Royal Center. How can I help you?

Kate – Oh hi, this is Kate from E+ Education. I need to check the availability of your venue for a product launch we’re doing very soon.

Woman – Right! Well, I’m sure I can help you out. We do have a conference room available. When´s it for?

Kate – Tomorrow.

Woman – Such short notice! Let´s see if we can arrange something at the last minute… We do have a small room that you could use.

Kate – I see… And how many people does it hold comfortably?

Woman – You could easily squeeze in about 40 people.

Kate – 40 people. I guess it´ll have to do in a pinch. Do you have any business facilities to plug in our laptops, projector, wi-fi, that sort of thing?

Woman – Oh yes, yes, we’re very hi-tech. We even have a fax machine and a water cooler!

Kate – And can you provide hospitality too?

Woman – Of course! We do a ‘business platter’ consisting of orange juice, sandwiches, canapés-that really means cheese and pineapple chunks on skewers.

Kate – That’s great! Okay… and most importantly, how much does it all cost?

Woman – Well, to book the room with all the facilities for two hours usually costs $1,000. The hospitality is extra.

Kate – You said you’ll throw the food and drinks in?! Won’t that get a bit messy? We don´t have a lot of time for cleaning…

Woman – No, I mean to say we’ll provide the food and drinks for free. Just don’t tell anyone, otherwise everyone will expect it!

Kate – Well, that’s fantastic! Thanks. If you can send us an invoice, we’ll sort the payment out as soon as possible.

Part A: Building Comprehension

After the flyer, what does the team need next for the conference?

They need a special room for their upcoming presentation at the conference.

The team needs a special room for their upcoming presentation at the conference.

E+ Education team needs a special room for their upcoming presentation at the conference.

What can Kate do to find a suitable venue for the launch on such short notice?

She can ask for recommendations, search on the internet, or make some quick calls.

Kate can ask for a recommendation, search on the internet, and make some quick phone calls.

She can ask for recommendations, search on the net, and make some quick calls to find a suitable venue.

Who does Kate ask for help?

Mary.

Kate asks Mary.

Kate asks Mary if she has any smart ideas to help Kate reserve a suitable venue.

Which venue does Mary recommend to Kate?

The Royal Center.

Mary recommends the Royal Center to Kate.

She recommends Kate the Royal Center.

Why does Mary recommend to Kate the Royal Center?

They’ve got good prices and great service.

Mary suggests the Royal Center because they offer good prices and great service.

Mary recommends Kate the Royal Center as they have good prices and great service.

Is there any conference room available at the Royal Center?

Yes, there is.

There´s a conference room available.

They have a conference room available.

Can the staff at the venue arrange anything at the last minute?

Yes, they can.

They have a small room that the E+ Education team could use for the conference tomorrow.

They have a small room for the E+ Education team at the last minute.

How many people does the room hold comfortably?

40 people.

The room can hold 40 people comfortably.

The room comfortably holds 40 people.

Who says they are very hi-tech?

The staff at the Royal Center.

The receptionist at the Royal Center.

The receptionist at the Royal Center says they are very hi-tech.

Will the food be served for free at the Royal Center?

Yes, it will.

It´ll be served for free.

The food will be served for free at the Royal Center.

Part B: In Other Words

The hotel will provide us a special space to host the event.

The hotel will have a special venue for us to host the event.

We´re used to finishing things around here at the last minute.

We´re used to finishing things around here on short notice.

Speaking English with foreigners is fun, you should experiment with it!

Speaking English with foreigners is fun, you should give it a try!

At times we need to assist our colleagues, specifically at the end of the month.

At times we need to help our colleagues out, especially at the end of the month.

Do you know any specialist in this field who can help us in a tough situation?

Do you know any specialist in this field who can help us in a pinch?

Do you think we should include some free books with this order?

Do you think we could throw in some free books with this order?

The fresh cut flowers were a nice addition to the venue.

The fresh cut flowers were a nice touch for the venue.

I couldn´t believe how many wild ideas they were freely exchanging in the meeting.

I couldn´t believe how many wild ideas they were throwing around in the meeting.

Did you remember to reserve our rental car?

Did you remember to book our rental car?

Part C: Language to Go – How to Book a Conference Room

What could you say to check if the venue’s available?

Could I check the availability of your venue please?

How would you ask about things related to business in the venue?

What business facilities do you have to offer in your venue?

How can you ask about the number of people the venue holds?

How many people does the venue hold comfortably?

What would you say if you want to check on things like food and drinks?

Do you provide hospitality, including food and drinks?

How would you check on the price?

What´s the total cost of the venue please?
