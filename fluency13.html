Recording 3.1 - Conversation - Apologize to Clients

Lesson 03 - Improve Basic Skills

Recording 3.1 - Conversation - Apologize to Clients

Narrator – Here we are, back at E+ Education where Kate is going to deal with an angry customer. In fact, he´s not just angry, he´s furious.

Kate – No Ted, what should I do?

Narrator – Just calm down! Go in and talk to him and say you´re sorry, but you have to sound like you really mean it, and you totally understand the problem. You can say things like: “I’m really sorry to hear that.” “That must have been very inconvenient for you.” Most importantly, you have to make a promise to deal with the problem immediately, and reassure the customer, like this: “It won’t happen again.” “You have my word that we will sort this out soon.” “We can do something for you right away to make up for this inconvenience.”

Kate – Oh, I see. Thank you for helping me cool the customer off. Hello, Mr. Smith. Now I understand that you´re upset. How can I help you put things right?

Mr. Smith – This morning, right in the middle of our English exam for the whole school, your website crashed! It was such a waste of our money, effort, and time!

Kate – I’m really sorry to hear that. What more?

Mr. Smith – As a result, we had to cancel the tests of 1,000 students this morning, and the questions were revealed, so we now need to build other sets.

Kate – That must’ve been very inconvenient for you.

Mr. Smith – Of course, it was! And do you know how much time we´ll have to spend to work on this again? A lot! And who’s gonna do that now? Who do you think has this kind of spare time?!

Kate – We promise that this problem won’t happen again. You have my word that we will sort this out for you soon.

Mr. Smith – How?

Kate – We could help you to build your test content again on our site. We’ll compensate the damage our website caused you to make up for this inconvenience.

Mr. Smith – Well, that kind of compensation would be nice, and the least you can do!

Kate – We’re about to unveil an E – learning course for the University Entrance Examination which we guarantee will help increase the passing rate of your high school by 25%.

Mr. Smith – Oh really?

Kate – Yes. We’re glad to offer you a discount of 20% off the registration fee for all students studying at your school.

Mr. Smith – Good, thank you! Now things are starting to look up again.

Kate – The site is going to go live again in exactly four hours. And I promise, this won’t happen again.

Mr. Smith – Sounds good. As you´re so lovely, we’ll continue to use your service.

Narrator – Mr. Smith has turned quickly from an angry to a happy customer. He´s not so hot under the collar now. That’s great to know! Come with us to the next lesson to find out what David will say about Kate´s work today.


Recording 3.2 - Conversation - Telephone Manner

Lesson 03 - Improve Basic Skills

Recording 3.2 - Conversation - Telephone Manner

Narrator – In the last lesson, you saw Kate learn how to say sorry. Today, let’s find out what more she can learn at E+ Education.

Kate – Hello? Who? What? No, he’s not here. Give me your number. I’ll tell him to call you later. 6…8…9, yep, yep. Okay. Bye!

Mary – Oh, Kate! Before David has the chance to make you cry, I think I should talk to you.

Kate – Yes, Mary? What´s up?

Mary – I have to say I think it’s a little rude of you speaking so directly on the phone like this. Do you know it negatively affects the image of our company?

Kate – Oh. Really?

Mary – Okay. I’m only a bit of an expert on the phone. Come here, I’ll give you some training. We can´t just chat with customers like we can with our pals.

Kate – Oh, great. Thanks, Mary. You’re so caring. I know you´re gonna help me get things straight.

Narrator – Wow. I think you and Mary are going to have a good relationship. Go ahead, Kate. You need to be all ears with her!

Mary – Now, let’s do some practice. When the phone rings, you say: “Hello, E+ Education. This is Mary speaking”. And then, “How can I help you?” Now you try it.

Kate – Hello, E+ Education. Kate speaking. How can I help you?

Mary – Oh, that’s perfect, Kate. You’re a quick learner.

Kate – Thank you for the compliment.

Mary – And, before hanging up, you can say: “Thank you again for calling.”

Kate – Oh. That’s great! The tricks of the trade.

Mary – Oh, James’s phone is ringing again. Go ahead and try it.

Kate – Oh, okay. Hello, E+ Education. Kate speaking. How may I help you?

Takashi – Please, may I please speak to James?

Kate – I’m afraid he’s not available at the moment.

Takashi – When is he going to be back in the office?

Kate – He should be back at about, oh, 3:00 P.M.

Narrator – That’s good. Go ahead, Kate. Ask him whether he wants to leave a message. You can say: “Can I (or could I) take a message?” Or: “Would you like to leave a message for him?”

Kate – Would you like to leave a message?

Takashi – Yes, please. Let him know that Takashi from the Japan International School called about rescheduling tomorrow’s meeting.

Kate – Oh my… I can’t hear his name clearly! What should I do?

Narrator – Takashi, it sounds like a Japanese name. Tokyo, Tokugawa, Takashi! Go for it. You can tell him to repeat or clarify his name by asking: “Could you please spell that for me?” “How do you spell that, please?” “Please let me see if I got it right.”

Kate – How do you spell your name? Is that T-A-K-A-S-H-I?

Takashi – That’s right. Thanks for double-checking.

Kate – Okay, Mr. Takashi. I’ll give him the message as soon as he comes back.

Takashi – Thank you very much.

Kate – You´re welcome. Goodbye.

Narrator – Very good, Katie. Now, don’t forget to give the message to James as soon as he shows up. Coming up next, James comes back. Kate and James will have a short conversation that´s really useful for Kate. What’s that you say? Before you find out in the next lesson, let’s practice this one first.


Recording 3.3 - Conversation - Email Language

Lesson 03 - Improve Basic Skills

Recording 3.3 - Conversation - Email Language

Narrator – It’s another busy day at E+ Education, Kate has learned some new skills and she´s dealing with the order from Mark Smith. How´s it going, Katie?

Kate – I’m excited about the contract. I’m emailing him right now and asking him to confirm his order. Done!

Narrator – Good luck, Katie. Keep your fingers crossed for good luck!

David – Kate, could I have a quick word with you in my office please?

Kate – Yes, David. Of course! Your wish is my command.

David – Now, look. I’ve read the email you sent to Mr. Smith yesterday since you copied me in.

Kate – Yes?

David – I’m concerned about how you write to your client. Your email reads, and I quote: “K. K – period – P-L-Z-C-F-M-U-W-N-T 6000.”

Kate – Yes. That is: “OK. Please confirm you want 6,000.”

David – Oh, what? For goodness’ sake! If I were the receiver, I´d stop the deal right now.

Kate – Oh. Why would you do that?

David – You’ve got to work on your email writing. Please re-send your message to Mr. Smith, in a way that makes sense. Don’t use all those crazy abbreviations and acronyms. Got it?

Kate – Oh, yes, I understand.

David – Thank you. We’ll talk again later.

James – What’s the matter, Kate?

Kate – David said I need to work on how to write an email more professionally.

James – Why? Let me see… Uh huh. Kate, that style you used is completely wrong for a business email.

Kate – So… What do I have to do to improve it?

James – You need to use specific phrases in a business-related email. You must begin with a greeting, such as: “Dear Mr. Colin”. Then you should thank them if they sent you an email first. After that, it´s necessary to state your purpose. Write something like: “I am writing to inquire about, or I am writing in reference to, or I am writing regarding…” Next, ask him to confirm the information with something like: “Please, could you confirm…” Remember to add your closing remarks such as: “Thank you for your consideration…” or “Thank you for your patience and cooperation.” Finally, follow up with, “If you have any questions or concerns, don’t hesitate to let me know” or “I am looking forward to hearing from you soon.”

Kate – Oh, okay. Well, I’ll try to rewrite it in a more suitable fashion. “Yours sincerely…” All right! James… James?!

James – Yes?

Kate – Could you please read through this before I send it?

James – Okay. Let me check it quickly. Much better. You can safely send it. I hope that Mr. Smith will think your last email was just a cat running across your keyboard.

Kate – Okay… Sent. I’m done! Thanks a bunch, James. Hello, E+ Education. Kate speaking… Mr. Smith? Yes… Yes…

Narrator – Well, luckily James helped Kate tidy up or improve her email. But now, Mr. Smith is calling Kate again. Is Kate in hot water once more?! Before finding out what happens, you need to practice this one first.


Recording 3.4 - Conversation - Saying No

Lesson 03 - Improve Basic Skills

Recording 3.4 - Conversation - Saying No

Narrator – In the last lesson, we stopped where Mark Smith had just called Kate back. Now let’s see how the story goes.

Kate – Hello, E+ Education. Kate speaking

Mr. Smith – Hello, Kate. This is Mark Smith from Brighton High School calling.

Kate – Oh hello, Mr. Smith. Is there any problem with your order?!

Mr. Smith – No, no. Well, first of all, you can just call me Mark

Kate – Okay, so… Mark. How can I help you?

Mark – I only wanted to thank you for the amazing course you provided us. And I was wondering if you’d also like to have dinner with me sometime soon?

Kate – Is there anything you need to discuss further, Mr. Smith, ah, Mark?

Mr. Smith – No, nothing to discuss further about business. The dinner is just personal.

Kate – Oh… I’m so sorry, I have to run now! Can I call you back a little later? Oh my God! Oh my gosh! What should I do now?

Mary – Kate, is everything okay?

Kate – Oh dear… Mary, help me… I’m stuck between a rock and a hard place.

Mary – What happened?

Kate – Mr. Smith wants to invite me out for dinner, and it’s personal.

Mary – I knew it. He’s not just around for business.

Kate – Yeah, right! So… What should I do? I’m not sure if it´s a good idea to mix business with pleasure.

James – Don’t say yes, Kate!

Mary – Why do you look so worried, James?

James – Nothing. It’s… It’s actually against company policy! I personally would never do it.

Mary – That’s right, Kate. This is what you say: “Thank you for your nice invitation. But I’m really sorry, having a non-business dinner with clients is against company policy. Blah blah blah…”

Kate – Excuse me, what exactly do you mean by “Blah”? Is that also a new business vocabulary word?!

Mary – No, just say it’s against company policy. It wouldn’t be appropriate. So, “With regret, I’m going to have to say no.” And don’t forget to say this sentence at the end: “But be assured that I won´t forget your kind gesture.”

Kate – Okay, thanks. I think I should call him back right now. He’ll surely be waiting for my call.

Mr. Smith – Hello, Mark Smith speaking.

Kate – Hello, Mr. Smith.

Mr. Smith – Kate! Please just call me Mark. So when do you have time for us to go out for a bit of a chat? I know a romantic spot around the corner…

Mary – Thank you for your offer…

Kate – Thank you for your offer. But I’m really sorry, I can’t accept.

Mr. Smith – Oh? Why not? I´m a really nice guy, and I certainly don´t mean any harm…

Mary – I’m afraid…

Kate – I’m afraid it’s against company policy. It wouldn’t be appropriate for me.

Mr. Smith – How boring! Try to loosen up a little! Don´t be so old-fashioned.

Kate – So, with regret, I’m going to have to say no. But be assured that I won´t forget your kind gesture.

Mr. Smith – I think I get it. Goodbye, Kate.

Kate – Goodbye.

Narrator – It doesn’t really sound like Mark Smith got it at all. Is he asking for trouble? Let´s be sure to find out in the next unit. But first, you must practice. Have fun and speak up!
