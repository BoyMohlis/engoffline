Day 02 - Catch-up Speaking

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Listen to Recording 1.5 from 1 to 3 times, without the transcript and try to understand it as much as you can.
✔️ With the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 1.5 from 1 to 2 times.
✔️ Then, with the transcript, listen again, and practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 1.5 from 1 to 2 times. Practice until you can keep up with the audio's speed.
✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 1.5 at least 2-3 times.
✔️ Note: Focus on the pronunciation of each word, especially the stress and the ending sounds.

Topic 1: Job Interview Practice

Narrator – So Katie, how are you doing out there? Are you nervous?

Kate – Well, I guess, a little. But I really want to work for them. And this position is so attractive!

Narrator – Well, don’t worry, you’ll be fine. Just be yourself and be confident.

Kate – But, but what should I say?

Narrator – Well, you can use some sentences to sell yourself a little bit.

Kate – And how do I do that exactly?

Narrator – Well, you can be specific and use the STAR method.

Kate – STAR?

Narrator – Yeah. S – T – A – R. It stands for Situation – Task – Action – Result.

Kate – Oh! Okay, I see. I think I can handle that.

Narrator – And, be positive.

Kate – All right.

Narrator – Last but not least, don’t forget to smile. And of course, dress for success!

Kate – That’s a great reminder. Thank you so much.

 

Topic 2: Common Questions

David – Good morning, I’m David, the Manager of E+ Education. And you are…?

Kate – Kate — it’s short for Katherine.

David – Oh, right, of course. Thank you so much for coming in and interviewing with us today.

Kate – Thank you very much for having me.

David – Now, Kate, your résumé is very impressive with many qualifications. So, do you have any teamwork experience that you can share with me?

Kate – I can say that I’m particularly proud of my work with the volunteer team. We cooperated to help a lot of disadvantaged people, especially young children.

David – Fantastic. So, what kind of sales experience do you think you can bring to us here at E+ 

Kate – Well a good example that comes to mind is that last year, in a very difficult market, I promoted a campaign that increased sales projection by 20%.

David – I see. That’s impressive.

Kate – And, punctuality is important to me.

David – Tell me about that. You manage your time well?

Kate – I’m particularly proud of my good time management. I manage my time by planning out what I have to do for the whole week. Also, each day I remind myself all my daily tasks, right after I get up in the morning. It keeps me on track and helps me to be more efficient.

David – That’s good to hear, Kate. Punctuality is our first priority here.

 

Topic 3: Structuring Your Answers

David – Finally, Kate, I want to ask you what exactly makes you want to apply for this job?

Kate – Because I really want this job.

David – And, what more?

Kate – And I will put as much effort as I can into it to get the work done.

David – Good! But why?

Kate – First, I want to work hard here because this job is an ideal match for my skill set and experience. Second, I’ve always looked forward to being a part of a fast – growing group like you are here.

David – Well, that’s absolutely right! E+ Education is the fastest growing group in the education sector. But what about you in particular?

Kate – Well, above all, your company offers a mix of short – term projects and long – term goals. I understand that the challenges I´ll face here can bring about personal and professional development.

David – I’m so sorry. I’ll have another meeting right now, Kate! I’m afraid I’ll have to go. We’ll call you later.

 

Topic 4: Making Suggestions

David – So, what’s the problem Mary?

Mary – We somehow lost the draft contract with the Global Development. I had it copied on a memory stick but now the stick’s gone. It’s vanished into thin air.

David – Impossible! We’ve been preparing for this the last three months and I urgently need it for my meeting now. Don’t you have it saved on your computer as well?

Mary – Unfortunately, not anymore. It was taking up a lot of space, so I´m afraid I deleted it.

Kate – Can I make a suggestion?

David – Yes, please! Anything that could help us out of this jam.

Kate – How about checking the recycle bin on your computer – – have you tried looking in that folder? There’s a chance that file´s still there.

Mary – Oh, I’m afraid it’s not. I looked there already.

Kate – Maybe you could ask your colleagues if they’ve seen the memory device.

Mary – They haven’t – – I already asked them.

Kate – Why don’t you look through your trash? You might possibly have thrown it away by mistake.

Mary – What do you think of me? I’m not that messy and disorganized. Excuse me!

Kate – Oh! Maybe we could search the whole office. That memory drive must be here somewhere.

Mary – You can´t just go and look at our confidential documents. You´re not a staff member here.

David – Ooh! All very sensible suggestions Kate – – thank you for your helpful input.

Kate – Oh, and I have another idea! Did you ever email the document to anyone?

David – Oh, that’s right, Mary! I remember you emailed it to me to proofread. I’ll go search in my inbox. Good thinking Kate!

Step 2: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Please use the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 1.6 from 1 to 3 times.
✔️ Then, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 1.6 from 1 to 2 times. You can use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary. Practice until you can keep up with the character's speed.
✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 1.6 at least 2-3 times until you feel confident with your speed.
✔️ Note: You should spend a lot of time on this step. You need to focus on the intonation, the linking sounds and the natural speed of the native speakers.
