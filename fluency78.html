Day 78 - Rapid Response - Reinstatement

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 13.6 from 3 to 5 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 13.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Lesson 13 Company Downsize

Recording 13.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Topic 1: Redundancy

James – Hi Mary, we’re back with an award for our company.

Mary – Oh! Great! We’ve had a successful year.

Kate – We have to celebrate. Do you know when David is back to work?

Mary – Well, he’s in his office now. Actually, he wants to see me right away. Talk to you guys later.

David – Hi, Mary. Sit down, I want to talk to you about a few urgent things.

Mary – You seem uncomfortable. Is everything okay?

David – We’ve got to move the furniture around, so to speak.

Mary – I need to change my desk or my chair?

David – No, not exactly that. I’m afraid we’ve got to let you go. We don’t have sufficient budget to keep you, at least until we know if the project is successful or not.

Mary – Are you firing me?!

David – Well Mary, we have to terminate your employment at this time.

Mary – After all the years I’ve worked here, why me? Why now?

David – Mary, I’m giving you seven days’ notice to vacate your desk. I know it’s tough.

Mary – Fine. Fine, David!

David – I hope you can understand my difficult decision. You’ve been a valuable staff member here for many years.

Mary – I need to go out right now!

Kate – Mary! What happened?

Mary – David fired me, or let me go. I don’t even know, I’m so confused!

James – What? No way!

Kate – He can’t possibly do that!

Mary – He just did. I guess I should take my things and be gone. Good riddance!

Kate – I’m so sorry, Mary! Here, let me make you some hot tea.

Mary – That’s very nice of you, Kate. I don’t know if I can actually drink it, my hands are trembling so!

James – I think the company is facing some redundancy now. David is a sensible man; he wouldn’t do this if he didn’t absolutely have to.

Kate – You’re right, James. This must be kinda hard for him too.

 

Topic 2: Career Advice

Kate – David, today’s Mary last day here. So, could you sign her card?

David – I know. I know. It’s hard for me too, you know. She’s someone you can really rely on.

James – Yes. I have to say, it’s not a good idea to get rid of her, David.

David – So sorry, guys. Look! Once we have our project back on track, we may need her back.

James – But by that time, she’ll have found a better job.

Kate – Oh my God, when she was talking to me about what she could do now with her life, I didn’t know what to say! I wanna at least give her a shoulder to cry on…

David – I think I’ll go talk to her and give her some career advice, you know. Hope she isn’t mad at me anymore. Come on, you guys come along too.

James – Come on, David. You know her, she can’t stay mad for two days straight.

David – Oh Mary! There you are. You know I don’t really want to let you go, right? It’s simply a necessary financial decision. Are you upset?

Mary – Yes, a bit, David, but I’m more worried about my next step. What should I do next? How’s my future going to turn out?

David – Oh, okay, sit down please, and we can talk. You need to think yeah, you need to think positively, Mary. Every dark cloud has a silver lining.

Mary – What if I can never find another job?

David – That’s not gonna happen. You’re a smart cookie. Now, have you tried looking in the job section of the newspaper?

Mary – No, not yet.

Kate – It’s a good place to start. And there are a lot of job-finding websites you could sign up for. Of course, some of them are free and some others cost money, but at least not an arm and a leg!

Mary – I haven’t thought about that. I haven’t really thought about anything yet!

Kate – Well, what about contacting some of the people you know, those who may know about a job that’d be appropriate for you.

Mary – Well. That’s a good idea. I could do some networking.

Kate – And of course, you could register with a recruitment agency. They’re good at helping find jobs for you and other unemployed folks.

Mary – Oh, brilliant! Thank you for your kind suggestions, David and Kate.

David – Well, it’s with great regret we have to let you go. Thank you, though, for all your hard work, and we wish you all the best in the future. Farewell!

 

Topic 3: A Chaotic Office

James – We’ve run out of water? Can somebody call the supplier?

Kate – Do we have any pens and paper left? I’m all out!

David – Could someone answer that phone please? It’s ringing off the hook!

James – Hello, this is E+ Education, James speaking. Yes, sure, sure. I’ll get right back to you.

Kate – No! Not again!! Hello! Hello!

James – Not that phone, Kate, the other one.

Kate – I can’t seem to keep track of anything these days. E+ Education. Kate speaking! Yes. Yes. No. No. Thank you.

David – Here, let me take this call. Hello, E+ Education. Sure, sure. Let me write down your number. Oh, hold on, has anyone got a pen? Where are all the pens when you need’em?

James – I think we’ve run out. Mary usually orders them.

Kate – There’s no paper either. I can’t even find a single paper clip!

David – Unbelievable. Oh, excuse me. would you mind holding for a moment? Hello? Hello? He hung up on me. This is all a bit hard to handle!

Kate – This is not good, David. We need Mary. I miss her personally, and we need her to help out around here!

David – That’s true. But currently we have no budget for her to work. It’s unfortunate, but everyone’s going to have to pull their own weight around here. We all need to hunker down.

James – You have to find a better way, David. I don’t think we can get the new project off the ground in this situation.

Kate – It’s been crazy here since Mary left. Loads of paperwork to do, proposals to send, and not a spare pen in sight!

David – Oh, that reminds me, where’s the invoice and delivery note for the School of Elite?

James – It was Mary who took care of that kind of paperwork.

David – Oh, it looks like I will have to find a way to bring her back.

 

Topic 4: Reinstatement

James – We’ve run out of everything, really. Nobody’s ordered any stationery or tidied up the office in a while.

Kate – Actually, David, if you want, I can talk to her.

David – I was a bit hasty getting rid of her and now I realize it. It was really just to save money. The owners are always harping on me to cut costs.

James – We know, David. It’s just, you know, we can’t earn anything in this situation.

David – Right, you have a point. I’ll talk to her, Kate. It was me who let her go, anyway. I’m totally responsible for this mess we’re in at the moment.

Kate – You will get her back, right? It’ll be a little awkward, I think.

James – I think so too. Kate is a good friend of hers, maybe she can talk to her first.

David – No, no worries. I’m the boss, I think the message will be better coming from me. Straight from the horse’s mouth.

James – You’re right, only you can make it official.

David – And it was my decision in the first place, so I really need to explain that the situation’s changed.

Kate – To super-sensitive Mary, can you just say you’re sorry? Tell her you made a big mistake, and you’d love to have her back. Well, in fact, we all would.

David – Yes, you’re right, Kate. I should do that.

Mary – Hello?

David – Hello Mary. It’s me, David. Yeah, how’re you doing these days?

Mary – Oh, I’m not too bad. I’m still looking for something new to keep me busy.

David – Mary, I have to say. I’m sorry. I made a big mistake with you and miscalculated my staffing numbers.

Mary – Yes? And?

David – So… I would love to have you back in the office as soon as possible. What do you say?

Mary – That’s good to hear. I’ll be back in the office tomorrow.

David – Oh! Excellent! Wonderful! Wonderful! Yes! Everybody’s looking forward to working with you again. See you tomorrow. She’s coming back tomorrow, guys.

Kate & James – Hurrraay!

Step 2: Practice rapid response

✔️ Without the transcript, listen to Recording 13.10 and give short answers from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Use the transcript and practice responding as instructed in the transcript from 1 to 3 times.

✔️ Practice listening and answering to Recording 13.10 from 3 to 5 times until you feel confident in giving full-sentence responses.

Recording 13.10 - Rapid Response - Reinstatement

Lesson 13 Company Downsize

Recording 13.10 - Rapid Response - Reinstatement

Recording 13.10 - Rapid Response - Reinstatement

Part A: Building Comprehension

What does Kate suggest?

Kate suggests she can talk to Mary to bring her back to the office.

Kate says she can speak to Mary to call her back.

Kate tells David she can call Mary back.

What did David realize he was when getting rid of Mary?

He found himself hasty getting rid of her.

David found himself a bit hasty when letting Mary go.

David was a little hasty terminating Mary’s work.

Why did David get rid of Mary?

He was forced to cut costs.

The owners are always harping on him to cut costs.

Due to the owners who are constantly talking about cutting costs.

Who is responsible for the mess?

David’s responsible for the mess.

David says he is responsible for the mess.

David is totally responsible for the mess

What does David do to bring Mary back?

He calls Mary to ask her back.

David himself calls her to ask her back.

David calls Mary directly to ask her back.

Has Mary found another excellent job?

No, she hasn’t.

Mary’s still looking for something new to keep her busy.

Mary’s still trying to find a new job.

Does David admit his mistake after terminating Mary’s work?

Yes, David admits his error when terminating Mary’s work.

He admits his mistake of letting Mary go.

David says he miscalculated and it’s his fault for getting rid of her.

Does David reinstate Mary successfully?

Yes, he does.

David reinstates Mary successfully.

David brings Mary back successfully.

When does David want Mary to be back?

He wants Mary to be back as soon as possible.

David wants Mary to come back as soon as possible.

David wants Mary to be back at work as soon as she can.

When is Mary coming back to work?

Mary is coming back tomorrow.

She is going to come back to work tomorrow.

Mary is going to return to work tomorrow.

Part B: In Other Words

He’s really exhausting my patience during this project.

He’s really making me run out of patience during this project.

When she was on maternity leave, it was tough to organize the office.

When she was on maternity leave, it was tough to tidy up the office.

The boss constantly pushes us to think of the customer first.

The boss constantly harps on us to think of the customer first.

You brought up an important reason why clients aren’t buying more of our products.

You have an important point about why clients aren’t buying more of our products.

If the client likes our proposal, I’m sure we’ll hear it from him directly.

If the client likes our proposal, I’m sure we’ll hear it straight from the horse’s mouth.

Wouldn’t it be great if we could get your computer to work again?

Wouldn’t it be great if we could get your computer back in action?

We must find someone new who can reorganize the mess in our supply room.

We must find someone new who can order the chaos in our supply room.

The teacher likes to make us speak English all the time, even during coffee breaks!

The teacher likes to keep us busy speaking English, even during coffee breaks!

Part C: Language to Go – How to Get Somebody Back to Work in Your Office:

What would you say to claim responsibility for a bad situation you and your company are in?

I‘m totally responsible for this mess we’re in at the moment.

What could you say to a person you just let go when you know you need her back?

Tell her you made a big mistake and you’d love to have her back.

What would you say if you realize that the message is better spoken by you?

I think the message is better coming from me, straight from the horse’s mouth.

How could you tell the person that your wrong calculations were the reason for letting your coworker go in the first place?

I made a big mistake with you and miscalculated my staffing numbers.

What could you say to tell the person the situation has changed?

It was my decision in the first place, and I really need to explain to you that situation’s changed.

What could you say to someone to let the person know everyone wants her back?

Everybody’s looking forward to working with you again soon.
