Milestone 1: Present Simple - Verbs Forms

Guide: To Be

1 – Affirmative

I am (‘m) a smart guy.
You / We / They are (‘re) all nice and friendly.
He / She / It is (‘s) very happy.

2 – Negative

I  am not  (‘m not) a smart guy. 
You / We / They are not (aren’t) all nice and friendly.
He / She / It is not (isn’t) very happy.

3 – Yes / No questions

Yes / No questions    Short answer 
Am I a smart guy?   Yes, I am.  No, I am not. 
Are you / we / they all nice and friendly?   Yes, we / they are No, we / they / aren’t. 
Is he / she / it very happy?   Yes, he / she / it is.  No, he / she / it isn’t.

4 – Wh-questions

How am I?
When are  you / we / they all nice and friendly? 
Why is  he / she / it happy?

Guide: Normal Verbs

1 – Affirmative

I / You / We / They read every day.
He / She / It reads every day.

Spelling rules for normal verbs after the subjects He / She / It

Most verbs Add ‘-s’ come → comes, eat → eats, like → likes
Verbs ending in ‘-ch’, ‘-sh’, ‘-ss’, ‘-x’, and ‘-o’ Add ‘-es’ catch → catches, wash → washes, miss → misses
Verbs ending in a consonant + ‘-y’ Replace ‘-y’ with ‘-ies’ cry → cries, study → studies, apply → applies
Special verb   have → has

2 – Negative

I / You / We / They  do not (don’t) read every day. 
He / She / It does not (doesn’t)  read every day. 

3 – Yes / No questions

Yes / No questions    Short answer 
Do I / you / we / they  read every day?   Yes, I / we / they do.  No, I / we / they don’t. 
Does he / she / it read every day?   Yes, he / she / it does.  No, he / she / it doesn’t.

4 – Wh-questions

With an auxiliary (Asking about the object)

Where / When / Why / How  do I / you / we / they   read (every day)? 
Where / When / Why / How  does he / she / it   read (every day)? 
Who do I / you / we / they  read (every day)? 
Who does he / she / it   read (every day)? 

Without an auxiliary (asking about the subject)
Who cleans the house in your family?

Step 4: Production

✔️ Use Guide: To Be and Guide: Normal Verbs to finish requests below.

Production: Present Simple

➤ Write 3 or 5 affirmative sentences using the Simple Present verb forms.

➤ Change those sentences into negative form, “yes/no” questions or “wh” questions and answer them.

➤ Use the given words.

➤ Use examples as your reference.

👉 E.g. My brother / intelligent

→ My brother is very intelligent.
⇒ My brother isn’t / is not very intelligent.
⇒ Is your brother very intelligent? (Question) – Yes, he is. / No, he isn’t.

👉 E.g. She / take / everyday

→ She takes me home by bike everyday.
⇒ She doesn’t / does not take me home by bike everyday.
⇒ Who takes you home by bike everyday? – She takes me home by bike everyday.

2. They / practice Yoga / 3 times per week
3. That lady / my boss
