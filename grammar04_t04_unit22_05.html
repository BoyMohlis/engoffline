Online Practice - Exercises
Exercise 1

Match the sentences from 1 to 8 with the suitable reporting verbs from a to h.

Questions   Reporting sentences
1. Why don’t you read the book?   a. Nick refused to read the book.
2. I’ll read the book.   b. Nick invited me to read the book.
3. No, I won’t read the book.   c. Nick suggested that I read the book.
4. I didn’t read the book.   d. Nick warned me not to read the book.
5. Would you like to read the book?   e. Nick promised to read the book.
6. If I were you, I wouldn’t read the book.   f. Nick denied reading the book.
7. Excellent! You have read the book.   g. Nick advised me not to read the book.
8. Be careful – don’t read the book.   h. Nick congratulated me on reading the book.

Exercise 2

Complete the sentences to report what was said using one of the verbs below and an infinitive clause. You may need to use a verb more than once. If necessary, add an appropriate object after the verb.

ask remind invite warn
  1. Would you like to have some tea with me? (He)
  2. Can I drive your car? (She)
  3. Don’t forget to buy some snacks. (He)
  4. “Don’t drive too fast!” (She)
  5. Can you read it for me, please? (He)

Exercise 3

Read the dialogue, then complete the text below.

Michael: Hi, would you like to come to my party next week?
Kate: Yes, what’s the occasion?
Michael: Well, I won three million dollars in the lottery last week!
Kate: Wow, congratulations! What are you going to do with all that money?
Michael: Well, I haven’t decided yet.
Kate: Why don’t you donate half of the money to charity?
Michael: That’s a good idea, I agree with you. Oh, would you help me plan the party? I can’t do it all myself.
Kate: Sure, I’ll help you.

I talked to Michael yesterday. He (1) _________ me to his party next week. I asked him what the occasion  (2) _____________. He said he (3) _________ three million dollars in the  lottery (4) ______________. I (5) _________ him on winning the lottery, then I wondered what he (6) ___________ with all that money. He told me he hadn’t decided yet. I (7) _________ him donating half of it to charity and he (8) _________ me. He asked me if I (9) _________ him  plan the party, he couldn’t do it all  by himself. So I (10) _________ I would help him.


Exercise 4

Choose the correct verb. In some cases, both options are correct.

  1. The doctor advised / told me to drink more water.
  2. Henry insisted / wanted to go home early.
  3. She suggested / wanted using the computer.
  4. Perry expected / advised to earn more money.
  5. He offered / suggested to help her.
  6. She longed / promised to call  him
  7. The boy denied / fed breaking the window.
  8. He offered / suggested her some candy.

Exercise 5

Choose the correct answer.

1. “Let’s go to the park,” Sarah said to me. – Sarah suggested __________ to the park.
A. go B. going C. to go
2. “Thanks for helping me,” Jenny said. – Jenny ____________________ her.
A. thanked me for helping B. thanked you for help C. thanked for help
3. “I can’t let you use the phone,” Mary said to him. – Marry prevented __________ from using the phone.
A. her B. them C. him
4. “I want to study abroad in England,” Mike said. – Mike _____________ of studying abroad in England.
A. dreamed B. dreaming C. dream
5. “Would you mind coming early?” she asked. She asked __________ early.
A. coming B. me to come C. me coming
6. “It’s best to leave now,” said Jerry – Jerry suggested __________ now.
A. me to leave B. me leaving C. I leaving
7. He said: “I stole the money.” – He _______________ the money,
A. admitted stealing B. admitted to steal C. admitted he was stealing
8. Tina said: “Don’t swim too far.” – Tina ____________________ too far.
A. warned us to swim B. warned for not swimming C. warned us against swimming.
9. “You should take a rest,” Carol said – Carol ____________________ a rest.
A. advised to take B. advised me to take C. A & B are correct
10. “You ate all the cake!” Zed said to him. – Zed accused him of _____  all the cake.
A. eating B. to eat C. not eating
