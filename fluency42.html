Day 42 - Rapid Response - Complaining about poor hotel accommodation

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 7.6 from 3 to 5 times. Use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary.

Recording 7.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Lesson 07 - Double Trouble

Recording 7.6 - Perfection - Native Speech

Topic 1: Learn to Compliment

David – Kate and James, I´d like to have a quick word with both of you in the meeting room now, please.

Well, James and Kate, yesterday was a great day until I received a call from Mr. Takashi Matsuo. But let’s focus on the good first. Kate!

Kate – Yes?

David – Well done with the presentation. I think I´ll take it a step further and allot a bigger budget for this project.

Kate – Oh, that’s exciting!

David – You were in a difficult spot in the presentation when the electricity died, without your slides and notes, but you know, you handled it very well. Great job. You remained calm, friendly, and professional. I was really impressed.

Kate – Thank you, David.

David – X3 Coaching is the result of very hard work, led by James. So, James, I´d like you to work with Kate on the project this time.

James – Sure, David, I can go along with that.

David – We´re making this one of our primary products. I want it not only to compete with the best quality, but also attractive to the market as well. Can you two work on this plan?

Kate – No problem, David.

James – Yes, we can handle it.

David – Good. Good! Good!

Now, James, I think I should have a private word with you.

Kate, on your way out, could you please close the door behind you?

Kate – Of course, David.

 

Topic 2: Give and Receive Feedback

David – James, I´m a little concerned about some things related to you recently. Is everything okay?

James – Yes, everything is good, David.

David – I know that this course is a big deal, and you’ve put a lot of effort into it. But that’s not your only task. Do you follow me?

James – Sorry, David.

David – Perhaps you should think about working on your time management, and how you distribute your energy to each of the jobs you need to do.

James – Sure, you´re probably right.

David – I think we´ve been having too much trouble with Mr. Matsuo and his organization recently.

James – I’m so sorry, David. The computer crisis caused us a lot of trouble, as you know. But it was my fault not to double check before sending out Mr. Matsuo’s order.

David – As long as you take responsibility for your actions, James, I can bear with you.

James – Sure. I’m going to contact him right away, explain the situation, and discuss some kind of compensation for him.

David – Good. Mistakes happen, but we need to learn from them, and how to deal better with the situation at hand.

James – I´ll try my best. I promise to give it my best shot.

David – Good to hear. Now, since the messed-up order this time is for another branch, which is way out of town, and we caused Mr. Matsuo a lot of inconvenience, I should go there myself right away and deal with the situation in person.

James – I’m really sorry, David.

David – No worries. I need to go on this business trip. And let’s hope things turn out fine by the time I get back. Now back to work.

Kate – James, oh, is there any problem?

James – Everything’s good, Kate.

Kate – I thought David was going to have a serious talk to you about the Matsuo order. I thought he was going to set the record straight!

James – Well, he did. And he also said I should work on my time management skills.

Kate – What does that mean?

James – He’s right. I haven’t done a good job managing my time recently. He’s going to meet with Mr. Matsuo to personally deal with this problem, Kate.

 

Topic 3: Hotel Booking

David – Kate, since Mary called in sick today, would you mind helping me get ready for my trip?

Kate – Of course. Poor old Mary, I hope her illness isn’t too serious.

David – Don’t worry, she says it’s just a simple health checkup or something. Now, this trip is a big deal since Mr. Matsuo and his organization is one of our main clients. Kate, could you help me reserve a hotel room?

Kate – Yes, I suppose I could…

Here it is…The Luxury Hotel.

Receptionist – Hello. This is the Luxury Hotel; how may I help you?

Kate – Hello! I´d like to make a reservation at your hotel. Could you tell me the availability and prices for a room, please?

Receptionist – When do you need the room?

Kate – I need the room for three nights, starting from tonight.

Receptionist – We have a wide range of services. The best single room is $350 per night.

Kate – Does the price include breakfast?

Receptionist – Yes, the breakfast is a full buffet with various food options and juice, as well as lots of bread, meat, and cheese.

Kate – Oh, and good coffee?

Receptionist – Yes, of course, we even have espresso and a bar of flavored coffees!

Kate – What about business facilities? Do you have things like internet access, pens, paper, maybe a projector?

Receptionist – Yes. We’ve got all these things. All you have to do is ask at the front desk for assistance.

Kate – Wonderful! Great! I´m wondering if you happen to have a room with an ocean view?

Receptionist – Yes, we do. There´s a gorgeous view of the sea from all the rooms on the second and third floors.

Kate – I’d like to go ahead and make a reservation for my boss please.

Receptionist – Yes. Now, what name will the reservation be listed under?

Kate – Mr. David Johnson.

Receptionist – Could you spell the last name for me, please?

Kate – Sure. J-O-H-N-S-O-N.

 

Topic 4: Complaining

Mary – I’m going to the hairdresser now.

Kate – Oh. Mary’s just gone out. I’ll get it.

Hello, E+ Education. This is Kate speaking…

David, did you sleep well?

… Oh, well … Okay … goodbye…

James – Is everything okay Kate? You look troubled by something.

Kate – Actually, David isn’t happy about his hotel room. The room is too noisy because there’s a live show nearby every night all night, and he couldn’t sleep well. The AC is also too loud and blowing cold air directly on the bed. And, here´s the disaster of all disasters: There was no coffeemaker in the room!

James – Oops! Nothing could be worse than that, especially for David!

Kate – Yes. And he said the clerk didn’t even greet him when he entered the hotel.

James – You’ve got to be kidding me. David would never accept such an attitude.

Narrator – Oh, Kate. Now you need to make some complaints on his behalf.

Kate – Okay. Well, here it goes.

Receptionist – Hello. Luxury Hotel.

Kate – Erm… Hello. Hello I want to complain.

Mary – Kate, give me the phone.

I’ll deal with this…Hello?

Receptionist – Yes. Something about a complaint?

Mary – I have a complaint to make. The room you gave my boss is really not good enough. No greetings at the front desk, a noisy room with a noisy live band and a noisy AC, and especially…no coffeemaker for my boss who loves coffee more than anything??? I would like you to resolve this matter as quickly as possible, or I´ll have to cancel our reservation.

Receptionist – Sadly, the coffeemaker has been out of order recently.

Mary – Okay. I’d like to speak to your manager please. We need to make absolutely sure that our boss is getting his money´s worth at your hotel.

Step 2: Practice rapid response

✔️ Without the transcript, listen to Recording 7.10 and give short answers from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Use the transcript and practice responding as instructed in the transcript from 1 to 3 times.

✔️ Practice listening and answering to Recording 7.10 from 3 to 5 times until you are confident in giving full-sentence responses.

Part A: Building Comprehension

What´s everybody totally focused on in the E+ office?

On their jobs.

Everyone´s completely focused on their jobs.

They´re all focused totally on their jobs.

What does Mary want James to give her?

She wants James to give her a break!

Mary wants a break from James.

Mary tells James she needs a break from him.

Is David pleased with his hotel room?

No, he isn´t.

He isn´t happy with his hotel room.

David is disappointed with his room.

How many problems are there with David´s room?

At least three.

There are at least three problems with his room.

David reports there are several problems with his room.

Which is the worst problem for him?

There was no coffeemaker.

The worst problem was no coffeemaker in the room.

For him, having no coffeemaker was the worst.

Did the clerk greet David when he entered the hotel?

No, he didn´t.

The clerk didn´t greet David when he entered.

David wasn´t greeted by the clerk when he entered.

Did the room meet David´s expectations?

No, it simply didn´t.

The room simply didn´t meet his expectations.

The room wasn´t up to par for David.

Does Mary hold back when it comes to complaints?

She doesn´t hold back.

When it comes to complaints, she doesn´t hold back.

Mary doesn´t hold back when it comes time to complain.

Does she want to make sure her boss is getting his money´s worth?

Yes, she does.

Mary wants to make sure of it.

She wants to make sure he´s getting it.

Would she like to speak to the manager?

Yes, she certainly would.

She´d like to speak to the manager.

Mary would like to escalate the problem to the manager.

Part B: In Other Words

I wish my father would take it easy on us sometimes.

I wish my father would give us a break sometimes.

We believe they´re satisfied with our new course.

We believe they´re happy about our new course.

The worst thing that could have happened was to lose the game to that team.

The disaster of all disasters was to lose the game to that team.

He always talks about the bad side of everything.

He always has many complaints to make.

What do you say when the client is extremely dissatisfied with your materials?

What do you say when the client is extremely disappointed with your materials?

If you practice a lot, you´ll be able to achieve much more!

If you practice a lot, you´ll be in the zone more often!

I’ve always reached my Dad’s level of requirements so that he could be proud of me.

I’ve always met my Dad’s expectations so that he could be proud of me.

I’m sure that all of you will receive good value for the money you paid for this course.

I’m sure that all of you will get your money’s worth from this course.

He is someone who will say something in the most direct way when you are wrong.

He is not someone who will not hold back when you are wrong.

Part C: Language to Go – How to Complain about Hotel Service

What would you say if the coffeemaker is not working properly?

I have a simple complaint to make: The coffeemaker in my room is not functioning properly.

How would you complain if you asked for a room with a view and you got one with no view at all?

I´m afraid I have a complaint about the view from my room. I asked for a room with a view, and I received one without any view.

What could you say if the room didn´t meet the expectations of your boss?

I´m sorry to say this but the room didn´t meet the expectations of my boss.

What might you say if your room wasn´t clean?

Unfortunately, the room didn´t meet my expectations because it wasn´t clean when I arrived.

How could you complain if the room was extremely noisy?

Excuse me, but I have a complaint to make. My room is extremely noisy.
