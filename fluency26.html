Day 26 - Catch-up Speaking

Step 1: Practice catch-up speaking at slow speed

✔️ Listen to Recording 5.5 from 1 to 3 times, without the transcript and try to understand it as much as you can.

✔️ With the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 5.5 from 1 to 2 times.

✔️ Then, with the transcript, listen again, and practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 5.5 from 1 to 2 times. Practice until you can keep up with the character's speed.

✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 5.5 at least 2-3 times.

✔️ Note: Focus on the pronunciation of each word, especially the stress and the ending sounds.

Recording 5.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Lesson 05 - Health and Safety

Recording 5.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Recording 5.5 - Perfection - Slow Speech

Topic 1: Health and Safety Procedures

David – Thanks everybody for joining me. Unfortunately, there’ve been several fires in the city recently, and the government has requested that each organization makes sure that its staff understand all pertinent health and safety procedures.

Mary – Again? Are you sure this is really necessary?!

David – Well, Mary, if you fail to prepare, you prepare to fail. Health and Safety Procedures need to be reviewed. James, what’s the first one, please?

James – If you see a fire, trigger the alarm, and call the fire service.

David – Correct! What’s the other important rule, Mary?

Mary – If you hear the fire alarm, get out quick! Find a place to hide until all the smoke dies down!!

David – Well, not really, Mary. We should walk calmly to the nearest fire exit, follow the green signs, go outside, and gather as soon as possible at the fire alarm meeting point.

Mary – Yes, and I’ll be responsible for checking who’s there, and who’s not. I’ll take the roll call.

David – Right! So far, so good. Now, is everyone clear about what to do if there’s a fire alarm?

James – But it seems we only have false alarms.

David – Maybe, but false alarms or fire drills are to make sure you know what to do if it’s for real. Anything else?

Kate – Oh, yeah. Absolutely no smoking on company premises.

David – Right, this rule should be followed strictly. Now, what are you doing sitting around here. Let’s get back to work everyone.

Kate – Mary, why does David take this so seriously?

Mary – He takes everything seriously. It’s just David being David.

Kate – Oh! That terrible sound makes me panic.

Mary – Now, don’t be nervous. Everyone, please keep calm. Things will be OK.

 

Topic 2: Emergency Practice

Mary – It’s unbelievable! The fire alarm has gone off! David has just left, and James is nowhere to be seen. Kate, you need to be in charge now!

Kate – Okay, everyone. There’s no need to panic. Please leave the building calmly and go directly to the meeting point outside.

Mary – Oh, I need to get my list first to check on everybody. Hold on. Quick. We need to get to the elevator, Kate! Quick!

Kate – No. Sorry but no elevator, Mary. We must use the staircase to stay safe.

Mary – But I’m too old for the stairs! My poor legs just can’t take it!!

Kate – Come on, you can do it with my support. Let’s go, one foot in front of the other! Could I have your attention, please? I need your complete attention. Please stay calm everyone. I’m sure there’s not a fire, just a bit of smoke. Now, is everyone accounted for? Is there anybody missing?

Mary – David?

David – Yes, yes, I’m here!

Mary – Kate?

Kate – Here, Mary.

Mary – James? James? Has anyone seen James?

There’s James coming out of the building. What happened to your leg, James?

James – Ooh… Oww… Ouch. My ankle really hurts! I think I twisted it…

Kate – What exactly happened?

James – I saw a cat and I tried to get it out of the building when I tripped on the stairs.

Kate – Oh, James! I’m so sorry about your sprained ankle.

 

Topic 3: Health and Safety Report

James – Ooh… Oww… Ouch. My ankle really hurts. It almost feels like I’ll never walk well again!

David – Seriously, are you okay, James?

James – I don’t think I’ve broken any bone, but it still hurts like crazy, and that stupid DVD box is still here.

David – Someone must fill out one of these Health and Safety Reports. Kate, could you help with that please?

Kate – Well, sure, but do we really have to do it? I mean, is it really necessary? The accident already happened!

David – It’s our procedure, and we need to act accordingly to the regulations, which means it must be done.

Kate – Wow, it’s not easy to put the answers into simple words!

Mary – Kate, do you want me to help you with that?

Kate – Yes, oh, please. The first question is: “How did the accident happen?”

Mary – Let’s say there was an emergency file drill. And James, while strictly following the rules, tripped while he ran out of the building.

Kate – That’s right.

Mary – So, the cause of the accident was a box of DVD’s left on the floor. Who left them there anyway?

James – That might have been me, Mary.

Mary – What do I always say about putting things away? Now Kate, it says here, “Were there any injuries?” right here.

Kate – Okay, so I’ll write, “Yes, a member of the staff broke his ankle.”

Mary – No, actually he sprained his ankle. I think he’d be in much more pain if it was really broken, wouldn´t you say that James?

James – What? Yeah. It still hurts a bunch though.

Mary – Hmm! Now finally it says, “What can be done to avoid this accident in the future?”

Kate – No more emergency practice?

Mary – No, we still need that. Store things properly, move things out of the way, and always keep the office tidy, right James?

James – Yeah, I suppose so… But I was only trying to follow the rules. Unfortunately, I also followed the kitty!

Mary – I know, you’re very exemplary, dear James.

 

Topic 4: Calling the Doctor

Kate – Now James. How are you feeling? Do you need to go to the doctor?

James – It still really hurts. What do you think I should do now?

Kate – I’ll get you some ice in a plastic bag to put on it. At least that’ll reduce the swelling.

Mary – I know a very good doctor. Here’s his office number. I think you should go to see him in case you’re really hurt. I’m sure you want to play football again with your team soon!

James – Okay. What should I tell him when I call? I’m so nervous, I’ve never broken a bone before, or even sprained an ankle.

Kate – Don’t be such a baby. Here, let me make the call for you.

Hello, is this Dr. Stall’s office?

Receptionist – Yes, it is. Is this your first visit?

Kate – Yes, it is. It’s our first time.

Receptionist – Okay. Could I have your name please?

Kate – Yes. My name is Kate. Oh, wait, no. I’m calling on behalf of my colleague, James.

Receptionist – And may I ask who referred you to our office?

Kate – Yes, it was Ms. Mary Jones. She’s a colleague of mine as well.

Receptionist – Okay. Let’s arrange a time for him to come to see the doctor. How about the day after tomorrow on Wednesday at 4:00 o’clock in the afternoon?

Kate – Well, James, can you see the doctor at 4:00 P.M. on Wednesday?

James – Can it be in the morning? It’s more convenient for me. I’m an early riser.

Kate – Excuse me, do you happen to have any openings in the morning?

Receptionist – Okay. Um . . . How about Tuesday at 8:00 A.M. or Thursday at 8:15 A.M.?

Kate – The earlier the better. I think Tuesday would be fine for James.

Receptionist – Okay. Could I have your phone number please?

Kate – Yes, of course. It’s 643-0547.

Receptionist – All right. And what’s the nature of your visit?

Kate – Well, to tell the truth, James tripped and fell on the stairs while exiting the building in an emergency fire drill.

He suffered a few scratches on his hands and knees, but we´re most concerned about the swelling of his ankle.

Receptionist – Well, did you put ice on it immediately after this happened?

Kate – Well, yeah, kind of… I just filled a plastic bag with ice and you know. . .

Receptionist – I see. Please come in 8 A.M. Tuesday. I’ve already set up your appointment with the doctor. Don´t forget to bring your photo ID.

Step 2: Practice catch-up speaking at native speed

✔️ Please use the transcript, listen and speak quietly after Recording 5.6 from 1 to 3 times.

✔️ Then, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 5.6 from 1 to 2 times. You can use the transcript and a dictionary if necessary. Practice until you can keep up with the character's speed.

✔️ Without the transcript, practice catch-up speaking along with Recording 5.6 at least 2-3 times until you feel confident with your speed.

✔️ Note: You should spend a lot of time on this step. You need to focus on the intonation, the linking sounds and the natural speed of the native speakers.
